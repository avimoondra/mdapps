Brief Profile:
Yes I understand that my GPA sucks- with good reasons all explained in the personal statement- tragedies and just having to work multiple jobs destroyed my GPA freshmen and sophomore year. Somehow I still only managed to get one C+ through it all, so I guess that paid off somewhat. I did reasonably well in the rest of my years. Anyhow, where one lacks, the other shines. I've found encouragement from other's profiles about getting into medical school with crappy grades, so hopefully this will help others realize that a lower GPA isn't the end of the world. :)

Summary of Application Experience

MCAT Scores are pretty good, wish I got a better grade in verbal- stupid proctor called time early, later fixed her error, but then ruined my concentration. Hopefully that S in writing, as well as my good grades in English classes and making it as a finalist to speak at graduation can cover my ass. I do not want to repeat the MCAT over again. Current grades in my special masters program are great, I'm on par with med students in medical physiology and way above average in medical biochem, hopefully this trend improves for both classes.

8/21/2006:
Received Howard and will finish this tommorrow- must purchase a money order. Nearly complete with Morehouse, but must call them- apparently my first and last name do not appear on their online application system. VCU extended my submission deadline because they have had trouble downloading off of VirtualEvals. Must call UIC tommorrow to see why my application is still considered in process if all my materials had been received. Only awaiting for UMC to review my plea to be sent a secondary application. Also awaiting, at this point, NYU to receive my preliminary AMCAS information so I can submit their secondary. Need to check in with schools where I have not received word of a completed application as of yet. Awaiting UNC's decision if they want to send me a secondary (essentially an interview invitation) or not. Also must check to see if my application is being held up because they are expecting my SMP program director's LORs. Also must prepare a list of addresses where SMP program director can send the update LORs. MSU does not require a secondary.
Up to this point, I have completed: 21 applications, with 2 in progress, 1 awaiting an appeal decision, and 1 awaiting an invitation to complete their secondary.

8/22/2006:
NYU Received.
Update: NYU Submmited.

8/23/2006:
VCU finally lists my application as complete.

8/25/2006:
SUNY downstate finally sends a notice that I am complete. I received a decision from EVMS... Hold for grades. Might get an interview if I do well this fall quarter. I'll cross my fingers.

8/26/2006:
SUNY downstate sends a decision- hold for grades and letter of recommendation from Graduate/Medical school coordinator- Can be faxed.

8/29/2006:
Rosalind Franklin, Tufts, University of Toledo (Med University of Ohio) all notify me via e-mail that I am complete. University of Missouri rejected me after sending an appeal to be considered for their school as an out of state applicant. Looks like its my first rejection.


10/5/2006:
So just minor little updates here and there, basically schools listing me as complete and being put into review. BIG UPDATE: I got an interview request by Tulane. Shocked, excited, happy, thrilled, nervous, all wrapped in one.

10/23/2006:
Had my Tulane interview. What sucked was that I was on crutches/wheelchair for it since I had surgery 10 days ago (Friday the 13th WoOooo) repair my right knee: ACL graft + medial menisectomy (half of meniscus was removed). It was good in that I had alot of help from New Orleans (people are ridiculously nice down there), and it was a great light humored segway into the interview (silver linings are always good). All in all, i left the interviews extremely happy, euphoric so to speak. It all went so well. A reply to my thank you email after I got home even gave me more of a reason to have a slight sliver of hope that I will get an acceptance from them next week. I can only hope and pray.

11/02/2006:
Pardon my french but HOLY SHIT! I got accepted by Tulane. I am so happy right now!! God life doesn't get any sweeter than this. I guess by having really crappy grades in college made me a much better speaker and interviewer.

12/1/2006:
No real updates, Told over the phone that I was rejected by Rosalind Franklin when calling in. Kind of strange to be rejected when most schools would probably put my application on hold to see my SMP grades first. Also found out on SDN that 'Decision Rendered' pretty much means a rejection from SUNY Buffalo on the online status page. I e-mailed to ask but was told that the decision will be mailed. That aside, still waiting for schools but not holding my breath as much after the Tulane acceptance. If nothing else changes, it would seem to me that I am destined to go here- I've even found myself checking up on the weather in New Orleans while I freeze my ass in teen-degree temperatures in Ohio lol. I'll keep updating, but I must admit, I am slacking a little bit in my work at my SMP.

1/8/2007:
Looks like I was invited to EVMS for interview via email!!! Wow Im pretty shocked, didn't think I'd get more than one interview!

2/8/2007: Guess it sucks to not be an in-state resident. Rejected from my own SMP's school @ University of Cincinnati.
