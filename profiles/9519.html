Brief Profile:
Graduated 2006 with Computer Engineering, B.S. and music minor (piano performance). Decision to go to medical school was during late junior year, took an extra year to finish pre-med requirements. Definite upward trend in grades after starting pre-med coursework.

Worked at software companies full time summers/part time school years starting freshman year until junior year, switched to pharmaceutical company doing bioinformatics work until now. Main interest is in computing technology and combining it with medicine. Want to be a physician first, then use that knowledge and expertise to advance information technology in medicine.

Hospital volunteer (~6 months)

Bioinformatics research at UCSD (~1.5 years)

Mentored by chief radiation oncologist at UCSD Moore's Cancer Center, saw patients, went to cancer boards at children's hospital, learned about radiation planning, extensive shadowing (~6 months)

Digital Nursing, Teams in Engineering Service at UCSD, Team Leader
Created a computer based documentation system for nurses at nursing care facility to take patient notes onto a laptop equipped with touch screen interface. Web based solution created entirely from scratch and is now successfully developed and deployed. Acted as both team leader and software team member. Now leading research team for publication in medical informatics journal to publish findings and contribute knowledge of computing in nursing homes. (~1.5 years)

1 Abstract: Accepted to American Medical Informatics Association November 2006 Symposium

Coordinated two Taiwanese American Youth Leadership Conferences in 2002

Chaired 2nd Annual UCSD Biotechnology Entrepreneurship Conference in 2005

Music minor: took private piano lessons for music majors the entire duration of stay at UCSD

BCPM freshman grades were low, was not thinking about medical school at the time, that brought down the BCPM quite a bit. I was born in Michigan and still have family/friends there so that is the reason for the Michigan schools.
A lot of the upper tier schools are medical informatics powerhouses so I figured I would try.
A lot of these schools are a shot in the dark, esp the california schools, but maybe my 'non-traditional' application will spark some interest.
Any advice would be appreciated, thanks!
