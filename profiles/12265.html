Summary of Application Experience

Taking time off after graduation to work in clinical research has made a tremendous difference for me in the application process. My experiences post-graduation gave me a lot to write about for my personal statement and much to talk about during interviews.

4/25 - If money were no object, then I would more seriously consider attending a private school in the fall. However, the financial aid packages from the private schools I have been accepted at have been extremely disappointing (it's been all loans minus a $1,000 scholarship from Temple and a $3,000 scholarship from Einstein). For this reason, I am planning to attend SUNY Upstate in the fall. The tuition break for in-state students will save me $16K - $24K per year.

If you will be the sole payer of your medical education, then attending your state school is the way to go. Medical school isn't like undergrad where private school education oftentimes exceeds that of state schools; you will receive a quality medical education regardless of the school you attend (people that try to tell you otherwise don't know what they are talking about).

My Advice:

For anyone with similar stats: Apply to no more than 15 schools; it will save you a lot of time, money, and energy. Apply early and be patient as this is a long process!!
