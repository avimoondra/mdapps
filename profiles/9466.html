Summary of Application Experience

Lessons learned:

1. Apply early! The deadline given by a school is at least a month too late.

2. Don't be afraid of giving your rec. writers some guidance. Tell them exactly what you're intending to do, and some of the things you'd hope they write about.

3. Consider your motivations, for medicine, research and trying to combine the two. Not only do you need a good answer for ADCOMs (and the friends and family who will think you're insane for signing up for that much school), but also you need to have a reason to get out of bed in the morning.

4. Use MD/PhD students and programs for guidance along the way. There's not a lot of info out there, so collect all the advice you can and judge later.

5. Don't buy into the pre-med mindset. If you do, you'll find yourself stressing needlessly, checking status pages compulsively, and missing out on the present. Try to stay busy with other things, like school, working out, music, fun with friends, etc.

School impressions:

Mayo

Probably the best place for clinical training in the US, bar none. A class size of 34 is as much attention as you can get at a medical school, not to mention the resources of the Mayo Clinic (Mayo serves the entire southern Minnesota community as well as specialty cases from around the world; George HW Bush was there getting his hip replaced on the weekend I interviewed). It seems like PIs here regularly find collaborators on the clinical side to take projects from bench to bedside. The MD/PhD Directors (note: website hasn't been updated; Dr. Isaya and Dr. Kaufmann are the director and associate director now) are focused on getting students into labs and getting pubs. As an MD/PhD at Mayo, the program funds your stipend for all 8 years, making you extraordinarily attractive to any lab you're interested in.

Drawbacks: 1) Rochester is a very small, Midwestern town and doesn't appeal to everyone. 2) Class size is very small (while good for teaching, might cause some personal drama). 3) Research is cutting edge in a few fields, which hopefully overlap with your interests.
