Summary of Application Experience

I started this application process last spring not knowing a lot of things. Reading SDN and shadowing to lots and lots of physicians (MD and DO) helped guide me toward where I applied. As you can tell above I applied to a lot of schools across the United States.

I was looking for a school that had friendly satisified students, helpful staff & professors, that was in an area that was fun to live in yet offered a dynamic patient population, offered good clincal rotations, and was the mostly affordable. Medical school is very expensive and this unfortunatly pays a part in the decision making process.

I took the April 2003 MCAT and my applications were sent out in June. This was for both AACOMAS and AMCAS. Secondaries were completed by September save one or two. The primary application fee and the $50-$100 dollar secondary fee (each!) started hurting my finances.

Interviews were mainly granted in early October and November with some others streching to Janunary and March. This is the most expensive aspect of the application process and one that hurt my pocketbook big time. It was also a main issue of me turning down some interviews as I just couldn't afford it after my acceptances, though I would have at least liked to have visted them.

I was very fortunate to have many interviews to US medical schools and even more fortunate to have a decision on where I wanted to go to school at. This is something that I was not sure of when I applied at the beginning of the 2003-2004 application cycle.

This is a very long process that I believe acts as a passage to medical school. In the end I have had to weigh lots of factors in my decision making process and the vibe I felt from the schools I visited. I am happy in my decisions I made.

For those of you that were wondering..... my 4 top choices out of all the schools I interviewed at were UT, NSUCOM, PSUCOM, CCOM/ETSU. Out of my top three I was waitlisted at two and accepted to NSUCOM where I will be attending this fall.

I feel very lucky to have had the experience I have had. I feel very fortunate cause I did not have much confidence at this point last year. Now I'll be a starting M1 this fall and getting ready to start classes to become a medical physician. This this the most exciting aspect yet.
