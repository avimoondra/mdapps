Summary of Application Experience

To those who read this, I'm just writing down random thoughts/feelings that I have during this process. I don't know how helpful it may be to you, but if it is, cool. :)

6/16:

Just sent my primary application out, but since CSUF lagged with my transcript (or well, I guess I lagged...), my app is on hold for now. :(

6/21:

Both transcripts are in, now I'm waiting in line to be reviewed. Should take 2 weeks or so I guess.

6/25:

AMCAS was processed; wow that was fast, only 3 1/2 business days.

7/11:

Still waiting for 3 letters of rec to come in. Secondaries are starting to trickle in, and given how impatient I can be, I'm shipping them off quickly. Or well, at least the easy ones. Still stuck on those essays that want to know why I'm so unique (when I'm really not, just your average guy).

7/12:

I just realized it's rather pointless to send out my secondaries as I wait for my letter file to be complete, but on the other hand, it gives me less work to do later I suppose. Oh well

7/15:

Huzzah, my letter file is complete. Now I can submit it to various schools; I could be complete at several schools by the end of July, neat! :)

7/25:

I'm complete at my first school, Baylor! How exciting :)

7/29:

So I got my BU secondary, and was kind of annoyed that I had to write 250-500 words on professionalism. Then, in comes UCD, which wants 9 essays at 6000 characters each. yeah, ok, sure. we'll see if I have time to do this one, but it goes to the end of the line. :P

8/1

Wow, my first interview invite! Vanderbilt (which only makes you do its secondary if they invite you for an interview) just sent me the mail.. how exciting!!!

8/18:

Well, I just got an invite to Hopkins, which I am really surprised by. I don't know, you just imagine these schools as places where all the amazing people go, and yet they want to interview me... it's just really amazing, I don't know how to describe it.

8/22:

For those of you who apply to Columbia, they take forever. They just notified me I was complete after having submitted my stuff like 6 weeks ago.

8/26:

Had the Vanderbilt interview. I don't think the interview counts for much... my interviewer barely asked me questions about myself. He mainly just talked about how great Vanderbilt is... weird stuff.

9/10:

Wow, Baylor rules. Seriously, everyone apply there. This school is so awesome, it's ridiculous. Or well, it's awesome and doesn't have the pretentiousness that accompanies Ivy League schools. That means a lot to me!

9/19:

Went to JHU, got a number of interviews to the east coast and to UCI, so I'm fairly busy right now... but it's exciting!

9/21:

Got my first rejection, how exciting! UChicago, which is actually a good thing, because now I don't have to waste money flying over there. A lot of us Cali students apply to a lot of places, in fear that we won't get into our state schools... and this is one of multiple schools that I ended up applying to just to be safe. So yeah, a positive and negative thing at the same time. Oh well :)

9/27:

Alright, I think I've pretty much been accepted at UCI. Both my student interviewer and faculty interviewer told me they really need people like me there, and my stats are certainly above average there. This means I can pretty much cancel interviews to places I don't really want to go to. Dartmouth is the first one to be crossed off, bc I mean, there's no way I'd even live out there. It's a great school (which is why I applied), but I'm a bit more realistic now and realize that the living conditions (i.e. surrounding environment and being close to the ones I love) matters just as much as strength of school. Still gonna go out for that east coast trip in October, though :)

10/20:

This process sucks. At the beginning of the week, I got put on hold at UCSF. Then, today, I got waitlisted by both Vandy and JHU. Oh well, I've always wanted to stay in Cali, but it still sucks being put aside I guess. On a positive note, UCLA rules. UCLA is my top choice as of right now, so hopefully I can get in!

11/16:

I was just accepted to UCI! My first acceptance! This is great, now I no longer have to worry about not being in Cali. I'd go to UCI before ANY other non-Cali school (including Harvard, Yale, etc -- but no interviews there thus far, so it's a moot point). UCLA is still my top choice (UCLA > UCI > UCSD [I hear a lot of SD students are miserable]), but it's great to know that I've been accepted to my #2 school. It looks like, for all intents and purposes, I'm done with my interview process. Thank God...
