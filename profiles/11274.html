Summary of Application Experience

7/8: submitted AMCAS, waiting for review. also working to get TMDSAS sent in, will update with that date later. personal statement took much longer than expected (a few weeks rather than days). MCAT went well -- no prep class, though, and i'm thinking that would've bumped it up another point or two. secondaries are looming in the distance, and promise to soon overwhelm.

7/12: submitted TMDSAS. in hindisght should've written essay prompt for the more specific TMDSAS prompt, but I think it'll be ok . now.....secondaries. oh boy.

8/24: in the midst of pumping out secondaries. interviews start up next week...september's going to be nuts, with travel out of town every weekend, coupled with travel for sports. can't wait for these secondaries to be done...at least there's deadlines on some of them to make me get rid of them eventually. the process is tiring and full of ups and downs, but more than anything else there's a lot of waiting and anticipation involved. best advice i can give: get stuff done as soon and as quickly as you can without sacrificing quality...there'll always be something more for you to do, and the earlier you get it in, the better your chances are. really, i just wish i'd done everything a couple weeks earlier so that i didn't have to worry about as much when school/interviews start next week. ah well, all i can do now is just work hard and do my best...

9/16: Mich: awesome. I'll update this later with stuff from my blog. UTMB: surprisingly good. UTSW: happening tomorrow. moving down the list, though, with their new relative grading system. Mayo: rejected today via snail mail.

10/7: Breaking my collarbone had 2 benefits: 1) my sister sent me a cookie 'get well' basket. delicious. and 2) it made a couple weeks go by really quickly when I was on the painkillers and dealing with the injury, so I could forget about the lull between the first wave of invitations (say mid-August or so) and this last wave (first week of Oct). Got three invites this week (Baylor, UTSA, Pitt). Baylor and SA both give about three weeks' notice til the interview, and Pitt went straight from secondary on Monday to interview on Tuesday. Withdrew from Pitt, but will be attending Baylor and SA's. Also this week finally sent in UCSD's secondary, and received and returned UCSF's secondary. Wish I would've kept up here with the dates of everything (when I hit each level in the process with each school), but hopefully these narratives will make up for that somewhat.

10/11: Picked up Yale (e-mail) and WashU (snail mail) last two days. Gonna go with earliest possible for WashU (rolling) and later one maybe for Yale (decisions on 3/15). Maybe around finals time and then make a trip up to NYC for a bit. Exciting...gonna hold off on scheduling that one until I hear from Penn...want to try coordinating these two if possible.

10/15: Yay. UMich liked me. Takes a burden off of my shoulders, but I'd still love to see Stanford and Penn. Ahhh, but still a great night.

11/3: WashU was awesome. I think they passed up UMich in my rankings due to the caliber and diversity of their students. Still waiting to hear from Penn (it's been three and a half weeks since I got my Yale one...could've gone this weekend for Yale and still not have heard from Penn). Duke's secondary...could do it this weekend, could just not. We'll see.

12/6: Ah, the ups and downs and mroe than anything else the waaiiiting throughout the whole process. Deferred for interview by Penn a couple weeks ago, got the UCSD interview invite on Friday in the mail, and the UCSF rejection in the mail today. Went ahead and scheduled my UCSD and Yale interviews, and now I'm just waiting to hear from Penn and Stanford on interview invites hopefully. Waiting on tons of others for accept/wait/reject news. Just have decided to go out and make the most of my senior year, seeing friends and putting a lot into my organizations. Happy Holidays!

1/16: No news for awhile. I guess I went on my UCSD interview about a month ago: liked it there, but I'm not sold on it as a top choice. It was tough to get a good read on it because the students were in exams, and I didn't get a chance to really get a feel for them. Depending on financial aid and how strong my desire will be to head to Cali, (and depending on an acceptance, of course), they could move quickly up my list. Should hear some kind of decision within a week or two (supposedly they meet every third Thursday of each month). Other than that, nothing else to report. Just more waiting. Break was awesome, though...read a lot, exercised a lot, slept a lot. Very energizing, and it's got me ready to go out in a blaze of glory this semester.

1/20: Just got my reject from Stanford. Sad. But life goes on...maybe Tim can go and I'll enjoy it vicariously through him.

2/1: Matched to Southwestern, but probably would go with Baylor over UTSW, so will withdraw soon. Most importantly, I hope that it'll open up some spots for friends that were not fortunate enough to match.

3/12: It's been awhile, though there really isn't much to report on. Went ahead and withdrew from UTSW--Oh, went to Yale! That was an awesome trip, and Yale and WashU are vying for the top spot on my list. Optimistically, though, I'll get waitlisted at both, and then jump up some time before May 15th. I've definitely been passed over several times for WashU, and with UCSD I've been sitting in that Acceptable Pool for several weeks now. A couple regrets: 1) my interviews at Yale and WashU were some of my weaker ones, and 2) I wish I would've applied to Duke, seeing as how a good number of students from my program got in, and Duke's program is looking more and more attractive to me as I go through this process. A final regret: not spending more time doing the things I really enjoy earlier on in college (been spending the last couple weeks volunteering as a coach at a middle school in the area, and going to a therapy clinic for children...if you hadn't noticed, I think I'd like to go into pediatrics). Anyways, back to waiting around for letters (though I;d rather get big packages!) from these last few schools, not to mention fin aid packages from Baylor and UMich.

Also wanted to say that all of my concerns were more than wiped away, and I really loved everything about it there. I know it's easy for a school to sell itself when you only get one date with it, figuratively speaking, but the curriculum has structure if you need it, and awesome students attending. We'll see, we'll see...

3/31: Got waitlisted as expected at both WashU and Yale. Got Yale's today via snail mail, WashU's about a week earlier. Letter of intent? Hard to say which school I'd write it to, especially with how Baylor is looking more and more attractive -- shortened basic sciences, cheap tuition, would be living with my dad (i'm appreciating my parents more and more these days), texas children's and the shriners hospitals for my interests in pediatrics, and so on... The only thing is, I'd be back in Houston, the city I've grown up in my whole life, and I think it'd be good for me to live elsewhere. The wanderlust and sense of adventure is getting to me. I think I'd get a lot out of living on my own in a foreign place, and I'd be meeting a lot of interesting people. Maybe I should defer and just travel for a year to get it out of my system, and then settle down in Houston. We'll see...off to mail my waitlist letters.

5/15: The big day when I make my final decision, right? Wrong. Was accepted to WashU and Yale today within a half hour of each other, and about 5 minutes after mailing off my deposit check to Baylor. Oy. Did go ahead and withdraw from UCSD and UMich, though (very regretfully for Mich, in spite of the cold). Off to try and study for my last undergrad final.

6/13?: Hard to keep track of the days on vacation in China. Long saga short, after about a month of e-mails, financial aid faxes and forms and letters, phone calls with the deans, and internet phone calls from China to the States, I have decided to matriculate at WashU. Lots of reasons: great rep, great program, lots of opportunites, were nice enough to help me out financially to make it less expensive than staying in state even, great OT program for my girlfriend, out of state (after living in Texas for 22 years), awesome diversity, talented students, fantastically nice staff, and so on. I think I'm going to wrap up this profile in a sec...

Before that, just a few last thoughts: I continued to update schools with what I was getting involved with, and I think I got involved in some pretty cool stuff that I was completely in love with. Played a club sport for five years in school, and started a summer camp for it one summer (which I forgot to include on AMCAS, but included in update letters), did some volunteering in the area I'm interested in, and helped to coach middle school sports. Tons of fun, exactly what I'd love to do with my life...I think if you show the schools were your passions lie, show them your personality and your love for...ahh, my travel buddy is here to take me out on the town. Will update later!

7/12: Ok, time to shut this thing down. Looking back at that last post, I just had to reiterate how tough of a decision it was, as Yale and Baylor are both fantastic in their own ways. In the end, the difference in money between Yale and Baylor/WashU was too much to choose going there, especially with how closely I had it ranked with WashU at the top of my list. And as for Baylor, I was just ready to get out and go off on my own a bit, though if they would have appeared to want me as much as WashU (friendly staff, good communication, any scholarship money at all), I probably would've stayed at home.

It's been a good run, and I'm thankful that things worked out for me this cycle. Best of luck to everybody applying!
