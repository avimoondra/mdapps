Summary of Application Experience

6/27- Submitted AMCAS. Verification will take forever...
7/6- Sent out GW secondary. Au revoir $105.
7/14- Primary verified! The funs begins!
7/16- Added Ros Frank. A nice school in Chicago, why not?
7/17- Submitted Case secondary. Now if I could only get my LORs in on time...
7/18- Submitted SLU and Jeff secondaries. Trying really hard to not think about how much money I'm spending.
7/19- Submitted Pitt secondary.
7/20- Submitted NYMC secondary.
7/23- Submitted Temple secondary.
7/27- Submitted HMS secondary.
7/31- Yawn. Is this the calm before the storm, or will be storm never get here?
8/2- Mailed BU secondary. Submitted UVM secondary.
8/4- Wake Forest rejection. So much for Southern Hospitality.
8/8- Committee Letter mailed. Soon to be complete. Eeeesh better hurry up with the remaining secondaries!
8/10- Mailed Ros Frank secondary.
8/11- Submitted Tufts secondary.
8/16- Submitted Drexel secondary.
8/20- Submitted Keck secondary.
8/27- Submitted Albany secondary.
9/1- First interview invite from Tufts!! Somewhat surprising and very exciting to have an interview offer so early in the season. Also received a pre-interview hold from GW... not sure if this is a rejection.
9/7- REJECTED by BU. :(
9/20- UCSF rejection. Not surprised, of course. Very nice and warm letter- class act over there.
10/3- Got Case Western interview invite!! I was really expecting a rejection based on the ominous 'status update' e-mail. It's official: Cleveland Rocks!
10/12- Interviewed at Tufts. Very impressed by the school and by the friendliness of the students and faculty. Would go here in a heartbeat!
10/14- Received UPitt rejection. Woe me! What will I ever do now that I can't attend such an amazingly unbelievable unique special school!? I was sooo looking forward to being in their viewbook, too!
10/21- Received rejection letter from GW. Somewhat disappointing, but I wasn't too crazy about the school anyway.
11/1- I'm in!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!! I love Tufts :) Thank you, Dr. O'Connor!
11/3- Pre-Interview Hold from AMC. This process is so weird- invites from Tufts and Case and a hold from Albany. Crazy...
11/14- .... of the Lambs...
11/15- Received official welcome to the TUSM Class of 2011. Withdrew from Albany, Ros Frank, and UVM.
11/20- My PI wrote HMS admissions to give my application a boost, only to be informed that I didn't make the cut. C'est la vie...
12/15- Interviewed at Case. Truly innovative curriculum, friendly students, and awe-inspiring medical campus manage to overcome the crappiness of Cleveland. My closed file student interview (~30 mins) and open file faculty interview (~90 mins) were both odd in the sense that no real questions were asked. Very hard to read; I have no idea how I did. Stay tuned folks...
12/20- I suppose if the best word you can use to describe your interviews at a school is 'odd', then you shouldn't be too surprised when you DON'T get accepted. Et tu, Case?
2/2- Sent letter of intent to Case.
3/10- Received HMS rejection letter.
3/16- Keck rejection.
3/23- SLUM rejection. Why did I apply there again?
4/3- Placed on the alternate list at Case. This is like a good book I can't put down! What *will* happen next?
4/11- Received Temple rejection.
5/24- Received Drexel rejection. No love from the Philly schools.
7/12- First day of classes at Case. I'm in Boston. I take it I won't be getting off the Alternate List.
7/17- Well, looks like there's not much more for me to do here. The season has come to an anticlimactic end. I am happy to have been accepted and will never have to go through this bullshit again- well not until residency application anyway. At last tally, Case has given me teh boot. Nevertheless, they're pretty classy at Case and I won't hold a grudge against them for inviting me to interview when my numbers didn't give me a chance and then sticking me with the worst interviewer ever (Dr. D---- D---, PhD, you suck). NYMC and Jefferson win the jackass award for not bothering to even formally reject me as of this writing.

Good luck to all future applicants!

Signing off.
