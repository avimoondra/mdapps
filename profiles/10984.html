Boston Medical Center - Endocrinology Research Assistant
1/2004 through 5/2004
- The laboratory work achieved by my supervisors includes the findings that a topical thyroid hormone cream can increase hair growth and skin thickness in mice. The RNA in several cell types in the lab mice was preserved and frozen for storage. The responsibility of my position was to further investigate the underlying causes of their findings. Throughout the semester, I repeated a particular set of experiments using different sets of RNA. My set of experiments began with gel electrophoresis of a particular set of RNA from mice under different treatment conditions. I then identified the optical density of that RNA. Following this I performed RT PCR for three specific receptors as well as a control. Lastly I ran a second gel electrophoresis of the PCRed RNA to test for increase or decrease of expression in these receptors in response to the treatment to which the mice were subjected. I would then record and analyze the results and report them to my supervisor. In the last several weeks of the semester, we began a study looking at the effects of thyroid hormone on wound healing in live mice. In this part of the study I was responsible for shaving the mice and assistance in the administration of either a control cream, low dose T3 cream, high dose T3 cream or intraperitoneal injections of T3.
