Summary of Application Experience

This is an expensive process...enough said about that part of it.

Interview impressions:

Pitt: My first interview, and my first time in Pittsburgh. I absolutely loved this place. I got the chance to spend the weekend prior to the interview in Pittsburgh and I was extremely impressed. The city is actually rather beautiful, tons of museums and other cultural attractions. Oh, and the view from the Duquesne incline is a sight to behold. Anyways..back to the school. UPMC is such a huge, amazing hospital system. The hospital facilities are great..the teaching facilities/classrooms etc. are okay, but it doesn't take away from my opinion of the place. The students there were so welcoming. The actual interviews were chill. Update 10/17...accepted!!! .

Vanderbilt: Great school, awesome anatomy lab. The students seemed pretty cool, although we didn't get as much exposure to them (I had the afternoon interview, so less free time to interact). Oh, and Judy is the nicest woman I've ever met. The Children's hospital is exactly what a Children's Hospital should be. Nashville was pretty fun for a smaller city. I love that Vandy students are among the happiest, and that they seemed to be prepared well for the USMLE. I just might end up here!!---2nd look was divine.

Case Western: Again, great school. I live pretty close to Cleveland, so it was nice to get back to the area and interview here. I probably had my most chill interviews here. The students were also really honest and receptive to our questions, and the staff was great. Tour was okay, I had a student who wasn't really very enthusiastic, so that kinda sucked..oh well. Great dual degree opportunities. But, overall, it was a really relaxed, great day. I'd be happy here. Update 10/22 Accepted!

Michigan: I don't really care for Ann Arbor, ugh. But at any rate, Michigan is a great school, but I don't think it's for me. I preferred Vanderbilt and Pitt considerably more. Nice facilities, though.

Penn Med: WOW. I absolutely loved this place. At this stage, if there is any school I would leave Pitt/Vandy for, it's Penn. Had a great time staying with my student hosts, and the school was just amazing. Facilities are awesome, the hospitals are great, and the students were some of the happiest I've seen. Penn also has a really interesting curriculum, one that helps Penn students receive the highest average board scores in the country. Philadelphia was pretty nice; downtown is cool, especially the historic district, but there are many rough areas, and I guess crime is rather high. I can't believe I have to wait until March to hear from them...hrmph. oh well.

Cleveland Clinic: Obviously, the idea of free medical school is always great. That being said, I really liked the way research was integrated into the program, and few institutions can match the amazing care provided at the Clinic. I'm unsure of the small class size...32 students is a little bit elementary school-like. I also don't know how well I would mesh with no lecture at all, all small-group learning, and no real graded evaluations. Overall, I'm glad I went and checked it out, and I will have to think hard about the curriculum and how it meshes with me, as I think it has some negatives and positives.

Decided to withdraw from Emory and Mt. Sinai after receiving interview invites due to my acceptances at Vandy, Pitt, and Case, and because I didn't feel like flying to schools that I didn't think stood much of a chance against the aforementioned, and therefore wasting money.

----

I recently attended Vanderbilt's 2nd Look and I had the most amazing time EVER. I loved my future classmates, and the administration and current students were equally wonderful and supportive. I can't wait to get together with the people I've met come July. Definitely attending! Vanderbilt University School of Medicine Class of 2013!!!!
