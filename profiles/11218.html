Summary of Application Experience

amcas and aacomas were both submitted 7/20/05. aacomas was verified 8/3/05, and amcas was verified as of 8/4/05. i have secondaries for gw, ou, osucom and uams and will have them completed by mid-august. all letters will hopefully be at interfolio by at least the end of the month. i'm a non-trad, so i'm not using a committee.

9/11/05 update -- i've cut down my list a bit and decided to focus primarily on d.o. schools and my state school. i've received an early acceptance to wvsom without interviewing (a new thing they're doing to expand their class size). they want $3k by 10/3. i'm going to visit in about two weeks, but i'm leaning again towards not paying it. their out of state tuition is through the roof and it's not a practical location for my husband. i'm still finishing up secondaries and feel like a complete slacker for not being done -- i'm just having essay burnout.

oh yeah, i'm also still waiting for my second science letter. hopefully, i'll get that in about two weeks. that's holding me up from getting any allopathic interviews.

9/20/05 -- received my second science letter yesterday and have everything sent out from interfolio, so i guess i'm a complete. i have another science letter coming in at some point, but it's an extra. i've completed all the secondaries except ohsu, uams, ou and rosalind franklin (on the fence about this one). also, thinking about submitting the secondary for lecom-erie just because of its cheap price tag and the fact that i'd only need to spend two years in erie. i hope to have those done by the end of the week, and then i'll be totally finished except for interviewing. :)

10/12/05 -- received my first real acceptance at kcumb! it's good to know i'll be going somewhere next year, and i did like kcumb when i interviewed there. i'm thinking about declining my interview at nsu just to save more vacation day. the flight's already booked, but i'm sure i can transfer the credit for something else. i'm getting no allopathic love thus far, but i'm not too concerned because the only allopathic schools i'm really interested in (ou and ohsu) are pretty slow. plus, there's that little issue of me not having completed my ohsu secondary yet.

10/20/05 -- so when am i going to hear from gw, drexel and temple. i've been complete for a month now, all schools are rolling, and i've heard zip. oh well. got an interview invite for umdnj-som today, which i'm excited about -- cheap tuition, good rep, good clinicals all sound cool. ou and osucom supposedly don't do anything until november (will i have any vacation days left?), and i haven't heard anything from unecom (maybe they don't like me :( ). i declined my nsu interview even though i had already booked tickets because i'm fast running out of vacation. the problem with being on the west coast and flying east is that you lose time, so it's basically impossible for me to get a flight out after work that will get me to wherever i'm going that night. consequently, one day interviews require at least 1.5 days of vacation. i knew i'd never pick nsu over kcumb, so it wasn't worth it to attend the interview.

10/25/05 -- i've reached the obsessive mail/email checking stage, and all i'm getting is spam and junk mail. :( oh well. i am getting hyped about my dmucom interview on sunday -- as you can see from my comments, everyone seems to be super positive about that school. i so need to take up yoga again because i'm so impatient these days.

11/1/05 -- so i've gotten 3 osteopathic school acceptances and am waiting to hear from dmu. i'll see if osu and umdnj wow me, but i'm thinking my decision will probably be between the schools i've already interviewed at. i haven't heard anything from unecom, so i figure they're not interested. for allopathic schools, i guess i'm just waiting to hear from ou and ohsu, and i probably won't hear from either for ages. i withdrew from wvsom -- i really liked the school, but it would mean that my dh and i would necessarily have to live in different towns. also, the cost is prohibitive.

11/4/05 -- okay, i just got my first allopathic interview today, and i've got to admit the feeling was cool. i'm not using osteopathic schools as a backup, but it's nice to see that i can get some interest in the allopathic world as well. in the interim i've got to make some decisions about osteopathic schools and am feeling conflicted. according to the myers briggs tests, i'm a hard-core p, a p off the charts type of p, and i can so see it with this process. i hate to close doors and am always seeing possibilities. thus, i hate the thought of declining a school and turning down a prospect. yep, silly, but that's how i'm wired. luckily (or not) i've missed out on the perfectionistic aspect of being a p.

11/25/05 -- received my osucom acceptance today, so i'm 5 for 5 on the do school thing. my first allopathic interview is next week at ou, and i'm still hoping to get some instate love and get an ohsu interview, but oh well. my big news is that i received a half tuition scholarship to dmu, which makes it cheaper than even my instate school would be. i have to keep an 85% average and pass the comlex on my first try to keep it, but hopefully i'll be able to do that. i'm visiting the school in two weeks (interviewed here in portland). it's winning in cost and reputation, so i hope i'm not totally turned off by des moines. i've never even been to iowa. :)

12/4/05 -- well, i just got back from circuitous trip going from portland to oklahoma city to philadelphia for interviews. about a year ago, i would have thought it was fun, but now it's just exhausting. i think i bombed my ou interview, so, hmm, my one allopathic interview might come out to nothing. everything else was nice, and i like all the online resources they have. the mod system also seems quite cool. on a brighter note, i think my umdnj interview went okay, and i really liked that school. they meet on dec. 19. i don't know when ou decides, but i do know they're prompt.

still nothing from ohsu, temple or gwu. i did, however, get a complete postcard from drexel dated 11/28. since i was complete on 9/20, i don't think it reflects well on them. i'm going to des moines this week and am almost positive i'll be giving them a $500 deposit. i'm also going to put down osu's $100 deposit. the whole money situation at dmu makes it a lot easier to turn down ccom, kcumb and comp.

12/5/05 -- weird but i just received an interview invitation from drexel in the mail today about two days after i got my complete postcard. eh, i'm feeling crazy and think i'll call 'em and try to schedule one in what april?

12/19/05 -- yay, i got into ou and received a scholarship. it's still pricey because of being out of state and all, but i'm jazzed. honestly, i really liked ou when i visited. it seems like a really student-responsive school, and i know the mods are a great way to bond and meet people.

12/20/05 -- all right, i'm going to try to respond to some of my comments. first, thanks for all the well wishes! second, about lecom being creepy. i don't know, i could be wrong, but the school seems very authoritarian with their dress code and strict attendance policy. that's an environment i would have trouble adjusting to. also, have you seen that website with all the tacky busts and sculptures and whatnot at lecom -- that stuff is just downright weird. i'm sure it's a good school, and the low cost coupled with the variety of different curriculum paths is a definite plus, but yeah i'm glad i didn't submit a secondary.

third, for which school i'm going to pick, i don't know. i've always really liked osteopathic medicine, so it does feel like a loss somewhat to pick an md program over a do program. however, i've got to admit some things seem easier for mds -- for example, i would only have to take the usmle instead of both the comlex and the usmle. also, the clinicals are probably better at ou. but, dmu and umdnj are both cheaper than ou and very good schools. so, they're all getting deposits, and i'll figure it out later. :)

1/27/06 -- first update of 2006. i'm still undecided as to where i'm going, and i still haven't heard from my state school, which is starting to p&ss me off. but whatever. i've got an interview scheduled at drexel on 4/6/06 but am not sure if i'm going to go. it'd be more expensive than ou, and philadelphia is just starting to seem awfully far away. for that reason, i'm also starting to think umdnj won't be in my future. so it's down between ou and dmu -- ou's home but expensive, and dmu's some place i've never been but cheap. honestly, i don't see the do v. md thing as being a big issue because i'm not shooting for derm or opthal. right now, i'm thinking psych, im, ob/gyn or pm&r, and these fields are all open to do's. i know i might change my mind, but i really think my personality is going to push me more toward fields like this than toward the road fields. it's still a tough call, i guess.

1/28/06 -- there seems to be a trend here -- as soon as i get ranty about a school, they give an interview invite. see above with ohsu. ranted about it yesterday, received an interview invitation today. my interview has to be sometime between 3/13 and 3/23, so i guess i'll schedule that on monday. i guess that also means i won't know anything about them until close to 5/15.

3/21/06 -- so my ohsu interviews went okay, but who knows. i'll hear in about six weeks whether or not i'm admitted (slim chance), declined (another slim chance) or put on hold until may (most common possibility. if i'm put on hold, i'll know around mid-may or so whether i'm admitted or waitlisted and where i'm ranked on the waitlist. the situation sucks because they regularly admit people up to the very first day of school, which doesn't work for me -- i'm not that flexible. i decided to decline my drexel and temple interviews because i'd rather go to ou. both schools seem nice, but philadelphia's not where i need to be right now.

5/12/06 -- latest and possibly final update since my it's all over. OHSU did not offer me a seat in their class -- the person I spoke with on the phone didn't mention a waitlist, either, so I'm assuming that's not happening. I've withdrawn at UMDNJ-SOM and DMU, so I'm off to Oklahoma. Cheap living, close to family, still close to Colorado and New Mexico -- I don't think it'll be all that bad. :)Well, I guess it's time to start focusing on moving. As a word to the wise for any premeds who are thinking about applying in a few years, think about moving to a state that's friendly to its instate applicants -- it makes your life less stressful during your application year, and it'll save you money. I love Oregon, but it's unfortunately lacking in educational opportunities.

5/13/06 -- Okay, I guess my last post wasn't the last because I've got to reply to some comments. One, I only took the MCAT once, thank god. I did take a Kaplan class, which was helpful but not magnificant. The materials were good, some teachers were great and some sucked. I don't know if I have a magic MCAT answer. To the comment about writing a blog, wow, you made my day! I'm glad someone enjoys my totally long-winded comments. My husband blogs, so yeah, I can see the appeal. Hmm, I'll think about the SDN blog thing. Anyway, again, your post made me happy. :)
