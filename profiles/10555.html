Summary of Application Experience

07/06 - Submitted application. Waiting for Brown GPA to go to hell!

07/15 - Application processed. Brown GPA fine, but graduate work changed to be part of 'senior year.' Submitted academic chage request (and spoke to an Amcas phone operator, who couldn't help me himself but was very infomative. I'd recommend calling for quick, friendly answers.)

08/29 - Three weeks volunteering in Thailand put me way behind schedule on secondaries. I hadn't realized that most would be sent out w/out the primary even being read! Anyway, I'm just catching up, have submitted 6 of 12 to date, and added Cornell, Columbia, Drexel, Jefferson and Tufts to my list. At this point, all the schools are blurring together, and I just want to be in a good city.

09/14 - First interview invite, U of CT.

11/02 - Trusted in the Mills College letter sending service -- what a mistake! When I had no response about a completed file from Tufts, Drexel and Jefferson after a month, I finally made some phone calls and found out the letters were never sent. Even after figuring things out, it took another month to get them out the door! Finally, I think my secondaries MIGHT be all complete...

11/14 - ABSOLUTELY fell in love w/Penn, though that might have just been because I saw former Brownies having a blast in their 2nd year. Am considering writing a letter of intent, but don't want to hurt my chances elsewhere.

11/17 - First rejection. I actually feel about as good about it as the acceptance -- at least I'm not in limbo!

02/04 - Back from Thailand again, and pulling out of schools under BU. I think I found the right mixture of applications, secondaries and interviews, so that I've gotten a chance at all my top schools while holding down a great job. Just waiting to hear from UCSF and Penn at this point -- fingers crossed!

03/23 - In honor of the suckiness of waitlists, I have withdrawn from Sinai, UConn and the Cornell waitlist. I'm hoping that the good karma of this will mean that people accepted at but uninterested in Penn, Columbia and UCSF will do the same, and let some of us waitlisted suckers have a chance before August. Until then, I'm not even going to try to think about the logistical nightmare of moving across the country when you don't know what city you're heading to...
