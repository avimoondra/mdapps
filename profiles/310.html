New York University - "Reason for Withdrawal: For whatever reason, NYU and I just didn't click. I was incredibly excited fo..."
Combined PhD/MSTP: No
  • Submitted: Yes
  • Secondary Completed: Yes
  • Interview Invite: 8/4/2014
  • Interview Attended: 9/24/2014
  • Waitlisted: 1/27/2015
  • Accepted: No
  • Rejected: No
Summary of Experience:

Reason for Withdrawal: For whatever reason, NYU and I just didn't click. I was incredibly excited for my interview day, but the host I stayed with and I did not click and I had that same feeling with other current students I met. I should have withdrawn sooner, but I had the overwhelming feeling that I'd be wasting the money I spent going out to interview.

School Summary: NYU is a very innovative school, which works in its favor. Its 1.5-year curriculum seems to be working very well, and the students that I met seem happy. That said, the students seemed a little more reserved than the friends I have, so that made me slightly uneasy.

The 3-year program is not of particularly interest to me, but definitely has strong appeal for anyone who is sure of what they want to do.

The housing at NYU is good, but definitely a little industrial looking. You cannot beat the price, though (as is the case with all the NYC med schools).

Lastly, the MMI was very relaxed. A few of the questions were a little odd, but definitely don't feel pressured to sound incredibly knowledgable. Just be yourself.

Pros: 1.5-year curriculum seems very successful; Bellevue provides amazing clinical training; reasonable, affordable housing.

Cons: Students did not seem to match my personality; location is a little far from the subway.

Cornell University - "Reason for Withdrawal: I loved Cornell on interview day, but I had already realized my lack of desir..."
Combined PhD/MSTP: No
  • Submitted: Yes
  • Secondary Completed: Yes
  • Interview Invite: 8/12/2014
  • Interview Attended: 9/23/2014
  • Waitlisted: 3/11/2015
  • Accepted: No
  • Rejected: No
Summary of Experience:

Reason for Withdrawal: I loved Cornell on interview day, but I had already realized my lack of desire to live in NYC and been accepted to other great schools. In addition, I was accepted by Penn later in the day, which was one of my post-interview top 2.

School Summary: Cornell is the one school in NYC at which I interviewed where I got the sense it was a powerhouse. Most of the students were incredibly friendly, confident and outgoing. I love those qualities in people, but it might not be right for some.

The school has recently switched to a 1.5-year preclinical, which is seemingly going through some kinks along the way. The school seems to cater itself towards preparing its students for specialization, and that is not a bad thing at all.

The location of the school is in a more affluent region of the Upper East Side, but they do try to do their best to ensure students have access to underserved populations if they so desire. The school is not very conveniently located in regard to the subway, and of all the schools in NYC I interviewed at this seemed to have the least desirable location.

Pros: 1.5 curriculum will give more research opportunities to students; students are very social and outgoing; nice subsidized housing for 2nd year and after; access to NYP, Memorial Sloan-Kettering, HSS, and Rockefeller in a few block radius.

Cons: Location is not ideal, in my opinion; curriculum still going through some issues; first year dorms seem a little industrial.
