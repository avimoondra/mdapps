Summary of Application Experience

A few notes on the schools, in case people are interested:

Northwestern - the secondary is a huge pain and has a 2-week turnaround time, plus they give group interviews. Only apply here if you're sure it's a top choice, otherwise it's not worth it.

Harvard - sends out secondaries really late. I didn't get mine til the beginning of September. It also took them a really long time to register that my application was complete.

Chicago Medical School - I got this interview invitation long after most of the rest (end of December). Their essay question was pretty silly, so be prepared to BS ('At Rosalind Franklin University, we celebrate and support a 'Life in Discovery.' Briefly describe how you foster growth and discovery in one aspect of your life - academic or personal').

Cornell - I wasn't invited to interview here until the beginning of March. I already had an acceptance and I was just too burned out to do another interview.

UMass - the only school I still haven't heard a peep from, as of the end of April.

Wash U - The interviewer here was very, very mean to me. I'm not sure whether he was a jerk or just trying to challenge me, but this was the toughest interview I had, and not in a positive way. Also, the students I met were lukewarm about the school. I got the impression that there's no reason to go here unless you're getting your MD/PhD. I withdrew as soon as I got an acceptance.

BU - The essay question on the secondary was so ridiculous and BS-y that I couldn't bring myself to write it. ('Please write a 250-500 word reflection on the concept of professionalism in medicine and the traits by which it is constituted. Focus your comments on ways in which you have been challenged by your own life experiences en route to a career in medicine, as well as on ways in which your own experiences have shaped your understanding of the profession of medicine and your own determination to undertake the obligations inherent in this career. Use this essay to provide us with insights that are not reflected in your AMCAS Personal Statement or other parts of your application.') What? This was the only secondary I didn't complete.

Hopkins - This interviewer was also very tough, but he was less of a jerk about it than the Wash U guy. He asked me for lots of lists (ex. what are 5 traits that make a good physician?), but I don't know whether that's typical. The mingling with the 4th-years wasn't as awkward as I'd heard, but it was obvious very quickly which applicants they liked. The interview day was definitely designed to pick out the applicants who were particularly outgoing.

Pitt - interview invitations are sent out at the same time as secondaries (mid September).
