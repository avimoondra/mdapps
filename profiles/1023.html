Summary of Experience:

I was dreading the MMI, but it turned out to be quite fun! I had a very positive experience overall. NYU has great clinical and research facilities, which I believe they are still expanding. The only downside is the student housing. I don't see myself in that college-style dorm for 8 years, but the high cost of living in the area really leaves you with very few options...

Update 01/07/14: a very subtle rejection email... did not hurt very much after Northwestern and WashU offers. I thought I'd love to live in NYC for MSTP before and during the early application season, but I think that has changed a little since I interviewed at different places. Maybe I'll end up there in 8 years. Who knows? :)

Update 01/27/14: got a strange email saying that I'm missing vital info for the admission process. Note that this is after I got rejected. Got a good laugh out of it, haha! Might have been for MD consideration. Withdrew on their application portal just to be safe.

Summary of Experience:

Did not particularly like how the casual weekend was organized... It would have been easier to actually get information from current students had it not been so crowded. The schedule was very spread out, with a lot of down time. The interview day was relatively laid back. Most faculty interviewers are enthusiastic and friendly, with one or two exceptions. The student population is quite heavily local (~30% graduated from NC, probably even more if counting people who are originally from there). Based on the research and clinical strengths, UNC would be an excellent program for people who don't mind southern living. I'm personally not so sure about this aspect...

Not much disappointment regarding the wait list. Will probably withdraw. I think this unofficially concludes my application season! I am quite happy with how it turned out. Now I just need to decide where I want to go! Wow it has been a crazy journey. :)
