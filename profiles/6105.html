Interview impressions (ranked on scale of 1-10)

*NYU(9/19): [Stress: 2; Research quality: 8; clinical facilities: 8; Responsiveness of administrative office: 6; overall: 7]

The school has top notch research and clinical facilities. The research buildings are new and there is plenty of lab space. The anatomy lab was straight out of 60s and reminded me of a dungeon. The faculties were very nice. I had 4 interviews. One with the director, and 3 with faculties. Each interviews were roughly One of the faculties was on the MD ADCOM, so I do not have to go there again for an interview, should I get rejected from MSTP. The students had very collegial temperament.

*UT Houston (10/30): [Stress: 3; Research quality: 10; clinical facilities: 10; Responsiveness of administrative office: 6; overall: 8.5]

It was very low stress interview. I was expecting the 3rd degree as it is MD Anderson, but all of my interviews were very friendly. I had my MD/PhD and MD interviews on the same day, so I had 6 interviews 30-45 minutes each on one day. I was exhausted at the end of the day, but I had a great time nonetheless. The clinical and research facilities are undeniably the best in the country if not the world. TMC is right in the hussle and bussle of the city, so crowded places are not your thing than you are at the wrong place. TMC is going under constant expansions which is annoying but at the same time very good. I got the feeling that they retain many of their MD/PhD graduates. This could be potentially good thing for career development. Students were helpful and seemed very happy. They have unique curriculum in which you do MS1,2,3; then take step 1 and then join the G1. It allow you stay with you MD class for MS3 and also gets your feet wet with clinical medicine. I think it is very good to get you oriented for translation research. You can do some longitudinal clinical exposure during your graduate years, and it would count towards some credits in MS4, so you can finish MS4 in about 6 months which will give extra time for residency apps. ***currently my top choice***
