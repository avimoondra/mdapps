Summary of Application Experience

Alright, so first let me explain the deferral. Case has this fabulous deferral policy that allows deferring students to reapply. I wouldn't have done this, and wasn't planning on it, but my husband is having a terrible time getting a job in Cleveland. I don't want to leave him in Colorado. So Case is letting me reapply to whatever schools I need to. The dean told me on the phone that she realizes Case might be the place for me right now, but in a year that may have changed. It makes me want to go there even more. Here's hoping for a job in Cleveland. All the other schools I've applied to are in places where my husband's company is located, so his odds of getting a job are better. Here's hoping.

10/19: I submitted AMCAS really late (9/26) because the dean at CU said they didn't need the app to be submitted early. He suggested I submit later with more clinical experience (which I was lacking last year). So that's the plan with that. Hopefully it doesn't screw me over TOO much at the other schools, but we'll see. So, the GPA may change when I get the new info.

11/16: Well, I feel really lucky right now. I applied late which I know is a no no, but I got my secondaries done very quickly and I am complete everywhere now. On 11/15 I was lucky enough to get a phone interview scheduled with Mayo. Mayo is my dream school. I love everything about it, not to mention I have family there, and I haven't lived within driving distance to family for 7 years. Not to mention their fantastic financial aid, and the opportunity to see cases you'd never see anywhere else. I hope to go into neurosurgery (but am obviously not committed since I really don't think you can decide before you see it) and I would have a great opportunity to do some awesome research here. I haven't heard from CU yet, and I'm hopeful to get an interview here since I did last year. I was on the waitlist at CU last year and since then have been trying to get more clinical experience as per the Dean's request. CU may not be my dream school like Mayo, but it's in my top 3. Not to mention it would be so much easier for my husband and I to stay here than to move away. At least his family is here. Well, with the secondaries complete...it's waiting time!

1/25: Best day of my life!!! I am so excited to report I've been accepted to CU!!! My husband can keep his job and we can stay with our friends!! I feel so lucky right now I can hardly stand it! University of Colorado School of Medicine Class of 2011!!!
