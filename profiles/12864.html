Summary of Application Experience

Don't stress out too much. You cannot even hope to guess how this stuff will turn out. For example, why would I get offered interviews at big name schools like Boston, Northwestern, and U. of Chicago and then get waitlisted at SLU? Bottom line: it's a true crapshoot.

Take a full length practice MCAT every week starting in February if you're taking the April test. I took 3 over Spring Break. Shoot to get 10+ full-lengthers under your belt, study what you didn't know on them.

The best money you will spend in this application process: $180 to buy the practice tests off e-mcat.org, they are exactly like the real MCAT and your score on them will be close to what you're going to get (I got 34,34,35,36 and got a 36 on the real deal).

The worst money you will spend in this application process: whatever it costs to take an MCAT review course. A wise MSIII once told me, 'If you can't motivate yourself to study for the MCAT, you might want to think about a different field.'
