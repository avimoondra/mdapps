Summary of Application Experience

11/23- Well, finally got some real news back from a few a places. Unfortunately more bad news than good. The rejection from UCSF really hurts, was definitely my number 1 choice. I will appeal the decision (UCSF actually accepts appeals), but I doubt it will have any effect. The Mayo rejection is easier to stomach since I didn't really want to live in MN, but it is a great school. MSU was really weird. I got the 'on hold' notification a couple weeks ago then yesterday I wrote them to tell them that I would be in the area in December. They wrote me back the same day offering me an interview. It seems really weird that all I had to do to get an interview was ask, but hey I'm not going to complain. At this point in the process I'm starting to get nervous. I really expected to have more interviews at this point in time. I wish I'd applied to a few more schools since things don't seem to be going so well with the UCs. I must say I'm a bit puzzled by the process. I know my extracurriculars aren't super strong but my grades and MCAT should be competitive most anywhere. Almost December and only 1 interview...hopefully this works out.

1/30- Feeling a little better than the last time I updated. I've received 4 invites all out of state. I will try to get them all taken care of in February. I must say I'm quite puzzled by, and not too happy with, my home state schools. I've received no love at all from the california schools, but got interviews at all the schools to which a applied late. WTF? Should stay positive though, 4 interviews is a good opportunity...hopefully this works out.

3/1- Just got back from a 4 interview midwest trip. I liked all the schools I visited and feel like I could be happy at any of them. Wash U had amazing facilities (too bad they interview over 1000 people a year). Cincinnati also impressed me much more than I expected. Now I get to play the waiting game. Got more bad news from California, rejected by UCSD. I really wish I knew what it is about my application that the UC system hates. I would love to come back to California for med school, but it doesn't look any of the schools are going to make that possible...hopefully this works out.

5/12- Just called IU and found out that I can add yet another school to the rejection list. With May 15 just around the corner, I'm on 3 waitlists and that's it. I'm trying to stay positive about the process, but it just keeps getting harder and harder to do. Every time a rejection comes I think about my application and try to figure out why I've been so unsuccessful. My extracurricular/clinical stuff is nothing special and definitely the weakest part of my app, but I just can't believe that it totally explains all these rejections. I guess it could be an unfavorable rec letter, but that seems really unlikely. I really don't get it. Maybe I was way too overconfident, but I expected to be really competetive and now I'm just praying to get in somewhere. Reapplication is looking more and more like a reality all the time...hopefully this works out.

5/19- Wow, this process has been extremely long, frustrating, humbling, and stressfull but thankfully it has a happy ending. When I checked my email a couple of days ago and found the acceptance from UC, the feeling of joy combined with relief was unlike anything I had ever experienced. I cant express how happy I am not to have go through this entire process again. I definitely think some lessons can be learned from my application. First off, never assume that will get in somewhere. I really wanted to go to school in CA, and I thought for sure that I would get into at least one of the UCs. That leads to the second lesson to be learned: apply to a wide range of schools. Because I assumed I would get into one of the UCs, I didnt apply to very many other schools, and the schools I did apply to were mostly of high caliber. In fact, if I hadnt added a few more places in Decemeber, I would be preparing my second application right now. If I had it to do over again, I would apply to at least 20 schools: 5 dream schools, 10 target schools, and 5 safety schools. I am really happy to have the spot at Cincinnati. I was impressed by the school during my interview day, and the only thing that could keep me from attending is the unlikely event of getting plucked off the WashU waitlist. Whatever happens now, I am thrilled that I will be a medical student in a couple of months.

7/10- I got accepted to OSU today. I had already decided that Cincinnati is a better for me, but it was really nice to receive another acceptance. I'm just hoping it's a sign of things to come with WashU.
