Summary of Application Experience

12/1: After a few months of incompleteness, I was feeling vaguely depressed and angry at my inbox. Then the Stony Brook invite sailed in. To celebrate, I'm posting my profile, mainly so that people with thankless GPAs can have a little hope. Here's a hint: move to a state with more medical schools than, say, Iowa.

I admit to getting in-state preference, and I don't expect Weill to swoop down on me next to the sound of trumpets, but I am happy. If Stony Brook will interview me, I hold out hope for Downstate, my one true love.

12/13: Since I haven't heard from NYU, and highly doubt a last-second invite will come, I'm considering myself rejected.

12/22: Cornell tells me 'A decision has been made on your file.' My mailbox tells me that I am no longer in the current pool of current applicants. On the plus side, those Dr. Who DVDs came from Netflix...

1/5/07: Downstate interview invite. Yay.

1/7/07: Albany interview? What? This late in the game?

1/25/07: Stony Brook decisions came out today. But! Am still under review, after frantically willing the DHL people to have merely lost my address.

2/1/07: Have reconciled myself to praying for an SUNY acceptance with a slim shot at an NYMC interview. Next round should be 2/6/07, and I'm guessing the next SB adcom meet will be around V-Day. They usually meet twice a month, at any rate. If not, my job still rocks, underpaid style.

Later on 2/1: Downstate rejection. Eh. Did very much want the city.

2/7/07: Since NYMC was clearly reading my profile and saw my faint plea of 2/1...I checked my inbox today after 3 patients with screaming children to find an invite in my box today. Four interviews, baby. Given my stats, I'm deliriously happy. Not like I would've gone to Downstate anyway, you hear?

2/12/07: Minor good news after I overcame my fear of admissions phone calls and checked up at Sinai, AECOM, and Columbia. I haven't been rejected from any of the three yet. Fairly surprising since I know AECOM sent out a big batch o' rejections at the beginning of February (which last year, I was included in). To celebrate, I wrote an update email to all three. Of course, I haven't exactly received an invite from any of them...

I've decided to ignore the Philly schools, Jersey, and Tufts, as two NYC-ish interviews give me hope I can stay here. I'd possibly give my first-born for an additional interview at one of my few remaining NYC schools.

2/17/07: Though I should mention that I managed to bump my interview from late March to late February. I thank my friend for her decision to go out of town in March -- gave me the courage to call up. My apartment lease is up on the first of June and my landlord refuses to do a month-to-month; I'm a bit worried that I might be homeless if I end up on SB's waitlist (would need to move to Brooklyn; only reason why we'd do so.) NYMC is notorious for dangling you for 12 weeks before a decision and I feel a little more comfortable with the extra three week buffer.

2/22/07: SB apparently read the correct undergraduate GPA and waitlisted me. This is a bit unfortunate, but having another interview scheduled takes some of the sting out of it. Plus the movement is fairly decent. What's depressing is that since said movement starts in mid-June, I'm definitely going to have to negotiate month-to-month with my Evil Landlord. Which he will then gouge us for. Ah well.

Also, Columbia finished interviewing today, so marked as a no. Perusal of SDN has taught me that AECOM and Sinai finish issuing invites around the first week of March, so I will officially write them off on 3/9.

3/1/07: Eight days early, I call AECOM about a last-minute recommendation to find...rejected yesterday. So much for that. Am remarkably calm, perhaps because I have decide NYMC is my one true, true love. Am sending not one, but two special extra letter of rec for them, and crossing my fingers that it'll be that last necessary push. Also, Sinai claims to stop issuing invites as of this Friday (although adcoms notoriously lie), so they get offically moved to the rejection pile a week ahead of time if I don't hear by tomorrow. A special shout-out to the doctors who helped me draft my rec today.

3/5/07: RWJ (lame), Tufts (not sympathetic enough), Columbia (nicest letter yet) and AECOM (at last, official) pre-interview rejections arrived today. Coincidentally, my letter of rec was sent to my interview schools, Drexel (for fun) and Mount Sinai. Am not adding them to the pile until those official rejections arrive on my doorstep. The other Philly schools are not worthy of such desperate measures.

3/26/07: Yale rejection arrived at the beginning of the weekend, which reassured me. I was beginning to think they'd loved my application so much that they'd hung it up to be a shining example to the application cycle of 2008. Spent some time this weekend painstakingly explaining to my relatives that I would not hear anything until May at the earliest, August at the latest, and my insistence on this line of thought was not an elaborate plan of disinformation. Hmm...

4/20/07: Temple rejection last Thursday, followed by Mt. Sinai the day before yesterday. I hear Jefferson's stopped interviewing, which is good; I might have applied to the Philly schools, and am hanging on because of the faint boost to my ego a third invite would bring, but I decided mid-cycle that I've lost my love for that city. Still waiting on NYMC, and making puppy-dog-faces in the general direction of Stony Brook. It occurred to me that I'm at the 9-month point of this drawn-out lovefest, and a pregancy metaphor seems pretty appropriate.

5/4/07: An actual conversation from inside my head this morning:
Me: Hmm, that LOI to NYMC should have been sent to them at least a week ago to be effective.
The Collective Insanity That Is SDN: Late Feb interviewees are due to hear around May 15th.
Me: Gah, composing this is taking forever. Seriously. I started this draft three weeks ago.
TCITISDN: LOIs are practically required for NYMC admission!
Me: And of the one guy I know who was accepted last year, wasn't it after he pleaded through his pre-med advisor AND sent two letters after being waitlisted? Am so in trouble.
My email inbox: Inbox (1)
Me: ACCEPTED! WHAT? IS THIS SOME KIND OF SICK JOKE? WHAT?!?
TCITISDN: (utter silence)

And so it was a very, very good day.

I'm going to keep a running commentary on whether or not I get into Stony Brook, which I'm still very interested in, but then will severely retro-edit this thing once I make up my mind.

[addition: Upstate acceptance came within several days. Not quite as awesome as that first acceptance, but that sweet 18k tuition does make me weak in the knees.]

6/15/07: Since I haven't sent them a letter, am unsurprised that SB has yet to send me a singing Valentine admit, courtesy of DHL. Heh.

For all of those with lousy GPAs: have hope. Look at your application, honestly assess your strengths, fix the easiest ones first...and above all, realize this process is a giant dartboard. Sometimes you don't hit the bullseye the first time around. Keep working at it.

Feel free to post if you have comments; am trying my best to cheerlead the underappreciated.

Next stop Canada! Sorry NYMC, don't feel like giving you the quarter million in debt when an in-state school will give me the time of day. Too bad, I really did like you, your faculty and your cool anatomy lab until I found out first-year tuition was 40k. I can make myself pretend professors, a beach and an anatomy lab out of construction paper to console myself in almost-Canada.
