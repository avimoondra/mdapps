**status** (most recent to least)
5/19/09: One year from the date of my last post, I am starting to really prepare (mentally and physically) for beginning medical school in July. Some reflections on the pre-med process:

- Applying to medical school is, above all else, a HUMAN process. Be yourself, within reason. You want to be sociable, competent, and genuine, but not so raw or flawed that it causes (or sounds like it has the potential to cause) any problems. Have you ever been in a position to judge/award/admit applicants for something where there were far more qualified applicants than you have spaces for? What kind of people did you want to accept? Approaching the admissions process this way was healthier for me and kept me sane.

- Really put time and effort into your personal statement. Get a multitude of different people to critique it. In order to do so, you you'll be better able to articulate how your experiences shaped who you are and why you're here. Every person has a story, how they came to where they are. Understand your own.

- Like everybody always says, apply EARLY EARLY EARLY. It makes such a difference, and it's easier said than done.

- Work hard, but don't pin your entire existence on this. There is always next year.

- Good luck!

I am looking forward to hearing from my contracting organization and placement in the JET Program!

Although UC Davis and OHSU were my top choices at the beginning of this application cycle, I am more than satisfied about being accepted to Case, especially after Second Look Weekend. Now, if only I can get my deferral approved...I will be presenting my case to the deans tomorrow morning. *sweats*

4/4/08: Waitlisted at Dartmouth. Not particularly surprised, or disappointed. It's good to have closure from the rejection letters that keep trickling in. OHSU hurt, however. I actually like the school. And UCI didn't even send me a letter, I had to find the status online. Going to Case Second Look Weekend...very excited about it!!

3/27/08: I loved Dartmouth! It was cold, but beautiful. Location and community definitely out there and much different from what I'm used to. I could live in rural New Hampshire for 4 years. Very warm reception at Dartmouth, impression on par with Case. I think the chances of getting accepted are slim, but we shall see. I think my application cycle is about done. Already the end of March...no sound from UCLA, Davis, or Irvine. No news is probably bad news. I would have liked to interview at one Cali school at least. Other rejections trickling in, but I'm not too bummed.

3/10/08: No news from the schools I'm still waiting to hear from (UC Davis, Irvine, and Oregon H&S). Going to Hanover next week! A Dartmouth alum convinced me to take a look. :)

3/6/08: Still in time for an interview? Invited by Dartmouth via e-mail and snail mail today. Do I want to go visit New Hampshire? Debating cost, chances, and whether the school sounds like where I want to be.

3/5/08: Noticed that 'final decision' letter on UCSD was sent 3/3. I'm not that thrilled about the UC's now anyhow. UCLA and UC Davis are the two I'm actually interested in attending, and both of them are a long shot at this point. Irvine may still be in reach, but my heart's already in Ohio. Also got USC rejection online, not surprised and not very disappointed. I'm very excited about Case Second Look Weekend!!

2/27/08: The Ohio State status page was updated yesterday...I have a second acceptance! I really did not feel like my OSU interview went so well; however it is a nice school. I now have 2 schools to choose from! Still leaning more toward Case, overall. =)

2/5/08, Part 2: Thanks to the tipoff, I checked the online Case site--it's been updated, and I'm in! My first acceptance! Mommy I'm going to be a doctor!! I can't say how relieved I am; having at least one acceptance is the most important thing, and better yet when it's not a backup school. That lifts one huge weight off my shoulders...I loved Case and I'd go there without a second thought if I don't have a shot at Wash U...and the UC's reject me for good.

2/5/08: Interview at Wash U yesterday...loved it! How about that for a birthday present? The school was absolutely beautiful, visiting arrangements top notch (free dorm room and pizza dinner--can't complain about that!), students were friendly, interviewer fantastic. The atmosphere was noticeably different from Case, however. More academic, more stratified, more dignified...less spontaneous with that feeling of close, unfettered community. Still, program seems great. We will see how things turn out.

2/1/08: Interview at Case was fantastic. Am keeping my fingers crossed. My med student interviewer really liked me; I don't know about the faculty interview (I thought I messed up, but then I always feel that way). I would definitely go if I get accepted...unless a miracle happens with the UC's. Interviewed for SLU in SF Bay Area on 1/31...felt okay, but not super thrilled about it. Nothing else new except more rejections: Stanford, UCSF. My California dreams are whittling down.

1/17/08: 4 interview invites by now, including Washington University. I'm really excited!! (and nervous...) First interview is on Jan 28 at Case. I am hoping for at least one California school interview; was a bit discouraged by pre-interview hold at UCSD but I'm still waiting to hear from the rest.

12/7/07: Yay! My second invitation! All hope is not yet lost. XD

12/5/07: 6 more secondaries to turn in. I know...things are being turned in late, late, late! Just keeping my fingers crossed at the moment.


**background**

Graduated in Dec 2006, after 4 years and an extra semester. Entered UC Berkeley early through the High School Honors program, then transferred to Bioengineering major; did a smattering of activities here and there, with a few core commitments in student groups. One month after graduation, began working at a medical center, then applied during the 2008 cycle. Turned in AMCAS relatively late, in early August; was not verified until September. Prepared letters of recommendation around that time also. First secondary was submitted in early September; submitted most secondaries between October to early November, complete at most schools by late November-December. Yes...very late application! If I had to do this a second time, I would definitely apply earlier. The mantra of my life.
