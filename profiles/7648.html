Summary of Application Experience

So far, I haven't gotten many interviews (just the one) and I've been a little bummed. But the one I did get is a favorite school and it only takes one, after all, so I am trying to stay positive. I added some lower-tier schools to my list just a few days ago and am hoping to get their secondaries and fill them out before October comes along!


10/2- WOW!! I got invited to Cornell. They're my number one choice. I can't even believe how good this feels!!!


10/18- Been a long time since I heard anything. I sent an update letter to Michigan, Brown and MCW yesterday. Can't wait for December...how can my interviews still be TWO MONTHS away???

10/19- Saw an envelope from Vanderbilt and was sure it was a rejection because of how quickly it came. But it was an interview invitation and that makes me so excited. Vanderbilt is SUCH an awesome school! I'll be there on November 16th.

10/23- It's been an exciting couple of days! I doubled my number of interviews but it only leaves me hungry for more!! I am excited about Rosalind Franklin because it is close to home...about time I got an invite that I can actually drive to!

10/26- And they just keep coming! Got Madison today and I couldn't be happier about it. The odds are really good at getting accepted this year if you get an interview because they just changed their policy. And I hear the new med school is beautiful. Plus, it's my hometown and my parents would be walking distance. I am really excited for this one!!!

10/26 (11:00pm)- Just got home from a fabulous evening listening to a fabulous band and drinking some fabulous beer and I got that letter from Columbia that I have been looking for but never expecting to see. I literally ran from my mailboxes into my apartment to scream to my fiance that I had a letter from Columbia. Didn't even have to open it to know what it was (good thing it was an invite and not a rejection, hey?) I am floating on Cloud 9 right now. Gonna drink some wine and watch some fabulous TV. This one makes me feel really good.

11/1- As of right now, I am very happy with my interviews. I'm still really hoping for a chance at Brown, Vermont, BU and Stanford. The other schools on my list would be awesome too, though! I feel very lucky to have gotten the interview invites that I have gotten and I'm looking forward to starting the interviews in two weeks. I will do six within about a month's time! It's going to be super fun though!

11/5- Well, one of my prayers was answered. My status page at Brown has changed to offer me an interview! This makes me crazy happy!!

11/5 (5:00 pm)- Now I got MCW too. This is a fun day!

11/18- So, I interviewed at Vandy on Friday and completely fell in love. From the beautiful children's hospital to the wonderful community vibe to all the smiling faces in Nashville, I can totally see myself going there. I never knew I could love a place as much as I loved it there. I am so excited now and really hoping to get in!

On a side note, I checked my email in the middle of the day during some downtime and saw that I had an invite from Keck! It really does happen when you least expect it! So, now I have nine interviews and I feel foolish for assuming I wasn't going to get more than 2 or 3...

11/19- YAY!!! I got my Vermont prayer answered now too!! I am sooo happy about this invite. I know I will really jive with Vermont's atmosphere. Coming from the UP of Michigan, I will be happy to live in another snowy climate! I can't wait to see this place in the winter!!!

11/26- Just got done with my interview at UW. It was nice...brand new facilities and a lot of diversity in education. When I got home and checked my email, I'd been invited to interview at BU! I'm bummed that they can't fit me in when I'll be out there for Brown, but I am happy to get the interview regardless. Boston is a fantastic place to study medicine!

12/10- Haven't posted for a while. Not much (read-NOTHING) has happened in the past couple of weeks. I did interview at Rosalind Franklin on the 27th of November and forgot to put my thoughts on here. It seemed like a nice school, very relaxed and a lot of good clinical exposure. The students are all a little older, which is a draw for sure. I really enjoyed my interviews, too. I'm lucky to have gotten along well with them instead of getting a 'stress' interview as I know they can give sometimes. A girl in my interview group was seriously shaken by one!

12/14- I'm so glad for the holidays coming up. I will be in NY next week for my three interviews and there is just no better time to be in the city than right before Christmas! I'm very, very excited. Plus, I think it will be easier to relax knowing that adcoms are NOT meeting and deciding my fate. I had a tiny freak-out today because I'm getting anxious about not knowing anything. But I will know all my decisions in 3 short months. I just have to enjoy myself right now...

12/26- So I just checked my mail from the holidays and got two interesting things. I got accepted to RFU, which is totally awesome of course, but I also got waitlisted at Vanderbilt. Since I've gone on five interviews since Vandy and haven't found a school I love as much, I'm going to start seriously pursuing getting OFF that waitlist now! But it is great to know that no matter what I AM GOING TO BE A DOCTOR!!!!

I just found a big smile at that thought...

1/8- I never talked about how my interviews in NY went. They were AWESOME to say the least. The schools out there are all fabulous and I'd be hard-pressed to find a reason to turn them down. I really liked Mt. Sinai's mission and the neighborhood was really cool too. I have another five interviews coming up in the next month, and I am getting excited again to see what those schools may bring me. The Vandy waitlist hurts less and less every day when I think about all of the other schools I've seen!

1/14- I had my MCW interview on Friday and I was really impressed with the school. Plus, it's super close to my family and to my fiance's family. I'm really hoping to have them as a real option in a week! I'm getting excited to see USC, UVM and Brown. Vandy waitlist doesn't even hurt anymore!

1/14- Waitlist at Madison. But I didn't want to go there and I really didn't try in my interview. It is starting to make me nervous to be getting waitlisted everywhere though...

1/20- On Friday, I interviewed at Keck and totally completely loved it. I got to sit OUTSIDE in JANUARY for my student interview. It was crazy and awesome. But I also got an email that day that said I was waitlisted at MSSM. It was my second choice. I'm not putting my rankings on here, but Sinai was my #2 and now they've been beaten down since they don't want me :(

Plus, the Packers just lost, so I'm not in a great mood. But, anyway, LA was cool. I have Vermont and Brown this week. Can't wait to go back home...

1/22- ACCEPTED AT MCW!!!! I'm so excited! I also interviewed at Vermont today. It was nice. Cold- but I'm used to that!

1/30- Left field interview invite from Michigan. I debated on logistics of getting there and taking off more work and whatnot, but in the end, I've decided to spend the money and bite the bullet. It's MICHIGAN!

2/5- I changed my mind about UMich. I was a last-minute invite and I didn't have a chance to arrange my schedule to accommodate the interview. It's a sigh of relief for me, actually, because I'm so sick of interviewing. Going back to LA in March with my fiance, though!

2/11- I scheduled an interview in March for NYMC. It's a good location for jobs for my fiance, so I will go if I don't have a better option by then. Got waitlisted at Vermont, which is absolutely no surprise because they waitlist nearly everyone from out of state. If I want to go there, I have to push for it. But I don't really think I will. I'll have to see how everything else pans out. I'm holding spots on a number of waitlists right now. Still waiting to hear from Brown (should hear on the 15th), USC, Columbia and Cornell. Then I can make some decisions! I hope this will be over soon...

2/22- ACCEPTED AT USC!!! I have to wait and see about financial aid, but I am 99 percent sure that I will be at Keck next year!!! I LOVED IT THERE! I am happy, happy, happy. All these months of applying and waiting and traveling and interviewing- it was long and hard and I thought I would die from the stress- but it's all been worth it for this moment. I am going out tonight to celebrate!!!

3/24- Accepted today at UW! It came out of nowhere but it was AWESOME! I'm so pumped!!!

5/7- After a loooooong thought out process, I decided to withdraw from all schools but USC. I loved it there and really want a chance to settle in California. Plus, my fiance has job prospects out there and it just makes sense. It's more expensive in the long run, but I'm ready to take a chance on something exciting and have an adventure!
