**Short summary of my 2 years of applying to med school**

Total Interviews: 16...I'm seriously broke :(
Total Acceptances: 6

In 2007, I applied to about 20 schools and interviewed at:

Johns Hopkins: very impressive, but the interview process was stressful and intimidating
Duke: AMAZING!!! my ultimate top choice, but it was not meant to be :(
UC Davis: out-dated curriculum, little research focus
UC Irvine: mediocre facilities, didn't feel like i fit in

I was waitlisted at Duke and Irvine and was not accepted to either.

In 2008, I applied a lot earlier and to >35 schools. I was accepted at 6 schools and waitlisted at 5. My advice to everyone: regardless of your stats apply early and broadly (don't apply to only top schools unless you discovered or cured a disease...seriously!) and interview at as many places as you can afford b/c it's a great experience. Enjoy the process, meet students, and learn as much as you can from your faculty interviewers. People you meet will be your future classmates, friends, and colleagues. Going to second looks is important. Rochester was my top choice among acceptances until I revisited Rochester and Wisconsin. Rochester is great, but I didn't like the city. Wisconsin just rocks!!

Good luck :)
