Summary of Application Experience

After 17 applications, 'immigration problems' galore, 5 interviews, 13 rejection letters, 2 waitlist notifications, 1 undecided admissions committee, and $5000 later-- I have been accepted and will be attending New York Medical College. My 'numbers' aren't spectacular and my non-US citizen / Perm Resi status didnt help -- But what really got me through is the post-baccalaureate research fellowship I've been doing at the National Institutes of Health for the past two years. http://www.training.nih.gov/

My first year at NIH, I studied to take the MCAT for the THIRD time, jumped from the low 20s the first two times to a 28! with a great Princeton review course, went down on my writing. :( Luckily I have a great PI, who gives me sooo much opportunites to enhance my app -- projects, papers, etc. Did REALLY BAD my first year undergrad (bad as is D+ in G Chem and Cs in Biology) - but brought it up significantly the next 3 yrs, and also took some academic classes during my fellowship. I think the big jump from really BAD to above average, even though it still evens out my final numbers to 'average', was to my advantage and showed schools I can improve drastically.

Some good advice for someone with my numbers - BE AGGRESSIVE and keep writing to the schools. I applied super early in June, which was a wise choice, and literally EVERY MONTH, i wrote each school something like papers published, new projects, things I volunteered at (even though it was even for a day!), people I'm helping in the lab, etc...even the littlest things can help I think -- to keep your file on the top of the pile :) . Keep trying to enhance your application in your post-bacc years and be around people that can help you do it. Don't apply and waste your money if you're not somewhat competetive. Be ready, financially, EMOTIONALLY, and have backup plans ALWAYS. This was my first time applying and I was surprised that I got 5 late interviews even with my low numbers, and I'm sooooo happy and relieved to get into at least one school. If I can get in, then there's hope for you -- just gotta play your cards right.
