Summary of Application Experience

I didn't decide for sure to go to med school until the beginning of my junior year of college, which seems really late to most people. I had started out in engineering, and although that probably had a slightly negative impact on my GPA, it meant I took far more science than most of the pre-meds at my school. I really enjoyed almost all of it though (the extra physics being the glaring exception to that rule) and I think it helped me get a better grasp on what I truly wanted to do.

I think I waas a little abnormal in the actual application process as well. I didn't really use resources like this site, SDN, or really any outside help. If I could do it all over again, I think I would have chosen different safety schools to apply to, because when it came down to it, I know I wouldn't have been happy at a couple of the ones above.

I submitted my AMCAS in early July, started receiving secondaries shortly after. All my secondaries (except UW, which didn't send me one) were done by about the third week of classes in early September. Cincinnati was my first interview, two weeks before Thanksgiving, and I was really glad to have them be first. Less pressure there than if it had been, say, Columbia. The day was fine, the school was alright, and my interview went pretty well. It was very question-and-answer format, but she seemed to like me. Sure enough, I was accepted a week later. Whew! Good start! And now I have the security of knowing for certain that I have a spot.

NYU interview in early December. Liked the school WAY better than Cinci. Bellevue hospital was great, and what's not to like about NYC (except the cost of living)? My interviewer wasn't having a great morning though, so I'm still not really sure how that affected his evaluation of me. I think I am least confident about this interview.

Wash U interview in the middle of finals week. Had a horrible, almost-migraine-like headache the night before the interview, which I was less than thrilled about. The interview, however, went just fine. And as usual, Wash U pulled out all the stops and lived up to my previous experiences with them. They took care of everything, made sure we met current students from all four years, and definitely had the best lunch of all the schools I went to :)

Columbia's invite came over Christmas break. I actually almost missed it, because they sent it to Cleveland even though I was in Atlanta. I only found out about it when the admissions office e-mailed me to say they were very sorry but had to re-schedule me. I, however, wasn't sorry in the least, as I would've had about 4 days to get travel arrangements together after getting back to school had that not happened. The interview itself was great. My interviewer was a lot of fun, and I absolutely LOVED the school. Tied with Wash U, in my opinion. I don't think I could have chosen one over the other, except that Columbia was in New York.

Shortly after that the wait-list letter came from all three schools. Kind of a bummer, but at least I had an offer. I wrote my letters of interest, and just hoped. By May I had decided it was time to start making plans to move to Cincinnati. Looked for apartments, starting setting up my paperwork & financial aid. I couldn't quite get excited about it though, because I had loved other schools so much more. After months of waiting, though, the phone call came from Wash U on June 15th! I wouldn't wish a wait-list on anyone, but it was the best feeling in the world to hear the Dean on the other end of that phone :)

From start to finish this whole thing took about a year. It was, without a doubt, the most nerve-wracking and expensive year I have ever experienced. But it all worked out in the end, and I couldn't be more thrilled about where I have ended up. St. Louis, here I come!
