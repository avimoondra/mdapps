Summary of Application Experience

Just now starting with AMCAS - hope to submit by end of July. Have two recommendations in the bag (my research professor is one, the other is an ER doc and clinical researcher with Emory), looking for two more in sciences. My GPA did the opposite of most people's - started high (3.70 after 1st year, 3.28 after 2nd year) and has taken a dive in the last two years, a lot to do with burning out in my second year due to pushing myself way to hard (couple of 7-class semesters + research) and having my girlfriend move to Nebraska (i know, boo-hoo, but she was my life at the time). When i started to burn out, I assumed it was some kind of ADD thing and made it worse by trying to medicate it. Long story short, GPA has crashed to 3.0-ish and waffling, and for reasons I don't really feel like talking about in admissions stuff. So basically I'm in the tedious position of having a lot of good credentials (MCAT, medical experience, recommendations, evidence of intentions for serving community, research), but a pretty disappointing academic record considering the amount of enthusiasm and dedication i really have to becoming a doctor and serving underpriviledged communities. It'll be a glaring juxtaposition to claim dedication when my transcript shows underachievement. Therefore, a lot of schools I will be applying to will fall in the category of pipe dream. I would be excited to get into Arkansas, my home-state school, and perform much better and aim for a good residency. I would love, though, to go to school in California or at Baylor. My long-term plans are to go into surgery (maybe trauma?) and work off my debt/get wartime experience with the military, and work part time in the U.S., part-time in a volunteer capacity with some international medical relief organization like MSF. Good luck to everyone!
