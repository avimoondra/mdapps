Summary of Application Experience

6/12/06 -- Submitted AMCAS. On hold pending transcript receipt.

6/19/06 -- MCAT scores available. At first I thought the series number (31) on the left of the page was my total score, but then when i looked across at the individual section scores, it didn't add up. So when I saw the real score -- a 40S, fully 10 points and 3 letters higher than I had expected -- I squeaked. The scores are automatically uploaded to AMCAS, but they still don't have my transcripts. Am going to call in the morning to see what's up with that.

6/20/06 -- Got the transcript-received email. So now I'm 'Ready to review.' Someone on SDN said that there are currently 1300 people in the queue, so it might take a while....

6/26/06 -- First secondary invite! From Columbia. But I'm still not verified on AMCAS, so methinks this is just a clever ploy to get my money. Still, it's a start.

7/10/06 -- AMCAS was verified over the holiday weekend. The secondaries just keep rolling in. I have three done so far (just need to proofread and write the checks/type in my credit card info), and the three VA schools still to do.

8/14/06 -- Back from vacation with two interview invites in my mailbox. Sweet!

8/28/06 -- Complete at all schools.

9/2/06 -- Busy preparing for my interviews. I recommend searching the interview feedback on studentdoctor.net for questions. Also, let your professors know ASAP if you're going to miss a class for interviews. Most are very understanding and will let you make up exams, but in some cases, you might need to juggle things around a bit.

9/12/06 -- First interview went well, I think. I knew the school mainly through its website, so the tour and talking with students really changed my opinion -- for the better. This place is focused on sculpting good clinicians, which is EXACTLY what I want to do in life. This school has zoomed to the top of my Supposedly-Unranked List.

9/26/06 -- Counting down the days until Oct. 15, the first day that schools are allowed to tell us if we got in. It seems interminable. On the upside, I think I'm improving with each interview. Most of them have similar questions (like the ubiquitous 'why medicine'), so each time I have a better, more focused answer. As for the nonverbal, subconscious interview skills, those are harder to hone. It's just a question of being comfortable in your own skin, I think.

10/2/06 -- Just returned from my insane interview triple-header. Extremely exhausted; it's time for a large dose of caffeine and then off to a full day of classes!

10/8/06 -- FYI, never ever do an interview triple-header (3 in a week). I've been thinking back on my responses to questions, and I think I got progressively worse as I became more and more tired. Which really sucks, because the last school there is one that I would really love to go to. Also I fell pretty far behind in classes and with my thesis research, which explains why I'm still at school during our Fall Break, enjoying cold showers and no food (not even crappy dining hall concoctions). OK, done whining. =)

10/15/06 -- So here it is, the big day that everyone's been counting down to. And I got ... nothing. That's if you don't count Friday's rejection letter from Stanford. I know it was always such a reach, and since one entire question on the secondary asked for scientific publications (of which I have none), it wasn't really a surprise. But the first rejection always hurts, especially since there are no acceptances yet to soften the blow. I'm hoping for some love this coming week. Otherwise, I may have to throw Plan B into action -- and trust me, I have an AWESOME Plan B.

10/17/06 -- It looks like I'm not going to be living in California after all, but that wasn't too much of a surprise. Still no acceptances. Waiting is more painful than anything else, including the 8-hour torture that was the MCAT.

10/18/06 -- I'm in! Just as rejections hurt, acceptances are beauty itself. And UVa is a well-respected school; I have ties to the community; I'd certainly be happy to stay here. Hooray!

10/26/06 -- USPS lost a decision that was mailed to my house and forwarded by my parents to my school address two weeks ago. Pissed doesn't even come close to what I feel right now. I'm going to call the school tomorrow, but getting the news over the phone is going to suck. A lot.

11/1/06 -- Honestly, I think this process is slightly screwed up. You can research all you want online, but really, no one knows how they're going to like a school until they get to the interview. That's when you really find out about the curriculum, the patient population, the type of students.... In most cases, my opinion of the school has stayed more or less the same (went down in one case), but some schools that used to be at the bottom of my list are now waaaaay up there, primarily based on what I've seen at the interview. If only schools were more amenable to pre-application tours, the way undergrad schools are!

11/20/06 -- I have choices! Choices are good! There's a little snafu with Feinberg's status page right now (it says class of 2010), but I hope that I'm still in despite some strange computer glitch. Now I'm checking out for Thanksgiving Break; happy turkey-eating, everyone!

12/4/06 -- The Feinberg letter arrived the day after Thanksgiving. The website hasn't changed though, which is strange, but oh well! Very excited, and completely unmotivated to study for finals.

12/12/06 -- I finally worked up the nerve to ask G'town what happened, since I hadn't heard from them since the file complete email. Apparently, they mailed me a letter on Sept 18, but the post office decided to eat it for lunch or something. What is with this? I've never had a problem with USPS until now. V. bizarre. Anyway, G'town wouldn't tell me what the decision was, so I asked them to mail me another letter. Sigh.

12/15/06 -- Mixed bag today: rejection from Georgetown and acceptance from VCU.

12/29/06 -- Interview invite from McGill = best early Christmas present, possibly ever.

1/18/07 -- Surely I can't be the only one with senioritis?

1/26/07 -- Done with all interviews!

2/11/07 -- Getting waitlisted at McGill really hurt. I am considering sending a letter, but since they are only taking 9 international students this year (Quebec government mandate), I don't know if it will actually help.

3/23/07 -- Having heard back from all schools except the ones I'm waitlisted at, I am still no closer to a decision. No clear winner emerges! I'm going to go on as many Second Looks as financially and academically possible (still in school) and try to make a decision by the end of April.

5/17/07 -- I made my decision just after all my Second Look weekends. For the most part, they were nothing really special, but The School gave me the warm fuzzies, and I'm super excited for next year. There was a slight hiccup when McGill called with an acceptance, but I was so much happier when I interviewed and Second Looked at The School than at McGill, so -- there you go.

For those of you who are still reading, here's my advice for the admissions process:

1. Make a list of schools by reading the MSAR, then visit websites/talk to students/lurk at SDN to choose the ones you're actually going to spend money on. My initial list was 40, I brought it down to 12, and in retrospect, I probably could have saved at least 200 dollars by not applying to at least two on my list.

2. Speaking of saving money, this is an expensive process. Save where you can -- $10 shoes from KMart and a $1.99 bottle of black polish will take you just as far as Prada heels. (Plus, cheap shoes meant I never worried too much about getting them muddy or wet.)

3. Chill at the interviews. The schools that I was waitlisted at were the ones that I was most nervous about, so I probably unconsciously stiffened and came across as awkward.

4. Stay open-minded. Your opinion of a school can and will change, and don't be afraid to acknowledge that your dream school is not the right fit for you.
