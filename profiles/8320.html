Summary of Application Experience

I'm taking a year off, and have a job as a research assistant doing clinical work.

My primary application was submitted and verified by June 20th (I think), or whatever the day the primaries were first sent out from AMCAS.

I decided to apply very broadly and submitted 27 primaries, which I narrowed to 20 secondaries. I received early interview invites from some of my top choices, which is so great, though I wished I would've known cause I would not have spent the money for some of the applications I submitted. But what's an extra $700 to get into med school??? Oh well, like I've always said, this process can be a lot like a game of Contra, Spray is always the best for the long haul, but for some of those level boss's the precision of the Laser can be clutch.

Very excited to go to columbia, unfortunately it's my first interview of the year cause I'm just sticking with the date they gave me in the letter.

Columbia interview went well, and the students there were so great. The way they run the tour is much better than the others I went on last week. I think there were more students walking around with us than there were interviewees and they were all genuinely interested in telling us why they chose columbia and why it's a great place.

Albert Einstein interview went so great though I don't know if it's for me.

Drexel interview was good, though the interviewer was from Thailand or Sri Lanka or something and was impossible to understand. The student interviewer was decent though. I liked the med school campus itself, but i'm looking for a more urban environment and the campus is about 10/15 minutes outside of the city where the hospitals and what not are.

Temple was a lot more impressive than I expected. It IS in a pretty shady area, though the med school campus and hospitals are all nice and have more of a campus feel which I really liked. The students all there were great as well and I felt very comfortable.

Case is next week, Penn is late October, and I scheduled Iowa for early December

10/2: Case interview went pretty well. I stayed with a student host and they were all pretty nice, a little nerdy, at least for my taste. I really liked their curriculum that is very self-directed and only requires you to be in class 8-12, which is perfect for me.

Looking forward to Oct. 15th

10/4: Wisconsin interview invite yesterday. I have heard very good things about Madison, and I went to a very similar school so I'm pretty excited about this interview. And according to USNews, for Out-of-state at Wisconsin: Applied:2379, interviewed: 138, accepted: 84. My odds look pretty good at this point :)

10/15: Got into Temple today, think I'll hear from Case Western tonite at midnight. It's gonna be a good nite to say the least. ;)

10/23: May withdraw from a couple of schools. I did really like Temple and after being back in Philly for the Penn interview I could definitely see myself living in Center City. As for Penn, it was even more amazing than I expected. Having heard the students were mostly ivy leaguers who looked down on the non-ivy kids I really made a point to talk to a lot of the students and tell them where I went (non-ivy). Granted it was my interview day so I didn't expect them to necessarily shrug me off, but I honestly did not get any negative impression at all. Everything about Penn seemed great: the 1.5 years of class work and 2.5 of rotations, the facilities, Philly, the students, international opportunities, etc. I'm really looking forward to mid-March already, ugh.

10/31: After withdrawing from a couple of schools to avoid the costs of interviews, I get invited to go to LA, probably the most expensive interview I could possibly go on. UCLA has always been one of my favorite places, so I have to go on this one.

11/2: Pitt interview was good, I live right here so it was nothing special.

12/10: Got back from my UCLA trip. I've been to LA several times and stayed with close friends so I made a vacation out of it. Anyways, the interview went very well. There wasn't much of a tour, but the students we met with were all very enthusiastic and helpful. It was pretty much just a luncheon with like 5 interviewees and 5 1st year students. The food was probably the best I've had at any interview as well (+). Ever since my first trip to LA I've wanted to go to UCLA, or at least have the chance. I honestly didn't have such high expectations for myself, but now that I am so close I really hope I get accepted.

Oh, and I got an interview at Miami the day of my UCLA interview. That was kinda awesome, I've been on a bit of a hiatus. 10 interviews out of 20 submitted secondaries...not too shabby.

1/23: Been a while. I think I'm pretty much finished up with interviews. A) unlikely to be invited to the schools I'm still waiting to hear from at this point and B) The schools I did interview at I really liked and I'm not sure if the interview travel would be worth it.

On that note, I really enjoyed Miami. I know some people are obsessed with rankings and what not, but I would have a hard time not going to Miami if I am accepted there as well as the other 'top-ranked' schools I interviewed at. I really feel like Miami is on the verge of a huge upswing...new dean from Duke bringing renowned faculty, new facilities...uh, would you turn down a chance to work and live in South Beach??? The patient contact is better than anywhere, students live on the beach, facilities are nice, brand new fitness center with amazing view, happy students, happy faculty, just an all around nice place to be.

2/22: Waitlist at Miami. A little disappointed, but I was told by my interviewer that the class may already be full and I would most likely be waitlisted. Other rejections have trickled in that I have been expecting. Anticipating a VERY LONG next few weeks as I'll be getting decisions from my last 3 schools

3/21: Accepted off the waitlist at Miami today!!! This is the perfect ending to a dramatic week (my dad went into surgery literally within 5 minutes of me getting the email from Dr. Hinkley). I am still waiting to hear from the other schools, but this is the exact situation I wanted to be in. I love Miami, and my best friend and room mate from college lives in south beach now, it's almost too good to be true.

3/27: Well, the wait is officially over...sort of at least. Waitlisted at 2 and rejected at 1 among my top 3 schools. The process never ends, LOIs and updates here I come.

6/16: I sent letters to both Columbia and Penn hoping to get accepted off the waitlists, but after calling last week, I know that it's not going to happen. All and all I am very pleased with how my applications turned out this year. I was fortunate to have so many inteviews, and I would've liked to have gone to one of the top schools I interviewed at (UCLA, Columbia, and Penn) which are in my opinion are the 3 most amazing schools in the country. I am very happy with Miami, and I'm very excited for the next 4 years. I know I will get a great education and have some fun living in South Florida, and hopefully touch up on my Spanish and get the opportunity to travel back to South America.

Most important advice I can give in order of importance:

1. APPLY EARLY, like before June 15th so that you're verified before AMCAS sends any primaries to schools (typically June 20th)

2. Apply broadly, I apply to reach schools even if you think you have no chance. Apply to schools all over the country and in various settings (city/rural) you never know what you're going to find out there

3. Take time to write a meaningful Personal Statement and Secondary Essays. I know my numbers weren't the best, but I had interviews at several top schools in the country and I don't think it was just a fluke. My essays and personal statement were all complemented on several occasions and I think really made the difference in my applications. If you know your numbers are low or average this is important.

4. If you really want to go to a school and have a compelling arguement... let them know. Write a letter to the dean of admissions, and put yourself out there.
