Summary of Application Experience

most schools sent secondaries or automatically allow you to fill it out. I had to do the schools whose deadlines were first, not by which ones i really wanted to go to. I took my time with the secondaries because i wanted to make sure they were a good representation of me. the hardest part was getting people to read my essays and waiting for their feed back. so i accidentally sent in some with typos etc.

Awesome. Both people read my ENTIRE App. The sugeon i had said he enjoyed reading my App and wanted to see me again so he could convince me to be a surgeon. the student interview went well, we talked about art and obama. I really liked the program and hope i get in.

1/8 So my first time in Palo Alto/Stanford, its a nice area. the campus is beautiful. my alarm didnt go off so i was late but i still made it to my interviews on time. i was expecting more applicants. there were only 4 of us interviewing.

the student interview went well. i liked the doc and student i was matched with. the doc asked me ethical questions with abortion and assisted suicide. i answered honestly. i took a stance against performing an abortion and refused to refer the fake patient to a doctor that would perform the procedure. i made my reasoning clear and made it clear that i would be empathetic with the women. my stance was clearly not his, but i had to be honest. if the school does not want me because of my stance then i know its not the right school for me.

the interviewer may not have agreed with me, but i dont think i shot myself in the foot, and in the end he told me he didnt care what i thought he just wanted to see how i would answer the question.

i encourage every applicant to be true to who they are and what they believe no matter what stance you take. Dont pretend to be someone you are not, they need to see the real you in order to see if youre a fit for the school.
