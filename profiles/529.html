Mistakes I Made Along the Way:
**I did not pre-write any secondary applications. Big mistake. When they come flying in every day, things get really stressful. Do yourself a favor and pre-write some school-specific answers while you're waiting for your AMCAS app to get verified.
**My Non-Clinical volunteering experiences are pretty weak (at least since coming to college). It's not even for lack of trying, it's just very disorganized. Through various clubs and opportunities, I've tutored, visited hospices and retirement homes, cleaned up litter on the side of the road, etc. But it's all very sporadic. Rather than being focused on one specific non-clinical experience, I sort of haphazardly exposed myself to all of them (which in and of itself isn't a weakness). This strategy ends up looking pretty weak when you have to actually sit down and document hours on a secondary, although I suppose I have plenty to talk about in an interview (if I get one, knock on wood).
** I ended up withdrawing from Wayne State & Colorado SOM pre-secondary submission (post-secondary invite) via email, because I just didn't get that strong feeling that I wanted to go there. Withdrawing via email is very scary, especially when you know that adcoms might talk to each other. I really hope that my email was polite and that their adcoms were gracious about it. I guess we won't know how it affected me until after the cycle is over. :/
** I made my school list before I received my MCAT score. If you think my list looks kind of haphazard, that's because it is. It was formulated off of my own intuition about what my scores might have been, an intuition which was very bad and fluctuated dramatically depending on my mood. I wish I had taken the MCAT a little earlier, that way I could have cut out a few early on.
