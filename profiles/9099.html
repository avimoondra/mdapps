Summary of Application Experience

S - Submitted Secondary, C - Application Complete, I - Received Interview Invitation (Date), A - Accepted, R - Rejected, W - Waitlisted

5/6: I've decided on Vanderbilt. I think it's the best choice for me, and I really liked everyone at second look. I hope this profile will help you in your application!

3/21: I just went ahead and withdrew from UAB. They've taken their sweet time getting around to making a decision, and I have no desire to interview there anyway. Even if accepted (unlikely, given the hold), I see no compelling reason to go when I'm sitting on acceptances at Vandy and Emory. I like TN and GA better, and it's not like it would be any cheaper. So now the interview season is officially (well, as soon as I get my Yale rejection) over. I'll be making my decision in April after I get my financial letters and second look at Vandy.

2/20: Waitlisted at NYU and finally received the official rejection from GWU. Pretty much as expected. Now we just have to wait for Harvard, Columbia, and Yale. It's also doubtful that I'll get anything from UAB, so I'm guessing the interview phase of the cycle is over for me.

2/5: Rejection from WashU -- only took them five months. I'm sure more will be on the way shortly as the non-rolling schools are basically done with interviews.

1/28: Non-related medical school admissions news: I finally found a Nintendo Wii at Wal-Mart. Woohoo! Now I will surely become a couch potato until matriculation.

1/27: Accepted off the waitlist at Vandy. The alma-mater comes through! I'm pretty surprised -- thought I'd be waiting until May or so to hear anything. I guess I was fairly high on the list then. Looks like NYU will be the last one I hear from. Good luck everyone. :)

1/26: A decision has been mailed to me from Cornell. Yay for rejection. Oh well, I don't care for all-PBL anyway.

1/8: After much deliberation, I've decided not to accept my Wake interview. I just can't see myself going there over Emory for a variety of reasons, and I think I'll just be throwing money away on airfare, hotel, etc. Still waiting on NYU :(

12/18: Accepted at Emory! Man, that was fast. Honestly, I was expecting to be rejected or waitlisted, so this is so sweet! I will probably hear something any day now from NYU if there's good news. Here's hoping...

12/12: Looks like the DC area is basically out.

12/11: Scheduled my Wake Forest interview for January and got rejected by UVA. I guess I saw that coming since I'm OOS, but I have friends there. So that kinda sucks. I foresee more coming unfortunately... :( Adding $500 for the NYU interview since that's what my parents feel was my contribution (Manhattan hotels are ridiculous!). My hotel at Emory was $194. I was really impressed by both of these schools, and I could definitely see myself at either one. Here's hoping I have some choices come May! Total so far: $2654.

11/30: Apparently everyone in Chicago hates me...

11/20: Interview at Emory. Wow this last week or so has been ridiculously awesome! :D

11/14: Accepted at UT-Memphis! I'm officially going to be a doctor!!!! w00t w00t w00t!

11/13: Northwestern rejection -- first one :( At least they finally told me something though after 2.5 months!

11/10: NYU NYU NYU NYU!!!!!!

11/9: Officially complete at all schools except Duke (probably withdrawing). Let the thumb twiddling continue...

11/3: Waitlisted by Vandy :( This *really* sucks, as it was definitely at the top of my list. I need UT to pull through for me now more than ever...

10/30: Interviewed at UT-Memphis today and loved it! Also received an interview invite to ETSU, so it was a really good day overall. I think I may not submit Duke because it's so long, I'm not sure I like their curriculum, and I doubt I'll get in anyway. Haven't decided for sure, but that's my current thinking.

10/22: Submitted Columbia ($75). One more to go! Of course Duke has six essays, so it's not like I'm almost done. And I should've finished this one a long time ago because I had it pre-verification. Oh well. Still waiting on inteviews... Total so far: $1960.

10/18: Submitted Georgetown ($130 - ouch). That's the last of the rolling admissions apps! Two more to go: Columbia and Duke. Now I wish I hadn't sat on P&S for so long; I would've only had Duke left. Oh well. So that makes seven more essays to do before I'm done. It's *so* tempting to just not do Duke... I would love to go though, so I guess I'll grin and bear it. Interviews with UT-Memphis start next week. Total so far: $1885.

10/13: Submitted UChicago ($75). Woohoo, light at the end of the tunnel... Total so far: $1755.

10/11: Interviews at UT-Memphis and Wake Forest! Both are a little odd though. For UT I have *two* interviews for some reason, on different dates and in different cities (Memphis and Smyrna). Didn't realize they did that. Also, I don't have an interview scheduled at Wake yet. I emailed yesterday (contact them, don't wait!) and got a response today saying I was in the top interview pool, but all of their dates were full through December. They put me on the cancellation list in case someone drops out. If that doesn't work out, they're going to start scheduling interviews after the new year once they get August MCATers reviewed. Still counting this as a win ;)

10/5: Submitted Yale secondary ($75). Yes I put off Chicago and Georgetown (both rolling), and that was probably dumb. I'm working on it! Total so far: $1680.

10/5: I would advise emailing Harvard admissions if you haven't received a notice of completion. I after waiting six weeks, I finally did and boom, complete the same day! Apparently they just hadn't downloaded my rec letters. Oh well. I'd kill for the interview :D

10/4: Finally received Duke secondary. Sheesh, what took so long? Website says return within 2 weeks. That's gonna suck... :(

10/2: Submitted University of Virginia ($75). Hurray, one more down. UChicago is next on the list. Not sure where in the world Duke is... Total so far: $1605.

9/29: On hold at George Washington. Blah :(

9/28: Vanderbilt interview scheduled for 10/9 - Sweet :)

9/28: My status was updated at UAB and I am now listed as 'Remain Under Consideration for Interview.' So apparently I have been reviewed but not offered an invite at this time? There are only two other people on mdapps that have an invite right now that I can tell, so I guess I shouldn't worry. Still kinda sucks...

9/28: Finally submitted Vanderbilt ($50). It was looooong and took forever. So happy to have the interview though, and now I can finally call and schedule my first one! Woohoo :) Total so far: $1530.

9/15: Finally submitted ETSU ($50) - it wasn't that bad, but I had to think pretty hard on the essays. Now it's hardcore time for Vandy... Total so far: $1480.

9/14: Added 'Complete status' so you can see when receipt of all required materials was acknowledged.

9/13: Submitted Wake Forest ($55). My essays were about 400 words apiece; I hope they consider that 'brief.' I thought I did a pretty good job on two of them... Total so far: $1430.

9/11: Submitted Weill Cornell ($75). Wasn't bad - short essay about why I want to go there. Probably should've done it early since it was short - I might have had an interview by now! Oh well. Wake is almost done, I'm just trying to think of what to put in the 'optional' field. Total so far: $1375.

9/8: Submitted UAB secondary ($75). I tried and tried to think of something ot put in the 'Is there anything we should know about you?' space, but I just couldn't think of anything meaningful... Hope that doesn't hurt. Visited Birmingham last week - actually pretty cool place! If I get accepted, I'd rather be there than Huntsville or Tuscaloosa. We'll see - long way to go. Total so far: $1300.

9/7: Sweet - interview invite to Vanderbilt! Alma mater showing the love :) Now I just have to start working on the 3000 words worth of essays... Makes me wish I hadn't procrastinated as much on the others that are due in about a week :

9/5: Submitted Emory ($90) - not too bad. They're getting rougher since I'm basically putting off the more essay-heavy ones until later. ETSU, UAB, and Wake are all priority right now since they have deadlines. Total so far: $1225.

9/1: Received ETSU, Wake Forest, and UAB today. Crazy, because Wake is due in 3 weeks and UAB in two. ETSU may have a deadline too, but I didn't check. Up until now, I think Northwestern was the only one that had a deadline from date of submission. I'm actually at the beach at the moment, so I won't be working on these for a few days... Good to know I got past the screens for Wake and ETSU though. I figured in-state w/ better than average scores would get me through ETSU. Some people haven't had very good luck with Wake though. Sweet :-D

8/29: Submitted Northwestern's secondary today ($75). Went and got 12 2''x2'' photos made last night for any pictures needed. Also received and email from Wake Forest that says my AMCAS has been reviewed, and they are sending me a secondary. The email didn't say anything about that, but I guess I passed the screen! Total so far: $1135.

8/24: Received Northwestern's secondary today - due September 7. Guess I have to get a move on... :(

8/22: Went ahead and wrapped up the UTHSC app ($50). Hurrah, another one down, and $50 poorer! Total so far: $1060.

8/22: Received UT-Memphis and NYU secondaries. Submitted NYU ($100) because it was really easy. Basically finished UT, but I have to get a picture and print out some stuff, so that's not going out quite as fast. Total so far: $1010.

8/21: Secondaries are officially rolling in. Submitted Harvard because it was really quick ($85). Working on Cornell and Emory. Total so far: $910.

8/19: AMCAS finally verified! This process is ridiculous - the people obviously have never heard of first come-first serve since people who submitted a *week* after me were verified before me. Oh well. My AO GPA is 3.99, and my BCPM actually went up a little to 3.88, so I'm pretty happy about that. They actually left off a 4-hour course during my JR year though, so I submitted a change request form. After all that, they still didn't get it right! On to the secondaries - no excuse for procrastination now :( As always, thanks for your comments.

7/31: Submitted WashU ($50) and GWU ($105) secondaries. Pretty easy, no big essays. Total so far: $825.

7/18: Finally submitted AMCAS ($670). Also added Cornell. And now we play the waiting game...

7/12: Finishing up the PS, then will submit (hopefully this week). I know, I know - should've had it done already.
