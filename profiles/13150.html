Brief Profile:
i do a lot of random things that don't thematically tie together - i'm in a liberal arts honors program called Plan II and take classes in really random subject areas like philosophy, astrophysics, english, problem solving, logic, medical ethics. if you already know of the program, let me tell you that as weird as you think it is, it's really really weird being in it. haha :) i love to make music - i was concertmaster for the university symphony orchestra. i teach violin lessons. i help lead worship for Chinese Bible Study, a part of InterVarsity Christian Fellowship. i design webpages for a company. i spent a summer at Baylor Med in a surgery internship program. i don't have any science research experience but i do research in a psychology lab studying romantic interactions and styles of communication. i enjoy cooking. i love to read and write. i'm writing my senior thesis on the topic of altruism in humans and in animals and the motivations we have for doing selfless things that are really just selfish.

as for premed - i kind of learned the rules of the premed students life as i went - from friends and from studentdoctor.net. i never joined a pre-professional health organization. i think as a general statement, i really don't like hanging around premeds that are obsessive about grades and competition. it makes my skin itch. i'm not sure if this was wise but i didn't make contact with the health professions office till a few months ago for applications.

Summary of Application Experience

my mcats scores are kind of skewed. i had a 102 fever that day and decided to take it anyway. i was practicing a little higher but i decided to keep it because i think i only suffered maybe 2 points. i also owned the writing section and i thought that would be cool to keep around -- to offset my verbal score.

i applied both in and out of state. i flew to my upenn interview and though the school was amazing, i realized that i wasn't really built for school out of state - i'm not sure why. so even though my interview went really well, i would still rather stay in state. maybe i'm just really emotionally tied to this place and the people here. i feel very much at home in texas. i guess the money issue is pretty complicated too. upenn costs ilke $55k a year. :(

the texas match matched me at my #1 choice - ut southwestern. i'm really thankful for that because my numbers there were right about 'average' for them. i spent a lot of time on my application - i think i wrote a pretty good essay. i got positive feedback on it during interviews.

interviews were fun. for some reason, i really enjoyed them. i just tried to be friendly and engaging. i talked about things that make my heart flicker -- like violin, guitar, psychology, cooking, family, friends, relationship... i don't know why but i really really enjoyed interviews -- even when the interviewer was clearly trying to mess with my head with ethical questions. one woman even asked me to sing for her...

the waiting game sucks. waiting for mcat scores. waiting for school decisions. but i do feel like i presented myself as honestly as possible - my passions, my loves, my desires, my motivations. all i did was be myself. i wasn't afraid to talk about things like my cooking prowess on my application. this was partly because i really believe that there is power in individual passion. and also and i didn't feel like i wanted/could pretend to be a 'science-personality' with the research experience and such.

some advice: be yourself. apply early. get feedback from peers (on essays, applications, struggles). be responsible. don't stress. don't give in. don't be afraid of being different. don't do this if your heart isn't here. study judiciously. enjoy life. enjoy college. enjoy your time with people because that's going to be your life's profession.

right now, i really feel way too blessed.

update: i will be going to ut-southwestern. the living accomodations in the new apartments will be really really nice and cozy. thankful, excited and a little nervous :)
