Summary of Application Experience

I think my research letter sucked. My PI is a dick sometimes and it might bite me if I get interviews, but I'm pretty sure I can spin the situation in my favor. The rest of my letters were solid so I think I'll be okay. Remember, it's not what is said by someone else, it's how you explain it.

Just a word to the wise. The whole secondary process is draining and idiotic. The UC schools ask stupid pointless questions to weed people out. UC Davis asks a plethora of innane questions such as 'What can you offer to society.' Please spare me of this. What can anyone offer to society. I want to be a doctor damnit so it makes sense I'll end up helping society. Some schools are honest and I respect that. Tufts was honest and upfront as well as Rochester about just wanting your money for the secondary.

I managed to get secondaries to all the UC's probably based on my GPA. My MCAT's are decent, the biology score is a little low, but hopefully my BCMP GPA will show that I know what I am doing. Luckily my verbal was great. If you are studying for the MCATs, use the EXAMKRACKER books and just study off those. I made the mistake of doing the Princeton Review also. It was a waste of money. Just study on your own about 5 months ahead of time and buy lots of practice tests. Study hard so you don't have to retake! It's a pain to study in the summer when others are either filling out applications or relaxing.

I applied to a lot of schools because I just want to get into a school. I don't really care which it is as long as it is U.S. based. If I have a choice I'll go with the best ranked. It may be shallow but I have talked to doctors who said that your school reputation is importane in the future. So take that into account if you have a choice.

I am in the process of sending secondaries and am waiting for interview dates if they come at all. I know this stuff is a 'crapshoot' and I'll be happy just to get my foot in the door. I am not expecting on getting into the top schools like UCSF, UCLA, Stanford, etc. I just did it in case they accidentally accept me in some fit of insanity.

When sending out secondaries in the mail, use priority mail with delivery confirmation. It's only $4.30 and it gets there in 3 days easy. This way your stuff won't get lost in the mail and you won't have to worry about some postal worker flunky jacking your check. The priority envelopes are basically light cardboard so people can't really see in the big ones.

Being a pre-med is a degrading experience because of the number of people who try and fail. Because of that everyone, especially professors treat you like an idiot if you tell them you want to goto medical school, probably because they couldn't get in, or they're sick of whiny pre-meds which are very common. Can't say that I blame them.

I also used INQUARTA. I know people say the owner is sleazy but I don't mind. People also say you can get the same stuff from your school. I goto a public school with a lot of other pre-meds. The school tries to help out, but it's so impersonal. They don't even fake they care. At least if you pay money they have to help you. THe process is already stacked against the individual applicant with so many people applying and so few seats, I think it's best to take every advantage possible and have someone in your corner. The company is helpful, prompt, and prepare you for everything.

(Note-I know people will say I work for the company but I don't. I'm just customer and no one forced me/asked me to write about them. I just want to relay my experiences. Some people might have it differntly if they have a sibling or realtive who is in medical school and knows the process well. I am basically the first in my family and extended family so I thought I needed the help).

It is expensive but the whole process is expensive and so is medical school, so suck up the cost and get in the first time. There is no way I want to do this crap again.

I probably sound bitter but I'm really not. Just a little annoyed but it's nothing too serious. I just want to get into a medical school and relax a little this senior year.
Sorry if parts of this aren't coherent, I don't feel like proofreading.

I will update as time passes. If you have any comments or questions just hit me up at [email protected]

10-9-04
A couple of schools have responded. Unfortunately, it's been mostly negative. I have been put on hold for UCSD, UCI, and Penn State. Vanderbilt rejected me pre-secondary. I thought everyone got secondaries for them. If anyone knows, please email me.

It's a little depressing getting rejected/put on hold. I know that a majority of people get rejected from a lot of schools, but it always stings a little when some institution tells you to take a hike. I thought the process was going to be a lot smoother when I got my first interview. Then came 4 straight messages from the above schools to beat it. I'm always a little apprehensive checking the mail nowawdays. Scared of those damn letters starting with, 'Unfortunately, due to the high amount of applicant....'

I wish I was trying for law school. Then I wouldn't have to worry about this BS. I feel like some bum, beggin any school to take me. This is too demeaning. You work hard for your whole life, and some arbitrary committee with some vague set of rules on acceptances tells you to get lost.

I know people will say 'Life's not fair, moron.' To you I say 'Eat me.'

11-11-04
Some updates

Some good news, I got interviewed at the University of Iowa. Great school and relaxed interview. I would love to go there, but who knows, the odds are against me. Luckily I have gotten a few more interiviews at the Albany Medical College and the Medical College of Ohio. I'm glad that some schools are showing me some love.

But I've gotten plenty of rejection letters to balance out any good news as Georgetown, Dartmouth, UC Davis, and Mayo have given me the finger.

If you're a California resident, good look with those stinking UCs. So far, Irvine, San Diego, and Davis have essentially rejected me. Basically, only SF and LA are left, and I'm no fool. With my stats, I know I'll be getting rejected from those two, and probably USC to boot. What pisses me off is Irvine. I go there for undergrad and the school isn't even ranked and they still put me on hold. I'm just contrasting this with the University of Iowa which is ranked #29 in the nation.

This process is garbage. A word of advice to all the aspiring pre-med students, apply to many many many schools. I did a lot (about 35) which I think is the smartest thing to do. Don't be an idiot and complain about the money. You're gooing to be at least $100,000 in debt on average, spend the extra $2000-$4000 on the application process and just get in. And apply to a wide variety. I mean, UCI gave me the finger but Iowa was actually interested in me. Whether I get in or not is another story, but at least I got my foot in the door.

Another point I want to bring up is the fact that UC schools and medical schools in general seem to relist the fact that they reject thousand of qualified applicants. What the hell? Shouldn't we try to find a place for them instead of treating them like garbage. Trying to become a medical doctor isn't some psur of the moment decision. I including many others put in countless hours of volunteer work, studying, and sucking up to just have a shot at even considering applying. It's brutal to think that all my work and my life essentially is in the hands of some jerkoff admissions committee who only know me by some numbers and some trivial questions they ask on their ridiculous secondaries. The worst part is, if I don't get in this year I'll have to wait another year at what will be my last chance at getting in.

If you're in high school or even in college, I would highly recommend just going abroad to do medical school. I know people laugh at the schools like St. Georges, but really, whose laughing when their students are practicing in the United States while other suckers like me are killing ourselves just to try to get into medical school. I have heard many good things about the schools in Europe as well and know a couple of people going that route. I also know many people who went to St. Georges and are back in the U.S. fully practicing medicine with other students who graduated from schools like UCLA.

Just something to think about.

Looking back on this, if I had known about it then, I would have just went through that process for the simple fact that I know I am smart enough and serious enough to become a medical doctor. I don't want to be an arrogant jerk but being a good doctor is about knowing your material well. And to the people who say you need good communication skills, bah, whenever I go see various doctors for checkups or other things in my health insurance, I always run into jerks who couldn't talk to a patient properly if their life depended on it. They just happen to know their stuff so well it doesn't really matter. Being abroad, you're basically in medical school and as such, the grading is pass/not pass so it is not meant to fail you like in the undergrad schools here.

Also, you don't have to waste your time doing other activites, and although I enjoyed a lot of them, to be honest, they are a pain when I am trying to maintain a high GPA. If GPA didn't matter then I wouldn't complain.

1-18-05

Well, luckily I have gotten that one magical spot at the medical school for Saint Louis University so I'm pretty happy about that. Going through this process, I didn't realize how grueling and stressful it is. Through your entire premedical years, your fate is in your hands because you can control what grades you get and your MCAT scores but once you send those applications in, you're reduced down to some 1 page essay with a list of activities. I'm just happy I found out relatively early in the process because I've heard of many people finding out in May and June. At least now, I can go in to my interviews at Ohio State and Albany with some peace knowing that if things don't go as planned I still have SLU.

5-4-05
As I type this I have a midterm and paper due in 4 hours and have been awake for the last 24 hours. I feel like a pathetic freshman all over again. Oh well.

To update, I managed to get into SLU and the Medical College of Ohio. I withdrew from Albany because in all honesty, it is essentially like SLU, just with less research money and on the east coast. I don't mean to bash the school or anything because everything I saw there was fine. I was rejected from the University of Iowa. I figured that was a longshot considering I was out of state. Now I am on my final waitlist at The Ohio State University. I really enjoyed my interview there and thought the school was fantastic. If I can get off the waitlist I will attend for sure. I made sure the school knew of that as well. But for now I have to make my realistic choice between SLU and MCO. I was kind of torn between the two and avoided making a decision on them but the deadline is approaching. I can pretty much say now that I will attend MCO.

When I started this process, I told myself I was going to be a ranking whore and go to the highest ranked place that accepted me. Technically, SLU is ranked number 62 according to USNEWS, but I just got a gut feeling with MCO. SLU is a great school with a lot of opportunities but when I apply for residency, if me and some SLU student are competing for the same spot with equal numbers I really don't think that the SLU name will give him more sway. I mean, if this was John's Hopkins or something, that's a different story. But I got a good vibe from MCO and their matchlist was essentially the same as SLUs so I really don't think I am missing out on anything really. Plus I get instate residency after a year at MCO, so I can get out with a pretty decent sized debt load. Couple that with the cheap living in Toledo, I can live like a king. Toledo seems like a 'whatever' city but I'm a pretty boring guy, so nightlife/arts/museums and stuff really didn't factor into my decision. As long as I have my 'gitar' I can be happy anywhere. But if I get into Ohio State, I'll go there for sure. But I'm completely happy/at peace at MCO.

The process has been humbling to say the least. I think I racked up something like 40+ rejections, 5 interviews, and 2 acceptances. Maybe 3 if I get lucky. I really think my letters of rec killed me. I plan on getting my grubby hands on them to see what's up. But I also think my MCAT score could have been a lot better. I scored poorly in the Bio section and I'm a bio major. Unfortunately, I had very poor study skills at that time, and actually woke up 2 AM the night before the test to memorize soem stuff on DNA and G chem formulas. I know if I had taken the August exam I could have rocked it. I'm sure everyone says this, but I really mean it. Honest!

I have gotten pretty lazy now this senior year knowing my future is somewhat in place. But I plan on reigniting that competitive fire so I can do well in medical school. Not competitive as in, be a jerkoff to my fellow students, but just study hard and be focused.

6-22-5
I got the call from Ohio State that I was accepted off their waitlist. I'm really happy about this because of all the school's I interviewed at, I really liked OSU the most because of everything it had to offer in terms of currriculum, research, atmosphere, and overall feel. I'm thrilled to be done with this process too. It can be really stressful especially if you are in the middle of the road in terms of numbers like me and your experiences aren't all that insane.

But it doesn't matter anymore. I got into the place I wanted and can breathe easy for a month or so until classes start.

My waitlist experience was something else. This was the only school i was waitlisted at. I initially interviewed at OSU in mid February which was kinda late. The admission people even told us that there were only a couple of spots left and that it was possible to gain an outright acceptance it was unlikely. So a week after my interview I find out I am waitlisted and turn in the proper form to OSU to stay on the waitlist. I then got a little aggressive because I knew that I needed to update my file to show that I was still interested.

So I sent them several updates/letters of interest/intent. My first couple of letters stated that I was still active in my outreach tutoring club and that I loved the school. I outlined how I was a good fit for the school and that I would be an asset to the student body. I also sent an email to the Dean of admissions, Dr. Batisky. He's a great guy and I did interview with him when I went to the school in February. He placed my email in my file along with my letters. I also included my intent to attend if accepted even though I was currently accepted elsewhere. Overall, I sent around 4 letters to the admissions office including 2 emails to Dr. Batisky.

I feel fortunate to get in, especially since I wasn't doing anything amazing during this last year. I wasn't really able to get letters of rec since I turned in 6 for my initialy application. And I wasn't doing too much stuff that involved working with people who were capable of writing me letters. So I felt a little disadvantaged when other people on SDN mentioned they were going to send more letters of rec and information about their new publications. But I did graduate cum laude and found out I was nominated PBK as a junior so I was able to update with those pieces of information.

Luckily it paid off in the end. I asked the admissions director if my updates played a role in my acceptance off the waitlist and she said that it does play a role in showing the school that you are interested. So remeber that something is better then nothing.

I guess this is my final update since everything else is complete. I have a few pieces of advice.

Be humble because with so many people applying there are applicants smarter then you and more personable then you. Also be able to deal with getting crapped on once in awhile. I don't mean take abuse, but for me, when I talked to professors and they found out I was pre-med sometimes I got the feeling they thought I was an idiot because a lot of people start out pre-med and end up changing/failing. Just stay confident and know your own abilities.

Study hard for your classes and especially the MCAT. Forget TPR and Kaplan and just use ExamKrackers exclusively. Buy all of their material, study that for 3-4 months straight and save some cash and get a 35+.

The premed years can be a pain, but remember that it will all be worth it in the end, when you get that acceptance evelope or that call from the dean. I still remember the day my mom called me, telling me that I got an acceptance.

Remember to do your volunteer stuff. No one wants to accept a student who doesn't have extra curricular activities. Try to shadow a doctor especially. You'll get some good stories to talk about and some decent experiences.

When you apply, if you are in the middle of the road, apply broadly. I spent a ton of cash on this garbage, but I don't care. It's an investment in myself and it will pay itself off. And it is better to apply broadly then reapplying. And also remember that this crap has a random feel for the majority of people. Unless you have a 40+ MCAT, nothing is a sure thing. I got rejected from 'unranked' schools such as NYMC and Rosalind Franklin only to get interviews at UIowa and OSU. Plus even being a CA resident I couldn't even get an interview at my own school UCI or any other UC. I guess that's life.
