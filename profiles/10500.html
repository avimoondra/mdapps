Summary of Application Experience

10.26 I've had a few interviews at Albert Einstein, UCSD and St. Louis and have interviews at Drexel and NYMC in December. Started off with a wave and now in a major lull period (4 weeks without another interview invite!) Got waitlisted at UCSD but planning to try hard to get off that waitlist! I'm working and volunteering full time so it's been hard trying to finish all the secondaries. Just got my secondary for Davis so I'm working on that now.

11.10 Got another interview at Albany... although they won't let me change it to the week of my NYMC interview so now I have to plan ANOTHER trip out to New York. This is getting expensive. I heard that the schools were supposed to be good about giving you interview dates, especially flying out from the west coast but none of the schools I've talked to so far have been that accomadating.

11.21 I'm going to medical school! Got accepted into St. Louis but I'm hoping I get other ones because it's really expensive and in Missouri. At least I got one...

11.28 Interview at UCI! Very excited for this one. Finishing my secondary for UCLA that I thought would never arrive. Booked my tickets for NYMC and Drexel... decided to stay in Philly for the weekend to look around.

12.15 Had my interviews at both NYMC and Drexel. I really liked NYMC though I'm concerned over the cost of tuition and you need a car because it's in the middle of nowhere. However, 3rd and 4th year students are allowed to live in the city and do rotations which is a huge plus. Drexel was equally as nice though I spent the weekend in Philly and don't know if I really want to live there. Next interview is 1/4 at UCI (fingers crossed).

2.4 So my interview at UCI went HORRIBLY. The guy kept cutting me off, asking really curt questions and didn't talk well about UCI... it's also been over a month and I haven't heard about interviews from any other schools and got a rejection from Rochester... BUT... I just got a call today (Saturday) at 6:00pm from the Dean of Admissions and I got into UCI!!! I'm thrilled because my top two schools are UCI and UCLA so I'm living the California pre-med/application dream. I got into a UC! I feel like a huge load has been lifted off my back! Still waiting for interviews from Davis and UCLA but am not too stressed about them anymore!

3.15 Right now I'm STILL waiting for interviews from UCLA and Davis, however, I am happily getting ready and filling out my financial information to UCI. It would be nice to interview at UCLA but I'm kind of over the whole process by now. I finally got acceptance letters from Drexel (3/7) and NYMC by e-mail (3/8) but I withdrew from Drexel but am holding on to NYMC in case something horrible happens with UCI.

3.22 FINALLY got an interview at Davis... for sometime in April. Not expecting much, but at least it's another school to consider. Also, finally got my UCLA rejection letter earlier this week. SCREW THEM.

4/19 Interviewed at Davis and the school was really cool. They have an MD/MPH option which I was looking for in a school. They also have a free clinic like UCSD which I really really liked. That may pull me away from Irvine. BUT... I have to get in first and they've already accepted as many as the class holds so I guess I have to wait until May 15th for the answer. Sacramento is not impressive and it doesn't compare to San Diego at all... but I guess I'd have to make do. UCI second look date is May 7th? so I guess that'll help me make a decision. Thanks for the comments... hope you guys are doing well! Davis is also unique in that it accepts a lot of non-traditional (i.e. older, minority, married students with families) into their program. I think they purposely look for these people and not necessarily those with the best numbers and activities.

4/22 Throughout the interviews I've met a lot of cool people and kept in touch with a bunch that went to UC's so I thought I would compile a list of schools that I've noticed a lot of UC people have interviewed at and got accepted into. I wish I would've known this earlier so that I didn't waste money on some of the other schools. By the way, the MSAR (medical school admission requirements) is also a great source to see how many out of state students the schools let in. Here are the ones to for sure apply to (if you don't have the 3.9 and 42 that is):

- NYMC - My interview had about 15 UC kids and 1 non-UC applicant.
- Drexel - Accepts tons of UC students.
- Albert Einstein - Also loves the UC kids.
- St. Louis University - Loves Cal kids so much they hold regional interviews in LA and SD.
- Vanderbilt - I didn't apply there but a couple of friends got interviews.
- Penn State - Friend accepted there.
- George Washington - Heard a rumor that if there is a good chance you'll get into a UC school, and you are from California, they won't even interview you (or at least that's what I keep telling myself)
- Rosalind Franklin - I've heard.
- SC - Loves research applicants, though they have no research at their med school... dumb.
- Georgetown - Watch out... heard they are anti-abortion.
- Chicago-Pritzker - So I've heard.
- Med. College of Wisconsin - They like our cheese.

The top four are ones pretty much all of my UC friends got interviews to and the majority at my interviews there were from the UC's. The rest I heard about... hope this helps.

So I'm still positive thus far about the whole process (fingers crossed). I hope that others find this helpful since I didn't have the 3.8 and 35 on the MCAT (and I took it twice!). Also, they keep calling me a 'non-traditional' student because I'm taking two years off between undergrad and med school... though not by choice; i had to take the MCAT over again. I encourage people to apply early and take time off to get those secondaries done! I'm still doing them and once the interviews start you feel like you should be done with that 'stage' and don't want to write anymore. I would love to hear any comments or feedback! Good luck to anyone else applying.
