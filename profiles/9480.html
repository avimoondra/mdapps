Summary of Application Experience

S = Submitted, C = Complete, II = Interview Invite, I = Interview date, R = Rejected, H = Hold, WD = Withdrew

11/21/06
I submitted AMCAS/TMDSAS in early August and was verified in last August/early September. My letters of recommendation were not uploaded to VirtualEval until 10/9/06.

I created this profile once I received my first acceptance. If I can get into a medical school with my GPA, anyone can. I slacked off the first few years at Hopkins, but made up for it my last 3 semesters and during my Master's program. You can overcome a subpar undergrad GPA if you're willing to work hard and keep at it (a good MCAT score and solid letters of recommendation don't hurt either).

One thing I find weird is that I've gotten an acceptance from EVMS as an out of state applicant, but haven't heard anything from the TX schools. I guess that shows how random this process really is.

11/27/06
Was able to switch my Tufts interview from 1/16 to 12/7. Back-to-back interviews is going to be tough, but it definitely works better for my schedule. Rosalind Franklin also decided to reject me, even though they sounded pretty positive about my passing the 'initial screen.'

11/28/06
Withdrew from a few schools I wasn't particularly keen on after EVMS acceptance letter (no public health schools or high interview travel costs, except for Wake Forest). If I get another acceptance or two I'll probably withdraw everywhere else except the TX schools.

11/29/06
Just found out UTMB is finished interviewing this Friday. No interview = rejection. Apparently UTSA is also sending out their final interview notifications on Friday. I'm not expecting good news.

12/4/06
No love from SA

12/9/06
Got back from MCW and Tufts. So far MCW is the only school I haven't liked, where I really couldn't see myself spending four years there. The students seemed stressed and not happy to be there, and my student interviewer didn't really seem to care about the interview. If the only time you have access to 3rd/4th years is during an interview, at least train them to answer your questions. He answered several of my questions, went off on a tangent, then abruptly ended the interview (even though I still had some questions and there was still 15 minutes left). Well, at least I know I won't be in Milwaukee next year.

Tufts was great. There was a lot of downtime, but it was filled with current students coming to talk with the applicants. I had some great conversations, and everyone I talked to was very enthusiastic about the area and school. The only downside is the cost (both tuition and cost of living are extremely high). If I'm lucky enough to get into Tufts, I'm fairly certain I'll apply for the 4 year MD/MPH program, although I don't think I did too well during my interviews. Supposedly the committee is meeting 12/16, so hopefully I'll hear some good news before Christmas.

UTMB also sent me a rejection letter, just to make it official.

12/15/06
Another week gone by with no news from TX schools. I'm starting to get resigned to the fact that I'll be spending some more time away from TX (and more money for tuition).

12/18/06
I got accepted to MCW. I'm probably going to withdraw in another week or two unless something drastically changes. Sometimes you just have to go with your gut feeling, and I didn't get good vibes while I was there.

12/21/06
Unofficially, I've heard all interview invites to Houston and SW were sent out. Looks like I'll be shut out of TX. I guess the TX schools are fairly unforgiving about a low science GPA, plus the fact that I applied 'late' (since TX schools are done with interviews much sooner than most of the other schools). Don't get me wrong, I'm happy with what I've received so far, but it is a little disappointing. Come on need-based aid, boy do I need you.

1/1/07
Came back from TX to see that I am now under 'active consideration' at Tufts. Looks like I'll be waiting another few months to hear back. Also, still no word from Drexel (and it's been more than 6 weeks now). Hopefully I'll get some news at some point this week.

1/4/07
Called Loyola and GW to check on my application. I was told that a decision on my application at GW would be made by the end of February (end of interview invitations?) and Loyola told me to call back if I didn't hear anything in three weeks.

1/9/07
Just came back from my interview at Case Western. Probably one of the most laidback and stress-free atmospheres you could possibly ask for. EVERY student seemed happy there. The hospitals affiliated with Case are amazing and the clinical experiences offered can't be matched except from a few other select schools. There are lots of opportunities to conduct research (it is built into the curriculum) or explore other aspects of medicine such as public health, bioethics, etc. I like the fact that the school is really pushing to integrate public health and medicine into one curriculum. Hopefully I hear some good news in another couple of weeks.

I'm still not willing to rank my choice of schools simply because of external factors to the admissions process that might come into play. I've basically gone into all my interviews asking myself a single question: will I be happy here? I really think more people should be going in with the same rationale, rather than looking at the rankings in US News or other such factors that are really unimportant in the long run. It is mindboggling to me that people will focus on the name of a school rather than where you'll be happy or where you'll be in the best situation.

1/11/07
Rejection letter came in the mail for GW (dated 1/5/07). Not really too surprised given that they got so many applications this year.

1/18/07
I decided to withdraw from NYMC and SLU today. I really took a longer look at them and figured out I wouldn't go to either over EVMS or any other school I'm still waiting to hear from.

Also, I still have no word from Drexel. I would find it extremely funny if I hear back from Case before Drexel.

1/19/07
I got rejected from Wake Forest. I was about 50/50 on whether to withdraw or not yesterday, so I guess they made up my mind for me.

1/23/07
I finally got a response back from Drexel: 'You will be receiving a letter shortly.' This week is going to really be hard on my nerves. I'll hear from my last two schools at some point this week...

1/24/07
I found out I'm on hold at Case today. I thought not getting any interviews at any of the TX schools was disappointing, but this is definitely much worse.

1/25/07
I got in at Drexel, which lifts my spirits a bit. In another few weeks I will probably send update letters to Tufts and Case. At this point it looks like I probably won't get any other interview invitations, so it is just a matter of waiting on my last two post-interview holds.

2/1/07
All interview invitations have been sent out for Baylor. I'm counting that as a rejection and will add the official date later.

2/16/07
Patiently working on update letters to Case and Tufts. No other news to report, though I've heard Jefferson and Loyola are still sending out interview invitations. Right now, I'd probably turn down an interview at Loyola. Money is getting tight, especially since moving is going to get expensive.

2/19/07
An actually pertinent update about my application status (from VCU):
'The committee's file review indicates that they would like to consider your file further. We are now in the process of scheduling interviews and will continue to do so through February. Unfortunately, the committee indicates interest in far more candidates than we are able to interview. Since we are dependent on the Committee member's schedules for interview spaces, we are unable to predict when or if we will be able to schedule you this year. Please continue to check this site to learn when further action has been taken.'

On another note, I still haven't finished working on my update letters.

3/4/07
Update letter to Case was sent out on Friday. I'm really hoping it will help get me off this post-interview hold. Also, VCU has finished sending out interview invitations, so I'm counting that as a rejection for now unless I hear differently in the next few days.

3/5/07
Rejection letter in the mail from UT-Houston. I like how almost all the TX schools' rejection letters have the generic month on it: February 2007 or March 2007, rather than the day.

3/8/07
I decided to withdraw from Loyola and MCW today. It is getting to that point where I will have to make a decision, and today was one of those times to trim down the schools I still have pending. At least this way someone may get good news in another couple of days.

3/17/07
I sent out an update letter to Tufts earlier this week, and finished all the financial aid forms I needed to. Still no word from Tufts/Case, although I've heard both are done interviewing. I should hear something (waitlist, rejection, acceptance) in another few weeks.

3/31/07
Decided to withdraw from EVMS on Monday. Looks like it's Drexel for now. I finally got around to ranking my acceptances/post-interview holds, and I'm thinking right now it's:
1) Tufts
2) Drexel
3) Case

Come on Tufts, though I'm pretty sure I'll get a waitlist letter in a few days.

4/3/07
Waitlisted at Case. Sigh.

4/9/07
I've been a bit busy the last week with the lab, so I hadn't gotten around to finishing up some stuff. I withdrew from EVMS, sent out another update letter to Tufts, and applied for the MD/MPH dual degree at Tufts today. Hopefully I get some good news in the next month from Tufts.

5/19/07
Still waiting on a couple of waitlists. If I don't hear back from Tufts or Case by June 15, I'll start making plans to head to Drexel. The worst part is the waiting and not knowing...

8/3/07
Officially a member of the class of 2011 at Drexel
