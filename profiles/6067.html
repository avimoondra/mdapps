INTERVIEWS/ OPINIONS / MY SCHOOL RANK

5.(tie) EL PASO
I honestly think this is going to be a really really really great school in a few years. They seem to really have gone all out to create the best curriculum/research opportunities around. However, el paso as a city was kinda scary; I didn't want to be so far from home (8+hours); and my interviews were HORRIBLE!!!!

> Interview 1 first question: "If you had a million dollars to put toward malaria research or alzheimers research, which would you choose and why?" I figured this was one of those 'theres-no-right-or-wrong-answer,-just-explain'-type questions, and I'm ashamed to admit I don't know a lot about alzheimers, so I said malaria and was able to discuss at length the first hand experience I've had with malaria patients while overseas. Well, it turns out he was a full time alzheimers research scientist (eek!!!) and I proceeded to dig myself deeper and deeper into a hole over the remainder of the interview, while he argued that malaria had no social/economic impact in the US.

> Interview 2 first question: "Explain to me every non-A grade you've made." Fearing this was a trap to weed out grade-obsessive pre-med gunners, I made some general statements admitting that my gpa wasn't sparkly, but that I've taken many, many, many courses above and beyond the requirements because I was interested in them, despite knowing they could be detrimental to my gpa, and that I worked hard for my grades and wasn't ashamed of my performance, and blah blah blah. This was followed by an excruciatingly long pause and a furrowed brow look from my interviewer, until she finally says "Ok, let's try this again: you got a B in class XXX why?" I answer. "You got a B in class YYY, why?" I answer. "You got a C in class ZZZ, why?" I answer. This was repeated throughout my ENTIRE transcript. I have a 3.5 and over 160 credit hours so you do the math at how many classes that is. (it was more than brutal)


5.(tie) TCOM
I had a really great experience here: they REALLY go out of their way to wine/dine interviewees. I also really like the philosophy and ideology of the DO degree and believe there's a lot that the MD world can learn from them. However, I was really unimpressed with the facilities/campus... Additionally, I'm not interested in family practice/etc. and, therefore, was severely worried about vying for residency positions with the tragic stigma associated with DO's.


4. A&M
I'm an aggie already, so this could have been a really easy sell. My interviewers were warm and friendly, and dealt no surprises. But I was actually really disappointed by the campus's facilities (I interviewed at College Station, and then did a second look at temple right before the match). I know Scott&White is a fancy-shmancy hospital; but honestly, the vast majority of the stuff we'll need to be responsible for learning during 3rd/4th years is routine crap that you could get at nearly hospital. Not to mention that because of the option of away rotations that nearly every school has during the 4th year, the caliber of the affiliated hospital is much less important than a school's curriculum, location, testing schedule, community, "feel", etc., etc. Finally, I was an undergrad-aggie that was highly involved with my pre-med-ish major and did a 5th year in undergrad. As a consequence, I knew a LOT of people that were starting med school around the state in the year that I was applying. In general, the people that went to A&M's med school didn't seem to be as happy/content as a few of the other schools.

3. HOUSTON
Houston is a fantastic school with a lot of resources. It's shiney and sparkly, and I'm into that. It's well established and highly reputable. I know several students and they all seem to really, genuinely enjoy being students there. If I had no other choice but to go here, I'm sure I would have been happy. However, I was really really really turned off by Houston at the interview. I felt like the whole thing was a big dog and pony show. They took us to see emergency helicopter pad and to the gallery above an open heart surgery (I'm not going to lie--all very very very cool). However, I got the impression that they would do and say anything for us to rank them highest in the match so that they could have their pick of who they wanted. The places we visited aren't opportunities available for med students. Residents? Yes. MS 1-4? No. I kinda felt like I was at a used car dealership. As an example, the "gym" they have for medical students is waaaaaayyyyyy far away and is the most rinky-dink little place I've ever seen, and definitely the least impressive of all the schools. Now, the gym itself is, of course, no importance... but literally one hour later after seeing this place, I sat across from an MS2 at lunch who went on and on and on and on about how great the school was, and then proceeded to tell us that they had the best work out facilities of any texas medschool... at that point all I could do was smile, nod, and swallow back the barf. :D Plus, because it's a more competitive school, there's the whole big fish/little pond vs. little fish/big pond thing. Oh and the Houston housing market/traffic thing. :/

2. SAN ANTONIO
I really, really loved San Antonio... if I hadn't matched to Tech, I would have gone there as a pre-match. The staff at San Antonio is incredibly supportive, and I know quite a few current students and they all seem to be genuinely happy and well adjusted. I love the feel of the city of San Antonio, and the atmosphere of the school was likewise fantastic. I did have one reservation, though: their testing schedule. Every 5-6 weeks they have one week with a test in EVERY subject, yikes. It'd be like living in finals for two years.

1. TECH
I went to undergrad at A&M and have no ties or anything to Lubbock/WestTexas, so I'd say I'm probably one of the most objective applicants to judge Tech (in fact I'm probably a more harsh judge!). I'll be honest: a couple years ago I naively joked "if Tech's the only place I get in, then maybe that's a sign med school's not for me!!!" However, I visited the school and was COMPLETELY stunned at their EXTREMELY impressive facilities (they got all the shiney/sparkly feel and then some!). Their faculty really has its act together and has recently put together a very impressive curriculum that has their board scores rising every year, with last year having 100% pass, and even beating UTMB (the previous, er, "board score whore" haha!) Plus, although the drive getting there takes FOREVER, I discovered Lubbock really isn't as lame of a city to be is everyone jokes. Great college town amenities (college football, lots of libraries and off campus study places, great housing market, huge rec center, lots of restaurant choices, etc.) without the hassle of traffic, crime, etc. Plus, because there are so few residents and fellows, the 3rd and 4th year med students have an un-paralleled opportunity to have one-on-one contact with BOTH PATIENTS and their ATTENDINGS!!! I know it might seem strange that I ranked Tech first, but really fell in love with the school, and honestly believe they have the BEST program I considered (which includes all schools in texas except Baylor and UTSW) and will be the benchmark in years to come. They've had a history of being a Podunk school out in west texas, but this has been seriously changing in the past 5-10ish years. I'm really excited and I can't wait to be there!!!
