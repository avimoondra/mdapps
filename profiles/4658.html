Summary of Application Experience

Happy about my early acceptance into Maryland debating on whether or not to continue and look into other schools. I applied to schools that I thought I would get into and I kind of liked. Terms of schools, I shot fairly low for my scores as I wanted to make sure I was in a medical school next year.

I turned down BU and Temple interviews and for the majority of the schools I am waiting on schools to reject me or for me to reject them. I think my $190-140 per school entitles me to find out if I would have gotten an interview or not.

11/17/09 accepted the acceptance to SUNY. Thought the school was a bit run down but I'll give them another chance and visit again in a few months.

2/21/10 Withdrew my applications from Tufts, Tulane, Rush, and Med Coll of Wisconsin. Keeping my applications open for NYU and Columbia, but I'm not really sure if I'm going to even go to those interviews. Right now I'm leaning towards Maryland or Miami, and UCLA too if I get accepted. I should've looked into each schools finances and scholarship/lack there of before applying because it's a big factor in where I'm going. Miami gave me a pretty large scholarship, which makes it cheaper than even my state school.

4/12/10 So I finally decided on Miami. I really liked Maryland, but I think it'll be a good idea to experience a new city and culture. I dropped UCLA before I heard back b/c it'll still be about $50,000 more than Miami, over 4 years, and I couldn't justify that. UCLA is better ranked and more prestigious but that's all it has going for it. Anyway I'm excited about Miami and getting to practice my Spanish.

A word of advice for applicants. Don't waste your time applying to schools you don't think you'd go to. Every school applied to I would have been content to go to if that was the only school I got into. However, I knew I was probably going to get into Maryland and go there over a lot of these other schools, and in the end it was kind of a waste to fill out a lot of these applications. Now if you don't know whether you're going to get into medical school at all by all means apply to as many schools as you want. Check out the statistics on MCAT score and GPA and probability of getting in to medical school. It's on aamc.org
