Summary of Application Experience

Abbreviations: R = Received, S = Submitted, C = Complete, Int = Interview, WL = Waitlisted

05-04-2005: Submitted transcripts to AMCAS
06-06-2005: Waiting for April MCAT scores, and need to finish personal statement
06-15-2005: April MCAT scores released. I'm relatively happy but would have preferred 1 or 2 more points. My score turned out to be surprisingly well-balanced. I was never able to get double digits in all 3 sections on ANY of my practice tests (darn VR) but I finally did it on the real thing. Mildly disappointed with BS score, thrilled with VR score, satisfied with PS score.
07-01-2005: AMCAS submitted. I literally rewrote half my personal statement in the last 24 hours after spending nearly a month working on it. I am happier with the new version.
07-13-2005: AMCAS verified, only minor corrections.
08-10-2005: Withdrew from some schools pre-secondary today, reasons mentioned above.
08-15-2005: Georgetown interview invite. My first! w00p!
08-23-2005: NYMC interview invite.
08-25-2005: VCU put me on hold for a possible interview. After reviewing my application they don't want to reject me or interview me yet.
09-01-2005: MCW interview invite.
09-05-2005: Done with secondaries! Woohoo!
09-05-2005: Temple interview invite.
09-06-2005: Drexel interview invite.
09-09-2005: SLU interview invite. They offer regional interviews. Guess I can save some $$$.
09-12-2005: Einstein interview invite.
09-13-2005: Einstein is almost completely booked for Sept. and Oct. so I have to wait nearly 2 months for my interview.
09-15-2005: Temple Interview--my first. I thought it went relatively well. My interviewer said I was a 'strong applicant' a couple times while looking through my file... I hope he wasn't just being nice.
09-15-2005: University of Vermont interview invite.
09-20-2005: Georgetown interview--felt ok at the time but I think my responses were rather stupid in retrospect, not a good interview, expecting rejection or waitlist at best
09-20-2005: George Washington has put me on hold until February. Lame.
09-21-2005: Rejected by Pritzker, not terribly surprised. My first rejection... now it feels real.
09-28-2005: SLU interview (Regional)--first time where I didn't really feel nervous. I think this was a turning point--I feel more confident and in control at interviews now. My interviewer was very nice and seemed genuinely interested in what I had to say. Other applicants liked her as well.
09-28-2005: UCSD has put me on hold for possible interview.
09-28-2005: Rosalind Franklin has also notified me that I'm in their possible interview pool.
09-30-2005: Penn State has put me in their 'Continue to Review' category.
10-03-2005: Drexel interview--had both a student and faculty interview, each lasted about an hour. The first one went very well. The second one went ok. My faculty interviewer suggested that my clinical exposure may have been too light and last minute but that didn't seem to be a problem at my previous interviews (some even said the opposite)--go figure. Overall, the school was very impressive considering they have a mediocre reputation. They have a very technologically advanced learning environment. Both curriculums that they offer have appealing qualities.
10-06-2005: Rejected by Albany--rather unexpected, shows the unpredictability of this whole process. Maybe they disliked something on my secondary... probably that silly question about 'what experience you would most like to talk about in an interview'
10-08-2005: Rosalind Franklin interview invite.
10-10-2005: NYMC interview--stayed with a student host, interview seemed to go well (with nice family practice doctor), beautiful campus, very convenient housing, best gross anatomy lab I've seen (and not in a basement).
10-10-2005: VCU interview invite--after being put on hold 6 weeks ago.
10-17-2005: Temple ACCEPTANCE! w00p! I'm gonna be a doctor! Now some pressure is off. Paid $100 deposit thingy.
10-22-2005: MCW interview--impressive school, great facilities, nice students, had two 30 minute interviews (with retired faculty member & 3rd year student), love the free t-shirt... of course, I might feel differently if I'm rejected
10-25-2005: UVM interview--nice looking campus, neat new curriculum. I think I made one stupid comment during an otherwise great interview so now it's eating away at me... ugh. I have no idea what was going through my stupid head. Beautiful area with lake and mountain views. It actually started snowing lightly an hour before my plane took off.
10-27-2005: UCSF pre-secondary rejection--I'm shocked! =]
11-01-2005: SLU acceptance--notification via email, why can't more schools join the modern era? $100 deposit to hold acceptance
11-01-2005: Withdrew from Tulane and Creighton
11-02-2005: Georgetown rejection--not surprised, my worst interview, didn't click with interviewer, was asked vaguely-phrased ethics/health policy questions, difficult to even understand what I was being asked sometimes
11-02-2005: Drexel waitlist--disappointed but not shocked, my faculty interviewer had a somewhat accusatory tone ('Are you sure you've had enough clinical exposure?' etc.) which made it hard for me to have an opportunity to express myself, I thought I handled myself relatively well (probably the reason it wasn't a flat out rejection) but felt like I was defending myself the whole time instead of selling myself.
11-03-2005: Complete at BU--THREE MONTHS after submitting secondary. Is that not insane? They finally responded to an email I sent in September inquiring about my status.
11-04-2005: MCW Acceptance--I really like this school despite the fact that it's in Wisconsin. Standard $100 deposit to hold acceptance.
11-07-2005: Einstein Interview--enjoyable experience, most highly ranked school I've interviewed at thus far, the housing complex may not be pretty but the rooms are gigantic
11-08-2005: Rosalind Franklin Interview--had two 30 minute interviews, mostly conversational but some questions about health policy, the future of medicine, ethical concerns about genetic testing
11-11-2005: Withdrew from Loyola and Penn State
11-14-2005: Withdrew from VCU (cancelled my interview)--struggled with this one.
11-15-2005: UCI has put me on pre-interview hold--California, why hast thou forsaken me?
11-16-2005: Dartmouth rejection--not surprised, competitive school and extremely small class size
11-17-2005: Rochester rejection--bleh.
12-01-2005: University of Maryland has put me on pre-interview hold
12-07-2005: USC interview invite--finally a Cali school acknowledges my existence... but I have to wait 2 months for earliest available interview date
12-19-2005: Waitlisted at UVM--put on list of 'qualified/alternate candidates.' I've heard that UVM initially waitlists tons of out-of-state applicants due to their small class size. They say to call back in May to check my status.
12-22-2005: Waitlisted at NYMC--I knew that this (or a rejection) was coming for a couple days since people with acceptances were informed by email on Monday, December 19. Based on what I know of previous years they have significant waitlist movement (more than UVM) so I guess I have a decent chance of getting an acceptance later on.
12-31-2005: Rosalind Franklin Acceptance--letter dated December 13.
01-01-2006: Declined RFU Acceptance--I was actually planning to send a withdrawal letter in a couple days anyway.
01-03-2006: BU Rejection
01-06-2006: Submitted extensive update letters to the 9 schools which have not yet offered me interviews detailing my research experience, hospital volunteer job and leadership responsibilities in past 5 months. I'm going to hold off on updating the schools I'm waitlisted at for the time being.
01-17-2006: Einstein rejection--didn't help that I was sick with a cold during that trip.
01-19-2006: Wake Forest interview invite--I guess update letters do help.
01-21-2006: Submitted additional update letters to the 8 schools which have not yet offered me interviews mentioning that I recently began a new medically-related part-time job--too soon since my last letters? I don't care.
02-08-2006: UCSD rejection
02-14-2006: USC Interview--had two closed-file (blind) interviews with one student (MS4) and one faculty member. The student interview was very informal, relaxed and short (~15 minutes). It almost makes me worried that I didn't deliberately lengthen it out but all the other interviewees mentioned similar experiences. (We spent like 5 minutes talking about computer/video games). My faculty interview was probably the single best interview I've had thus far. We talked for an hour and she flat out told me at the end of the interview that she wanted me to get in and that she would give me the highest interview scores she could. But alas, she is not on the admissions committee, so my fate ultimately lies in the hands of others. I submitted an update letter to the admissions office to include in my file.
02-17-2006: GW informs me that their interview season is officially closed but there's a 'very slim possibility' that I may still be offered an interview because they have placed me in a 'small pool of applicants' whose credentials they were impressed by. Mmm k.
