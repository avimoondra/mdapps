Summary of Application Experience

Although I'd thought about being a doctor since my early 20s, I didn't get around to seriously about applying until I was 32. I was living and working in Thailand at the time, so I packed up and moved back to the US and started taking premed prereqs at UConn. I applied to 17 schools and was surprised to get 8 interviews. With my crap PS score I wasn't sure I'd get any, but in retrospect my application interested adcomms because of my unusual background and work experience. I'm older than most applicants, I'd worked for a couple of non-profit organizations in Thailand--one in rural development and the other a US-based medical charity (Operation Smile), and I'd worked with Thai environmental and social justice NGOs shooting video and recording music CDs. My GPA at UConn was good, and my letters of recommendation must have helped as well. One of my recommeders was a professor from my undergrad days at Catholic University, and since we'd kept in touch and stayed close she was able to write about me as a person in the 18 years she'd known me. My personal statement was heavy on how my background and experiences motivated me to become a doctor, since I figured that would be the biggest question for adcomms given my age, non-medical background and relative lack of medically-related EC's. At my interviews I was asked about my motivation and commitment to medicine, but I think my age and background came off as positives and helped me get accepted at four schools (so far!).
