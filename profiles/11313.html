Summary of Application Experience

Arrg! After submitting my app to AMCAS, my last transcript arrived on June 27... As of July 13 I'm still waiting to be verified...

Woohoo! Verified today, July 17.

First interview invite! That was so fast, just 4 days post-verification. This will be fun... I've never been to Michigan before!

9/2 Got an interview and a hold this week. At least the one softens the other, but I can't always count on bad news coming along with good news.

HOLY SHNIKES! Just as clicked update, after writing the above statement, I received an email from Tufts!! Yay! GW can go screw themselves. *sticking tongue out at GWU*

HOLY HONEY BAKED HAMS! 2 invites in 5 minutes!!! Yay UCLA, and yay California lovin'!

9/13 Emory wants to interview me! I don't have to pay for a plane or a hotel, but I do have to miss a day of work. I was feeling down yesterday, after UCSD put me on hold, but I'm feeling more confident now with 5 interviews on my schedule.

9/22 Holy crap. 3 invites this week: USC on tuesday, UPenn on thursday, UC Irvine on friday. I'm ecstatic about these schools, considering that anything Californian and UPenn are my favorites, but... with 8 interviews (5 of which want to be in October) I'm wondering how I'm going to fund this.

10/11 I woke up this morning to find a Yale invite in my inbox. This interview season is going so much better than I expected... 10 interviews, 2 holds, and waiting to hear back from 8 schools. I feel confident enough to cancel an interview and save on plane fare. I think I'll cancel Tufts, since I don't want to cancel any California or New York interviews.

10/19 UCSF interview!! I nearly fainted with joy when I saw this. I was honestly expecting a rejection letter in the mail. Still, I most likely will not get in... but I am so honored to have been invited for an interview. Yay! I get to visit my Berkeley friends and hang out in my favorite city in the US: San Francisco!!

11/07 Well, I'm going to be a doctor! My boyfriend called with the news about a fat envelope from Emory as I was in my suit and going to USC for another interview. It really took the pressure off me, and I think I was more relaxed for that interview than for any other. So, if my dream school doesn't work out, I have a great school to fall back on. Yay!!!

11/16 I got into Mount Sinai!! Yay for NYC! To be honest, one of their biggest selling points for me is that they are so close to central park, which would be a wonderful place for long runs. Time to withdraw from Tufts and save some air fare!

12/6 I just found out that UC Irvine placed me on the alternate list. They didn't even bother to email me... I had to find out by checking the status page. Somehow, I don't feel too down about it. I think my lack of enthusiasm shone through in my interview. I had Irvine the day after USC, so Irvine was a bit of a letdown in comparison. It worked out though, because yesterday I was accepted to USC. So, I'm happy, and hopefully, someone who really liked Irvine got the acceptance that I didn't so s/he can be happy too.


Davis ended up giving me the Regent's scholarship! I also applied for, and got into, their rural-prime program, which gives me an additional 10K/year stipend. Plus, they gave me an institutional grant and subsidized loans. All in all, they gave me BY FAR the best aid package. I should be graduating with just under $100K in debt, or close to 1/3 the debt that Emory would leave me with! Plus, I love northern California. Sacramento isn't quite home, but it's close enough, and I think I'll end up liking it.
