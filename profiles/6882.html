Summary of Application Experience

In one word: Grueling!--Props to ALL applicants for making it through the process. Anyone making it this far has my greatest respect.

I tackled the challenges facing the typical non-traditional: catching up on all the missed pre-reqs, digging up old professor contacts for letters of rec(one was even dead already!), chasing down multiple transcripts (I had 4 to get, and some were 'archived' and not in the computer system), no pre-med advisor access, no committee letter, no peer-support groups...thank goodness for student doctor forums! Altogether a endeavor that seemed to beg the question: 'are you *sure* you want to do this??' My answer is you only live once and I want this bad enough to know I will certainly regret NOT doing this--and I'm running out of chances. So I'm giving it my best shot.

I've gotten alot of rejections, in spite of having what I think is a solid academic and MCAT record. I would guess it is my lack of clinically-based experience that is holding my candidacy back the most. Most heard comment in interviews: 'You are an interesting guy.' But it definitely takes alot more than just being 'interesting' to convince schools that I'd be good for their medical school.

I look forward to meeting some other non-trads like me on the interview circuit. So far, we haven't crossed paths yet...
