Summary of Application Experience

This was my second time applying, and the first time I received any interviews. I learned my lesson after the first year and applied early, arranged my letters of rec in as quickly as I could, and strengthened my list of extracurriculars before applying again.

For those of you who want to strengthen your application, I suggest volunteering outside of the usual hospital or clinical setting (if possible), where you interact with people in crises or stressful situations. Medical schools are constantly looking for applicants who will bring something unique to their university. I know I received many more questions about my work as a sexual assault victim advocate and as a Doula (hospital-based, but far more hands-on than a usual hospital volunteer position), or even about my tendency to write books in my free time, than about any other aspect of my application.

If you're looking to strengthen your letters of recommendation, it was really helpful for me to work as a TA in my undergrad facility. I coulld work more closely with professors (and get paid for it!), and they were able to provide a more detailed letter than if I had been a simple student. (Unfortunately, some schools require that the letter of rec be written by somebody you had as a professor in a class where you received a letter grade, so it's a good option to try and TA for a professor you already had). I also took some ancillary classes at a local community college (UCSD doesn't offer anatomy for some reason...) and was able to create a great rapport with one of my professors, an ex-nurse. Although her letter of rec couldn't be used for the universities that had very specific LOR requirements, it definitely strengthened the ones that were a bit more lax.

I also learned from my first attempt at applying to check out a school's stats for in-state vs. out-of-state acceptances. It's a bit disheartening (plus a waste of very-needed funds) to receive rejections pre-secondary from schools who only admit state residents. Since wording on a school's information page can be fuzzy, it's good to look at any actual statistics for IS vs OOS.

Above all, the application process is largely waiting. You may be interviewing at one school while waiting for a secondary from another, or accepted for some while waitlisted for interviews at others. Don't be discouraged if you're waitlisted - sometimes it takes only a week or two to get off the list, while others may take months. (My first choice - UVM - had me waitlisted for 6 months before they sent me their acceptance. MCW had me waitlisted for an interview for only 2 weeks, and I was accepted outright a week after the interview!)

So, no matter where you are in the application process, good luck!
