Summary of Application Experience

I will use this site to document my process of applying to med school, esp. since this is my second time applying. hopefully it may benefit others...as well as myself.

6/11/07: It has been exactly one year and a day since I submitted AMCAS. It's crazy to hear people have just submitted AMCAS for this coming cycle. I'm glad I am not among them this time. I am finally getting some rejections so that I can close out my mdapps. As for the UCLA waiting list, looks like things have slowed down quite a bit and according to some folks on SDN, no anticipated movement in the coming weeks. Looks rather bleak I'd say. However, I'M GOING TO MED SCHOOL BABY!! It's a great feeling to have, and to not only have one, but three acceptances, really makes my efforts these past two years worth it. I could of easily given up after being unsuccessful during the 2005 application process, but instead I improved my application, gained more experience, and here I am, c/o 2011 medical student. Just two more months before I offically start school at University of Wisconsin-Madison. Time to make the best of the time I have left in the Bay Area.

5/7/07: Got confirmations from Rush and Jefferson. Should expect a refund of my deposits within 4-6 weeks. Time to get started on that checklist that needs to be completed before I can start school. Still waiting from a couple of schools, not sure what's the hold up. Just another week until the notorious May 15th deadline. Let's see those waitlists be put to use!

5/1/07: Withdrew from schools today (Rush, Jefferson acceptances and Drexel, VCU waitlists to be exact). Hopefully it give some other folks a chance to garner an acceptance! Unless UCLA gives me a miracle, I'm going to Wisconsin baby!

3/25/07: Back to work. I had myself a nice pseudo-spring break (cuz I'm actually working and not in school). Went to the happiest place on earth (Disneyland), and was very tempted to stop by UCLA's admissions office, but found out I was put on the unranked waitlist this past week. Supposedly, it's the better waitlist to be on, but I'm gonna call monday and find out what details I can obtain from the admissions office. Got Waitlisted at Drexel and VCU. Got rejected from University of Toledo and Tufts. Even though I was bummed out about the waitlist from UCLA, Jefferson gave me a very nice letter. They're gonna award me a scholarship that pays half my tuition for 4 years!!!! How awesome is that! Makes my decision sooooooo much tougher now. I'm gonna wait for the entire financial aid packages to see what I can get. Anyhow, just about a month left for any last min interviews, and gonna figure out if I'm gonna attend any second-look stuff. This draining process is almost over for me....

02/14/07: Happy Valentine's Day, and congrats to me! I am accepted to Jefferson Medical College!! Sweet! The Dean gave me a wonderful wake up call by offering me a place in the class. How awesome is that! I really enjoyed my stay in Philly, it's a great city. I'm really tempted to go to the east coast, there's so much to do and so much to see. Good thing I have time before May 15th to decide. We'll see what happens =)

02/08/07: Interview at Drexel. On the outskirts of central Philly, so I had to take a little trip from center city. Very modern school, lots of technology infused throughout the building. Crazy thing happenned, I ran into my health worker/floor mate from the dorms. He's a second year there, and still looks the same. 6-8 weeks for a decision, man..such a long wait, but not as long as UCLA has made everyone wait. After my interview day, went off to NYC and had a blast! Too bad I lost my camera on the last night I was there. I had some great pictures. Maybe a good hearted person will turn it in? Anyhow, I'm done with interviews for now. Hopefully, more will come in the last few months of the season.

02/07/07: Interview at Jefferson. Stayed with a student host, by far one of the best hosts I've had this application season. I had a great time there. Even though I had an interview at Drexel the next day, he offered to host me an extra night to save me some hotel money. Coming to the East Coast really opened my eyes about what to consider in choosing a medical school. You have to weigh all the factors.

01/19/07: I got into Rush Medical College!!! Checked my mail on a friday, and to my surprise, there was actually something there for me. Now I have a choice on where to go, sweet! Where to go.....very tough question. I have until May to decide, so still have some time.

01/12/07: I got an invite to interview at Jefferson! Finally makes the delaying of Drexel interview worth it! Gonna hit Jefferson on 2/7 and Drexel on 2/8. I've delayed Drexel's interview since November, hope they don't notice.

12/15/06: Found out I got rejected from Case Western and SUNY downstate. It's a lot easier to accept the rejections when you're on vacation. I should always be snowboarding when I get my rejections.

12/13/06: Things have definitely slowed down a bit. Most likely due to the upcoming holidays, but I hope more great things will happen in 2007. Interviewed at Rush last Thursday 12/07, and boy, was it cold! Stayed with a student host for free, so I treated him to his favorite deep dish pizza. I could barely finish two slices while my host barfed down half the pizza. I flew in from Midway, and took the L Orange line train for two bucks and my host was nice enough to pick me up from the train station. Chicago is definitely a pretty city at night. They go out of their way to decorate all trees downtown with Christmas lights. My interview day went pretty well. People (students and faculty) at Rush are extremely friendly. The Admissions staff walked all 16 of us interviewees to our interviewers' rooms, which I thought was very nice, helping us avoid the mass confusion of finding our own way. On part of our tour, we got to see their anatomy labs full of second years volunteering their time to set up cadaver practicals for the first years to study. Talk about supportive! They even pay for tutors to help you out with classes! I had a good time at Rush, even though I believe I caught a cold due to the freezing weather. Should hear back in 3-6 weeks (due to holidays), and will be receiving a small business size envelope regardless of decision. Hope I get in! Now to enjoy the holidays....sweet!

11/27/06: Got an interview invite from Drexel. Scheduled it for Jan. 18, it'd be my first time in Philly. I can't believe I've been given 7 interviews and it's not even December yet. The last time I applied, I was only given 3 interviews total. Good things do come to those who wait! Edit: I got another interview, it's with Meharry. That makes it 8 total!

11/20/06: Started the day off by calling Creighton, to find out that I was given an 'Alternate Status', and was really bummed. Then when I checked the mail to check for the actual letter from Creighton, I also got a thin envelope from Wisconsin. BEST DAY OF MY LIFE!! I GOT MY FIRST ACCEPTANCE!! I'M GOING TO BE A DOCTOR!! I'M GOING TO MED SCHOOL!! TIME TO CELEBRATE! TWO TIMES THE CHARM!!

11/16/06: Interview at UCLA. Flew in the morning from Oakland to LAX, landing around 9:30 AM. Got to the bus station around 10:15AM. Took the Culver City Bus to UCLA Med school around 11:15 AM for only 75 cents (bus ride was a little over an hour)! Since I got dropped of at an earlier stop, I was on the wrong side of the medical center and had to find my way, good thing my interview day wasn't supposed to start until 11:45AM. UCLA has two sessions for interviews, a groups of 7-8 that start at 8:15 AM, and 11:45AM. There's an informal tour given by first-years, but only of the medical school and not the hospital. Some people had two interviews, one with a student, and one with faculty, I had only one with a faculty. My interview wasn't until 3:30pm, which meant a lot of lag time between interview and the free lunch (up to 10 bucks from a cafe). Luckily, I got to sit in during one of the first year's classes called a conference, but it felt like more of a problem set session and discussion. It was cool to sit in and listen in on renal system related stuff. My interviewer was extremely late, almost an hour! Good thing I didn't book an earlier flight. Overall, students seem very relaxed and happy. It's southern california weather. Good program. Will have to wait till after Jan 15 to start hearing back, which sucks. Hopefully, I'll get in!

11/7/06: More 'holds.' Placed on hold at Albany and Rosalind Franklin. Submitted secondary to MC of Ohio. Still waiting to hear from Creighton. Preparing for UCLA interview.

10/27/06: I had my interview at Wisconsin today, went well. Also received an interview invite from Rush

10/17/06: Decided to apply to 5 more schools: Case Western, Ohio State, Tulane, Medical College of Ohio, U of Vermont

10/10/06: Finished with secondaries (just did UWashington) and now just waiting to receive UCSD and Michigan State secondaries, and of course more interviews. Interview invites have slowed down, but I hope more come in during the next couple of weeks. I'm pretty anxious about 10/16/06 since I hope to hear back from 2-3 schools. I'm gonna keep my fingers crossed and pray really hard.

10/06/06: Had my Creighton interview today. Omaha, NE. Nice people, small school, kind of expensive, but I'll take it if they give it to me. Rochester is still my top choice so far. I have my U of Wisc interview in 3 weeks. We'll see what happens.
