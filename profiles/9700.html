Summary of Application Experience

Legend:
SR: secondary received, SC: secondary completed, C: complete, II: interview invite, I: interview, A: acceptance, W: waitlist, WD: withdrew, R: rejection.

05/01: I'm an international student, which complicates things, but so it goes. I almost applied in the 2008 cycle, then decided against it so I could focus on MCAT prep exclusively.

05/25: MCAT done! Will update with scores at the end of June.

06/28: Scores in: 36Q. Right around my practice test average, so I'm happy.

01/27: Need to meet all my letter writers and ask them!

07/11: Primary submitted! Better lateish than never.

08/05: AMCAS verified!

08/06: Composite letter writer turns letter in to Career Center. Should be able to send to schools by August 20 latest. Plan to kill secondaries until that time.

08/21:
Letters sent to all schools except Duke and Vandy

10/12:
A couple interviews down. Count so far: Interviews: 5, Rejections: 1, Nothing: 8. The wait is excruciating.

Loved Dartmouth. Seems like a small, friendly place. Hanover is beautiful. Was hesitant about diversity of patient population, but off-site rotations in other states (Hi Cali, Hi FL!) have changed that. Administration seems uber supportive. Think would be happy here. Interviews went well for the most part; I think I connect better with female interviewers in general. Was part of second interview group, so should hear soon after Oct. 15!

Had a great experience at Case too. Admissions staff is amazingly nice, not to mention amazingly attractive. Didn't feel as though connected very well with interviewers; faculty interviewer was kind of quiet (which I am NOT) and student interviewer was snapping questions at me briskly - I think my answers were too wordy for him. Oh well. The facilities are beautiful, and I LOVE LOVE LOVE the curriculum and great clinical exposure. Have doubts about how well they prepare students for boards though. Should hear from them by the end of October (22 or 23); fingers crossed.

Vandy on Monday - excited!

10/15: LOVED Vanderbilt. Great education, super smart students, super nice administrators, great quality of life. Administrators really care about students' welfare - they really try to make sure you hold on to the things that make you 'you' through med school. Love the emphasis program - would probably do it in global health. AND love the opportunities to get other degrees. Vandy matches really well and they score ridiculously on the boards- 243 average! Interview was fabulous - most comfortable so far I'd say. At the end, the prof. even said 'I hope to see you here next year' - not that that guarantees an acceptance by any means. Not sure about Nashville though - I don't know if it's just my bias against the southern accent, or something else.... some administrators definitely made me feel uncomfortable about my foreignness. That's the only damper; besides that I'm in love. Should hear back in 6-8 weeks.

10/17: Dartmouth Medical School accepted me!! I'M GOING TO BE A DOCTOR!!!

10/21: Count so far: Interviews: 6, Rejections: 1, Nada: 7. (and one acceptance).

10/22: Case! wheeeee!!

10/23: Pritzker interview. Loved it - interviews were really relaxed and conversational. Love everything about this school; the size, the location, the offerings, the people, the housing, the genuinely kind professors. Can totally see myself being happy here forever! Totally my top choice, and I don't see that changing....!
If you go to interview there, don't stay at the International House -- it took me an hour to check in and a half hour to get my internet access pin.

11/07: Columbia - omg, I'm in love. The tour guides were so enthusiastic; students seemed so happy, NYC is the bomb, interviewer was selling the school to me instead of asking me questions. maybe she does that to all her interviewees, who knows. Would be thrilled to go here!

11/14: Wash U. - amazing opportunities, facilities and faculty members - def most awk waiting room though. not really an option for me as they have an escrow account for internationals - ie. 250K upfront...um, no thanks.

12/05: Mt. Sinai: amazing Upper East side location, students seem happy. One interviewer seemed impressed; the other, not too much. Research integrated into curriculum. Decent dorms - unbeatable price for upper east side. Should hear by Christmas at the soonest. Not too much of an option for me because don't offer internationals any financial aid - not escrow though.

12/10: Emory: Very enthusiastic students and admissions staff. Don't know whether it was just my host, but students seem v. stressed! Innovative curriculum (basic sciences abbreviated to 18 months); also has global health institute and CDC. Grady hospital is interesting; seems to offer great clinical experience. Students seem to love it here. Lecture intensive; core competencies based curriculum. I like Atlanta. Should hear by Christmas, earliest.

12/11: Duke: Love love love this school's pathbreaking curriculum. It's like taking a fifth year w/o ever actually having to. Dean Armstrong's spiel was a near-religious experience for me. Students seem nice and awesome outside of class as well. Students bond intensely over first year, but are then separated for second year (clerkships), third year (research, additional degree etc.) and fourth year (electives all over the place). AMAZING global health opportunities with the amount of funding that is being poured into global health institute and M.Sc. in global health. Interviews were weird. Most of mine so far have been conversations, but these were very traditional question-and-answer style. I couldn't really get a read on what the interviewers thought of me; they were v. professional, but seemed to simply be going through the motions. Will hear in March.

1/15: Harvard: Gorgeous campus; gorgeous facilities; friendly, amazing group of students. Student interviewer was amazing and v. complimentary about my application - optimistic about my chances. Faculty interviewer was brusque and condescending though; I don't think I dazzled him v. much. I'm putting my chances for post-interview acceptance at about 25%, so I'm not holding my breath. All I need is urbanity + financial aid; and I'm perfectly happy.

1/27: Northwestern: Really enjoyed the interview day. Expensive though. Faculty interview was relaxed and conversational. Panel interview was rough because I had the most obnoxious kid from our group on my panel - note to future interviewees: it is *not* a good idea to posture yourself and dominate the panel. You will come across less as a leader and more as a bully.

2/23: Yale: OMG I'm in love. Love the philosophy, love the facilities, love the yummy food carts, love the people I met. Would be so happy to go here. Just my luck that I had two psychiatrist interviewers. The interviews went well though; I think I presented myself accurately. It's up to them to decide whether or not I'm a good fit! AND HEY INTERVIEWS ARE OVER! wheee.

Count so far: Interviews: 12, Acceptances: 5, Waitlists: 1, WAITING ON: 6.

03/07: OMG, OMG, OMG. Columbia accepted me. In disbelief. I was really, truly, not expecting to get in. Have always wanted to live a few years in New York City... this is my chance! Two days later, still so shocked.

03/18: Wow. HMS. Best birthday present ever. Who woulda thunk it. Must not turn into awful Haahvahd drone thinking everyone else is less worthy. I have tough decisions to make.

03/20: Ummm, Yale. Thanks. How'd I get so lucky with all these options?!

05/12 Update: What a ride it's been. I'm officially going to HMS since they offered me a great aid package. Good luck to everyone, write if you have questions!
