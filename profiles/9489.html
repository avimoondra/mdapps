Summary of Application Experience

July 2006: So I am applying to a lot of schools primarily because I have little choice--with such spectacular numbers I have to play the field. On the positive front, I do have a 3.97 gpa in two years of post-bacc undergrad, and 4.0 gpa in the Boston University MAMS (SMP) program. So I have that going for me.... After so many years of working my butt off, I am just hoping for the best.

I'm putting this up because I think it could serve as motivation and support (or perhaps a cautionary tale) for other low gpa applicants.

Mostly applying to mid to low tier schools, with only a few upper tier schools added for very specific reasons: either they have been known to consider SMP applicants; have personal fondness for the school (Emory); is in a city I love (Northwestern, U of Chicago); or have been cooerced by friends (Cornell?).


9/27: Up to 30 secondaries completed. 8 more to go. Also, complete at UF. My first school acknowledgement about becoming complete.

9/28: Complete at UMDNJ-RWJ.

10/04: Complete at FSU, Miami, Creighton, Michigan, SUNY-Buffalo, Weil-Cornell, NYU.

10/06: Missed the deadline to submit the Northwestern secondary. I really wanted to go there, but I just ran out of time with exams crushing down on me this week. Oh well, I probably had no shot anyway--and this is a relative statement, since I'm a fringe candidate anywhere, at best, but practically pluto-esque at places like Northwestern, Emory, Michigan, Pitt...

10/09: Completed 37 secondaries. I'll probably complete 2-3 more, before giving up. I'm already over 5k in fees, just for the primary and secondary fees. Complete at GWU, Emory, Tufts, Saint Louis, Einstein, Cincinnati, Pittsburgh.


10/12: Complete at Rush

10/13: Total secondaries completed up to 41 after Wayne State, SUNY Downstate, Vermont, Loyola. Still have Case and Virgnia left. Complete at Pritzker.

10/27: First rejection. University of Minnesota. Not that I wanted to go there. At least they had the courtesy to reject me pre-secondary, to save me from having to fork over another $75. Gotta respect them for that. Also, I've turned into a complete lazy ass.


11/1: Received phone interview invite from SUNY Upstate! And I almost blew it. I received a call from a telemarketer two minutes before the phone invite from SUNY, so when I heard the phone ring again, I was grumpy...I muttered something like 'Yeah...' and the my cell phone lost the signal! Luckily, the very nice lady called me back. I may not have made the best first impression, but after all this time, it feels really good to get an invite! I'm excited!
