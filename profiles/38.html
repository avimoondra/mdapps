Brief Profile:
Summing up:

Attended community college after high school, doing horribly. Multiple F's, withdrawals, repeats, etc. Was there off and on from 2002 to 2007, when I graduated with two A.S. degrees and a 2.1ish GPA (with a repeat grade replacement policy at that school.) in 2014, came back to community college and did an academic renewal program, where, after a break of 5 years, and after earning at least a 3.5 in a semester, all F and D grades are removed from cumulative GPA calculation. Applied and was accepted to four year university, where I'm now finishing my third semester and have a 3.9, with 4.0's the last two. On track to graduate next year with a B.S. in biology.

Worked as a tech in an ER for 4 years, scribe for a year, paramedic for a year, and paramedic instructor for 6 years. Volunteered as EMT for a year, worked in free clinic off and on for 4 years, went on a week-long medical mission trip to the Dominican Republic, have been volunteer tutor and mentor for the past year. Ran my own photography business for 5 years.

As it stands, I have close to 200 credit hours, most from community college, making it incredibly difficult to get my GPA to anywhere near med school acceptance numbers (for MD and DO, even with replacement), so I'm hoping that my clinical experiences, recommendations, and essay help me, but am applying as broadly as I can possibly afford and seeing what happens.
