Summary of Application Experience

Before you read on, please let me know if you have any questions about my application cycle via Personal Message. I'm happy to help, if I can. I have tried to make this profile fairly transparent so that similar applicants might compare their school list to mine, ask questions, etc. Overall, I have three regrets for my application cycle:

(1) I applied slightly late. There is really no reason that my AMCAS application could not have been submitted the first day in June or that my letters of recommendation were not submitted earlier.

(2) School list. Should have applied to Stanford over UCSF. Shouldn't have applied to Brown nor to UCLA. I should have applied to Emory and Baylor. Should have applied to more schools in NYC including Sinai and possibly others (it would have been awesome to have more interviews - and possibly an acceptance - in NYC).

(3) I don't go to a very prestigious undergraduate school. Admittedly, there was nothing I could do about this three years into college when I was readying my application, but I am absolutely convinced that this hurt me in the long run. I could have gone to a more competitive undergraduate school, and I should have (I did not for financial reasons, but this was shortsighted because I could have made it work). I am convinced that one of the unasked questions in many of my interviews was "Why did you go to that school?" because, otherwise, my application is - objectively - very strong. If I were an AdComm at a top medical school, I'd be wondering why this kid chose to go to a school that is >100 in the USNWR -- how would he do if he were at Berkeley/Princeton/Hopkins?

It is difficult to be too upset with this application cycle, though, when I have been accepted to a school I love so much. In many ways, going to UVA med feels like going home. Again, happy to answer questions from anyone.
