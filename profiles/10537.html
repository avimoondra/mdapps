Summary of Application Experience

Just starting out on this process. Wish me luck!

TMDSAS and AMCAS processed. Filling out those secondaries and waiting this out.

LOR's have been received and all secondaries should be done before school starts. Now I will play the waiting game.

UT Houston interview was awesome. I really like this school, and I'm hoping for some good news February 1.

12/6 - So it's nearly the end of 2005 and I have one interview, one hold, and five rejections. I'm starting to worry. I really hope there's some good news for me on February 1. Being a Texan does not mean automatic interviews at all TX schools, so don't believe the hype.

12/9 - I emailed all of the schools I haven't heard from. The only bad news I got was from UTMB, UTSA, and UTSW - I will not be going to those schools. But some of the out of states were more encouraging, like SLU, GWU, and JMC. Hopefully, I'll get some good news now that they know that I'm still interested.

Happy New Year...you're rejected!

So far it looks like this year is not my year for med school. After considering the advice of kind SDNers, I'm applying to several post-bac programs to strengthen my app. I know I can do this; I just need to be given the chance to prove myself.

I have a suspicion about what my premed advisor put in my committee letter. She's sort of always had it in for me, and when she was going to write my letter she said 'let's just give it a shot and see what happens.' It wasn't that enthusiastic. I don't know if I'm grasping at straws, but for the post-bacs I am not using a committee letter. Instead I'm having the LOR writers send the letters to the individual schools. At least I can eliminate one possible issue.

I got into the Biology MA in Medical Sciences program at Loyola University Chicago!! I have somewhere to go next year!

I got into the MS in Biomedical Science Medical Masters program at Eastern Virginia Medical School! Now I get to choose!

I got into Georgetown's Special Masters Program! It was my top choice. If anyone has any info on this program, please let me know.

OK, I am now 90% sure that my committee letter had something to do with me not getting interviews. When I used my own letters for Georgetown's SMP, I got accepted less than 24 hours after being complete. There were people with better MCATs than me on waiting lists, getting rejected, or finding out much later. I also got into Loyola and EVMS 2 weeks after being complete. I will definitely use Interfolio for my med school apps this year. My advice: if you start hearing that your premed office isn't that great, or you have the slightest doubt your committe letter won't rock, check out the alternatives. It could save you a lot of heartache, money, and time.

I got into Columbia MS in Human Nutrition, and Boston University MA in Medical Science!
