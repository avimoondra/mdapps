Summary of Application Experience

First of all, I know by the list of schools, it looks like I am one very anal and absolutely insane pre-medical student. But to be honest, I am quite the opposite. I applied to soooo many schools because if you take a look at my GPA and MCAT, they are quite low for an MD/PhD applicant. In addition, I applied quite late in the cycle. Knowing that a career as a physician-scientist is what I want to devote my passion toward in life, I took my chances at every school possible-- 'if you don't apply, you can't get in' =). (I was fortunate to have the financial backing to do this, but truly would not recommend it because it can be quite taxing when the end result (in my case) is mostly rejections. *I must say the MSAR is quite accurate regarding averages and what not...=)


10/07/06 submitted AMCAS. Be prepared for secondaries to start rolling in. Many schools don't even worry about verification, and just want your info (and probably money too) assuming you will be verified soon enough.

11/08/06 AMCAS verified (nearly exactly a month, heh). Be prepared for the second huge wave of secondaries to start rolling in. My inbox nearly exploded... probably because I applied to so many schools =)

12/05/06 My first interview invite! UAMS (Arkansas)

01/05/07 Interview at UAMS. My first interview! Described in one word: Tough. I was grilled on my reasons for choosing MD/PhD, and my reasons for pursuing an MD. Although the interview was rough, at the end of the interview, my interviewers were positive and it seemed more like this rigorous interviewing was merely their style.

01/31/07 MCV/VCU MD/PhD Interview invite! (My first choice!)

02/07/07 Drexel Interview offer, MD only.

02/19/07 Placed on alternate list at UAMS. (One of the great things about interviewing as an out-of-state applicant to this program, you find out your status in a little over a month!)

02/26/07 Interview at MCV/VCU. My first true MD/PhD interview. I enjoyed myself so much. I am truly thrilled about their program, and cannot wait to hear from them in two weeks. VCU has always been my first choice in programs (mostly for my desire to pursue neurotrauma research). I was thrilled to receive a secondary, thrilled to receive an interview invitation, and now I am over-joyed to have had such a positive interview experience.

03/01/07 At this point, as I look at my application process, I am extremely happy since I was able to interview at my first-choice MD/PhD program. I think applying to so many schools could be construed as a waste of money, but being the type of person I am I just had to give other programs a shot and potentially open up 'options'. I definitely wondered how applying earlier in the cycle would have changed my stress level since I am interviewing so late in the season, but my incredible interview experience at MCV/VCU pretty much trumps any questions of 'what-if'. I am incredibly eager to hear back from VCU in two weeks (the nice thing about applying late in the cycle: minimal waiting time)!

03/05/07 SLU Interview offer, MD only. Sweet! I didn't think I was going to get anymore interview offers. (My second choice!)

03/06/07 LOInterest to VCU, also updated with my 'provisionally accepted' 3rd author pub in Neurobiology of Aging.

03/15/07 Just got my VCU/MCV MD/PhD acceptance!!! I couldn't be happier! My goal in joining this site was to give hope to those with not so great stats... I can now officially say that if you put in the effort, anything is possible (even in the med school app process)!

03/16/07 Withdrawing from all other active applications. Will be starting my lab rotations in late June @ VCU/MCV!! I am relieved that I will not have to repeat this process. You don't need great stats to get into med school or MD/PhD programs; great experiences, and the ability to write and speak about them can show adcoms your passion for medicine (and science).

06/2007 Despite my written withdrawal from UAMS, I was accepted off the waitlist sometime in late June/early July..!?
