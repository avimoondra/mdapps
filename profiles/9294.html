Summary of Application Experience

I felt like too much of the process is very superficial and a lot of it comes down to a crap shoot. I started the application proccess early, getting my AMCAS in by July 1st and all my secondaries which I intended to complete by August 1. This put me at a huge advantage as far as getting interview invitations came. For the schools which I didn't get an interview request, I think it just came down to whether I met their number cutoffs--apparently I did not. For the schools I interviewed at, I was mostly concerned with the cirriculum, whether students seemed personable and HAPPY, and if I could picture myself living wherever that school was situated. Being from NYC and having attended college in Ithaca, Upstate NY was definitely not for me for the next 4 years. That became apparent after I interviewed at Buffalo. I had my Downstate interview in early October and it definitely met my requirements in the aforementioned criteria. They treated the applicants so well. Within two weeks of my interview I had been accepted. This allowed me to pull out my application from all the upstate schools, as well as the PA schools which I was never really in love with from the start. I received my AECOM acceptance in mid January. Ultimately my decision came down to: $20,000 a year less at Downstate, Brooklyn over the Bronx, and proximity to home. Sure AECOM might have marginally better facilities and a higher USNews rank--but is that worth the extra 80-100G's of debt over 4 years. Perhaps for some. Overall, I'd say I'm I was a bit dissapointed in not even getting a chance to interview at some of my dream schools (particularly the institution in which I received a publication and spent countless hours doing research in a drosophila lab, fu weill--yeah I am a bit disgruntled). I felt I was a competitive applicant, but it was just not to be. In the end, I am satisfied with where I am and am looking forward to a quality and relatively inexpensive medical education and graduating with no debt. My ending pieces of advice are: be early, be persistant, and be resistant (don't get discouraged over rejections because much of the process is superficial and numbers based--if you put in the time and effort, you will end up somewhere that wants you and ultimately the level of education will be the same. It is up to you to make the most of it.
