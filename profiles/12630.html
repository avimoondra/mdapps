Summary of Application Experience

Applying to medical school is a very frustrating process. I applied to 16 allopathic schools, then applied to a few more several months later.. apply early, it is much easier to complete secondaries over summer break than when you're in school full-time and working.. also some schools prefer earlier applicants. I learned about the DO programs and found their philosophy fits with mine..more importantly, I knew I wanted to go into family medicine and DOs are just as competitive as MDs in that field. Also, the DO schools seem to be friendlier regarding the whole process. I just found out I got accepted to the Problem-Based Learning track at WVSOM..I'm excited!! Only 24 students get into that track each year. As for VCOM, I loved it and my interviews went great.. one interviewer told me my file was great, I understood the philosophy,and that this interview was basically a formality to show that I was not an ax-murdererer as he said. Well, I got waitlisted. They said it was because they didn't have my Fall 04 grades, however, I faxed them to them the next day and I still haven't heard anything 2 months later. I loved the school but was put off by how they told me I would be a great fit there and they hope I go there, and then they waitlist me.
VCU/MCV accepts only on certain dates. I am still hoping to be accepted or at least waitlisted there..it would be the only instate school for me. More importantly, MCV looks at everything..I believe my LOR, my own experiences, and extra curriculars helped me out..they were never considered about my low MCAT score.. I do believe that anyone who can fork out $2000 to Kaplan will naturally have a higher score. I chose to take the April 2004 MCAT and didn't repeat it in August, as I knew a guy who was waitlisted at many schools, but not accepted because his application was put in in October (after August MCAT scores came in).
Marshall University was amazing. They only accept 8 out of state students, so my chances are slim but I did interview and was put on the alternate list. However, I believe that part of my interview was biased as the interviewer said up front that the committee would not be able to look past my low PS score on the MCAT..then she proceeded to tell me how she had to repeat it herself and eventually take a prep course....I felt she was begrudging me and unwilling to look past her own tribulations with the application process.
The rest is left up to God's hands for me. I have been very stressed and anxious, but I am relieved to have one slot at an excellent school..WVSOM is the #5 school in the nation for family medicine/primary care out of all allopathic and osteopathic schools..which isn't a small feat and their graduates do very well.
Good luck and keep praying.
