Brief Profile:
Took June 18, 2009 MCAT!!!!!! Annoyed by the 9 in PS but forget it, my score is better than I even dared to hope for!!!

I got my undergrad degree in scientific illustration (art) and now I'm getting a Masters in Anatomy at a medical school. My grades aren't amazing, but I've taken a lot of relevant coursework at this point. (graduate-level histo, biochem, immuno, cell bio.) I've taken gross anatomy with the med school class and I will be a TA for it in the fall, so I hope that experience will give me a boost. I'm also doing a research project on a clinically relevant anatomy topic, I will be the first author and if all goes well it will get accepted for publication. after that, i have another project up my sleeve so if i have time to do that too, i might get two first-author papers. My graduate GPA is 3.29 . . . which kinda sucks but, I hope adcoms will realize it's pretty good considering I had very little science background when I started. Graduate science is hard!

i guess i have a pretty typical volunteering record (hospitals, tutoring) and some really good shadowing. I've probably done about 60-70 total hours of shadowing, with a few different physicians. I plan to keep doing more though so that number might be closer to 100 when I actually apply. I also worked in a hospital as an OR Aide for ~3 months, 40 hours a week, so that's about 400 hours of non-shadowing clinical experience.

Update: by the time I was interviewing, I was in the middle of TA-ing for anatomy, so it was a really good experience to talk about. I had also submitted my manuscript for publication at that point, so it was another great talking point. And it got accepted for publication the day before my Rush interview! So I was able to mention it then, which I think helped a lot.
