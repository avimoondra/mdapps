Summary of Application Experience

Update 5/6:
Got the rejection letter from UCLA. bastards.
Update 4/29:
Finally, I got final decision letter sent on the UCLA status page. Now I only have to wait another week to receive my actual paper letter with the decision inside.

Update 4/21:
It seems like UCLA did send a 4/15 final decision batch but it was miniscule and only acceptances and I wasnt part of it. This is freakin ridiculous. been over 5 months and i have yet to hear shit back from them. Also, it seems like I won't be getting a straight accept from them either. The dean has mentioned how they are now in a 'holding pattern' and won't be accepting anyone until waitlist season. So i guess my best hope now is to just get a waitlist from these guys and get evaluated again against all the waitlisters. Man, this suxs.

Update 4/5:
Got rejected from both Davis and Irvine pre-interview today. This process has been totally unpredictable. I'm getting dumped on by schools I thought I could consider semi-backups.
Everything now comes down to UCLA. The school I interviewed at first and still have yet to hear back from. Already sent a Letter of intent over two months ago and now an update letter like a week ago. According to several sources, they are going to be sending a big batch of final decision letters (acceptances, rejections, waitlists... the whole shebang) around the middle of April that will clear the picture for a lot of applicants who have been waiting for them for a long time. I should be part of that batch.

Update 3/18: Got favorably reviewed list from Einstein and email rejection from USC. Two schools which at the start I considered my backups. Well, at least for Einstein it looks the only reason i got put on this list is because their class is already full and once a few people drop they will accept me.
Update 2/23: Rejected from UMICH.
Update 2/19: Received rejection letter from NYU today. Here they come......

Update 2/14: Just received letter from Mt. Sinai telling me I've been put in their 'alternate list' and the chances of me eventually receiving acceptance are 'favorable.' I am very disappointed. I thought my sinai interview was my best so far in this cycle and all i got out of them was a waitlist and they reject very few if any people post-interview. so, so far my only post-interview decisions are two waitlists (ucsd, and mt sinai) from schools that dont even reject applicants post-interview. :( got me very worried on what i might be hearing back from ucla especially since i consider these two schools to be less competitive than ucla.

Update 2/3: got rejection from Stanford today. I want to burn the farm.

Update 1/21: Back from chilling in NYC (and it was freakin cold!!) and interviewing at Einstein and Mt. Sinai.
A little disappointed with Einstein. Location (the Bronx) wasnt the best. All the disadvantages of NYC like traffic, shitty weather, etc. without the advantages of NYC since it was in a residential area. Mt Sinai on the other hand was well above my expecations. very professional and all their structures were state of the art. Interviews at both schools went very well. very laid back and conversational. my second interview at mt sinai was especially strong and i believe my best interview of this cycle. at the end of the interview, my interviewer said, 'you should do well' :)
UCSD: placed in 'pool of acceptable applicants.' I guess that's a quasi-waitlist.
UCLA: still havent heard back from them yet post-interview. Stay tuned though i should be finding out soon from my top choice.
Stanford: Still alive here for another week even though they've sent out another wave a rejections.

Update 1/11: Rejected from Cornell. Didn't really care for them at all. Additionally, it looks like I have been rejected from Columbia, Yale, UPENN, and NYU. All those schools are winding down their interview seasons and are about to halt giving out interview invites. Still holding hope out for a possible Stanford interview though. On the possibly positive side, I should be hearing back from UCSD and UCLA next week.

Update 1/6:
Still havent heard anything from any additional schools. Finished interview at UCSD. The office secretaries and everyone seemed really nice, but I got a sense that the students weren't very happy there. It was like the first week of classes of the new quarter and the students looked like people do during finals week at ucla. haggard, tired, unshaven. not a pretty site. regardless, i should hear back from them right after Martin Luther King Day in Jan.

Update 12/22:
1st interview done at UCLA (my top choice so far)
3 interviews (UCSD, MSSM, Einstein set for january)
still waiting to hear anything from a bunch of schools

here are some schools which I thought I would have been reviewed favorably at but havent been:

UCI - i think the reason they put me on hold was because I kept on bothering one of their administrators over why I was still 'pending review' after 3 months. Stats-wise I should be getting an interview from these guys. my MCATs are way above their averages and a few people with similar numbers as me have already been accepted there.

UC Davis - complete early October. they do tend to place less emphasis on numbers and more on your written work in your application once you get post-secondary. That and my late completion date might be affecting me.

USC - my numbers are way above their averages. but the thing is in my application I talked about some financial difficulties my family has been going through and they might have thought that even if accepted I might not be able to afford to attend due to their ridiculous tuition. There's a reason why their called U$C.
