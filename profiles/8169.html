Summary of Application Experience

Yes, I know 38 schools is a lot. I just know too many people who have been burned by this process...

6/15- Took MCATS. Waiting for scores...(practice scores ranged from 29 11P 7V 11B to 33- 12P 10V 11B). Don't want to predict in fear of being jinxed. Darn that verbal...you'd think coming from a humanities major my verbal would be higher. Mostly averaging 9s in Verbal.

6/25- AMCAS Submitted. Waiting for verification...(Still filling out TMDSAS).

7/3- Decided not to fill out USUHS secondary and will just apply for service scholarship instead (4 year commitment after residency). I feel it is important to serve in the military and some point, but I am not ready to make a 7 year commitment plus additional reserve commitment after residency. Also, I think a health issue is going to keep me out.

7/13 - AMCAS Verified. Woohoo! Time for secondaries...

7/13 Decided not to finish filling out TMDSAS...Secondaries are time consuming as it is, and the TMDSAS application is like filling out AMCAS all over again. I already am applying to a lot of schools as it is. Goodbye Texas!

7/13 Considering adding Oregon Health and Science and UMich. I actually really would love living in Portland, but they just don't take that many out-of-staters...I'll wait for my MCAT score to post on Wednesday.

7/18 MCAT scores posted....arggg. I will be retaking in September.

7/26 Eastern Virgina Medical School emailed me asking if I was retaking the MCATs again, recommending that I do in order to stay competitive. In some ways, I take that as a good sign...maybe I am looking too much into it, but to me it translates, 'We might actually like you if your MCAT was better.' I have some family nearby Norfolk, so it would be nice to be near them...

8/09 - I got a secondary to UCSD today! Very surprised with my MCAT score. I am so excited. It's the little things in this process that make me happy. Baby steps. Baby steps.

8/10 - Got my last letter of recommendation. Sent letters to schools. Yeah, I can officially be complete somewhere!

9/11 Retook the MCAT. I don't feel like I did better. I started out
strong on the PS, and then ran out of time, and was left frantically guessing two passages. Arggg! Why am I so slow! My speed is really costing me.

9/15 - First rejection from Vanderbilt. I have to take it all in stride, so I don't make myself nuts. It's kind of like getting the first scratch on a new car.

9/20 - Really getting tired of filling out secondaries. Got behind while I was taking biochemistry over summer school and studying to retake the MCAT.

10/4 Added Howard

10/5 Added Wayne State. I am starting to feel really burnt out on writing secondaries. I feel like I could actually be making a difference and spend more time time volunteering if it were not for all these secondaries. Started having pre-MCAT score release nightmares again. Argggg!

10/13 Added Wake Forest. I don't know why I didn't apply here to begin with.

10/12 Rejection from Wayne State. That was fast figuring I only added them a week ago. They didn't even wait for my second MCAT scores.

10/15 Got my second MCAT scores today. Happy with the improvement in PS, but dismayed by my slip in Verbal and the Writing section. I was distracted, because I thought I bombed the PS section. It feels good to finally be complete just about everywhere (well, I have about 3 more secondaries I want to finish). Cheers to my new mantra, 'I am more than my MCAT score. I am NOT my MCAT score.'

10/16 Oh my goodness Wayne State reversed my rejection today after they got my new MCAT score! Awesome! I didn't think such things happened.

10/20 Got rejected from UCSF today, my #1 Choice. I almost started crying. I have been volunteering/doing research there for almost two years and I didn't even get a secondary. And they rejected me before I got my second MCAT scores (rejection dates 10/12). I knew with my MCAT it was a shot in the dark, but still bummed. I guess I won't be returning to the Bay for a while.

10/31 Rejection from BU via email.
10/31 VCU Secondary Invite
11/1 Interview Hold from UCSD. Figure this is the kiss of death, but since I am a native San Diegan I think I am going to write a letter of intent.
11/15 Rejection from Eastern Virgina...sad. I put a lot of thought into their secondary. I have family nearby, so it would have been nice to live near them.
12/15 Rochester Rejection
1/18 Rejection from UCLA
1/19 Rejection from Northwestern
2/04: Opened mailbox to find a thin letter from Loma Linda...assumed it was a rejection, but it was an interview invite! I am so excited. My friend went here and keeps telling me how great of a school it is...it's California's little secret gem!
02/06: Rejection from Cincinnati
02/15: Rejected from U of Colorado.
02/26: Received a rejection from Wake Forest and what sounded like an interview hold from Howard. Then, minutes later my day turned around when I looked down at my Blackberry to see an interview invite from Davis!!!! I forgot where I was and yelped, 'Eeeek!' really loudly. Then, I suddenly remembered that I was at work in the pediatrics clinics. All the docs, staff and patients in the waiting room just all stopped at looked at me strangely for a second! Luckily, I work in a place where the sound of crying babies and screaming kids is prevalent, so I didn't look too out of place!
02/27: Since, I was out-of-town My grandmother called me to let me know that Albert Einstein rejected me and then said, 'Who wants to live in the Bronx anyway?' Gotta love my Nana...always rooting for me!
02/29: Rejection from Ohio State University. When I got my rejection my grandmother said, 'Good! You didn't really want to live in the cold anyway.'
03/XX- LOVED, LOVED, LOVED Davis. Very similiar in atmosphere to Loma Linda, but with newer buildings and great technology. All my interviewers made me feel really welcome. Just got to keep my fingers crossed for Davis and Loma Linda now...
5/29/08: Ed called telling me I was accepted to UC Davis today!!! I started sobbing tears of happiness on the phone, and Ed thought something was wrong with me and asked, 'Are you o.k.' It's been a long road, but well worth it in the end.
