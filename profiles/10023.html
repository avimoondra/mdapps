Summary of Application Experience

Primary submitted 7/31. I have a tendency to procrastinate.

Got kind of a later start for my secondaries than I would have liked (late August), thanks to two deadbeat rec writers who finally came through for me.

The interview process is long, and it sucks. Makes me wonder why I didn't try to take a year off. (Oh, that's right, because I know that if I did, I'd be a lazy bum. Better not to tempt fate in this sort of situation.) It can be overwhelming. On the flip side, I've really liked meeting the other applicants, and I've gotten some great insights into the various schools, and med school in general.

1/30: The application cycle is winding down... I think Cornell is the last of my interview invites. I went to Duke last week and am totally sold on Duke's curriculum. Right now, I think it's coming down to Vanderbilt, WashU, and Duke, but who knows?

I think that UPenn, Harvard, and JHU were probably going to be rejections anyway. UPenn hurt most, just because I really like the curriculum and the various programs. Oh well. I guess they were all long shots. I'm still very happy with my current acceptances.

3/5: In the interest of forthrightness, I changed my UPenn, Hopkins, and Harvard decisions into 'rejections,' because even though I technically withdrew, the rejections were already kind of a foregone conclusion.

With today's acceptance from Duke(!!!), I'm nearly done. Still waiting on Emory, but at this point, I'm going to say my cycle's pretty much over, so I've made my stats visible again. I'll jot down some more thoughts on the application process in a little while, but right now I've got a thesis to work on.

3/12: Hahaha... I got a rejection from Harvard today. Nice to know they received and read my letter of withdrawal.

3/13: Ouch, a waitlist from Emory??? Thanks, but no thanks.

5/9: Duke!!!
