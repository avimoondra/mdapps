R: Secondary received {For next year's applicants - All of the dates were the earliest dates that secondary invites were sent out for those schools except UCSD};
S: Secondary submitted/sent;
C: Application verified by med school to be complete;
IR: Interview invite received (September 12 - January 19); 13(TOTAL): 8(Sep), 1(Oct), 2(Nov), 2(Jan)
I: Interview date (September 27 - February 26); 13(TOTAL): 3(Sep), 3(Oct), 3(Nov), 2(Dec), 2(Feb)
W: Withdrew
WL: Waitlisted
A: Accepted!!!

MEDICAL SCHOOL APPLICATION TIMELINE
(June) AMCAS submitted 6/04; verified 6/14.
(July 01 - September 22) SECONDARIES/LORs received and submitted.
(September - April) INTERVIEWS received and attended
(September - August) ACCEPTANCES activity picks up for waitlisted peeps from early-May to end-June with little movement after June.

TIPS:
1) Use the MSAR (Medical School Admission Requirements) to find out almost everything you need to know about the fees, requirements, screening of schools.
2) LORs (Letters of Recommendation) - By June, make sure to have at least 2 science academic references, 1 non-science academic, and 1 extra (work, volunteer, research) for good measure. Not having the required academic LORs delayed my app process while I waited for my recommender to write it (I sent in 4 non-academic along with 2 academic to most schools b/c I've been working for a while). My LORs were very strong and one of the reasons I think I got so many interviews (along with really trying to make my secondary applications engaging and well-written).
3) TIME - If you want to get the earliest interview slots, submit secondaries with a short turnaround. Everyone who is AMCAS complete before the first week of July gets their secondary at the same time. Set aside lots of time and start before they arrive if possible. This whole process takes a lot more time than you think it will. During secondary season (Jul-Sep), it's worth it to drop a couple activities in order to complete quality applications in a timely fashion.
4) JUNE AMCAS? - Although interviewing early is better, applicants also often get admitted after interviewing in March and April. I met an MSI who started interviewing in January and got into several top 20 schools.
5) SCHOLARSHIPS - Scholarships/Grants from med school financial aid departments seem to come in the following categories i)URM (Underrepresented Minority) - offered by most schools; ii)RESEARCH - some schools offer to non-MSTP/MDPhD students who have significant research experience/publications; iii)ACADEMIC - Offered by a lower ranked school or schools in smaller cities to very strong applicants. NOT typically offered by top schools unless you are one of the other categories listed here; iv)DISADVANTAGED - offered to students who come from a low income background; v)OTHER (Women, Primary care, etc.)
6) INTERVIEWS - i) SHOW passion for medicine in your interview; ii) KNOW why you want to go to that particular school; iii) WRITE DOWN your impressions of the school/students/facilities immediately after the interview/tour/etc. You'd be amazed how easily you forget... especially if you interview at several schools in a short period of time.
7) STUDENT HOSTS - I stayed with student hosts at almost every med school I interviewed at. They are available at every university, but oftentimes you need to ask. I really enjoyed staying with hosts to get a feel for the university and to learn specific reasons why I wanted to attend (useful in the interview). However, I am able to sleep pretty much anywhere which is necessary when you are sleeping on strangers' air mattresses, futons, and hide-a-beds.
