Summary of Application Experience

INTERVIEW EXPERIENCES:

TCOM: 7/26/07 People were friendly, interviewers were mostly laid back. Expect to do lots of walking, however. The anatomy lab was cool, but the students kind of weirded me out going 'Awww how cute' when we (interviewees) were all walking around. As well, TCOM and its faculty and staff came off pretty cocky about their school. One person said 'This is a better school than anywhere else and you'll work harder here than anywhere else.' The arrogance was a big turn-off for me. My first interviewer noted that I used male pronouns in my examples and said that was 'interesting,' my second interviewer went fine though. Definitely my worst interviews altogether.

TECH: 8/8/07 The medical students all seemed nice and well adjusted, and I like the fact that the faculty were sensitive to the recent USMLE score trends and revamped the curriculum so as to change that. My interviews were laid back, however my 2nd interviewer was very inattentive. My first interviewer kept saying 'good' or 'great' after all my answers. Lubbock was fine and I think I'd be happy here.

UTMB: 8/10/07 I loved it here! Their USMLE scores are awesome and their students seem great! There are cooler places to live, but that's really secondary in importance to me. I had great interview experiences here (minus walking across campus in my suit during the humidity, but a nice lady drove me all the way across campus for my second interview!). First interview was just a conversation, really laid back and great. Second interviewer said 'Holy smoke! You have great grades for a physics major...I will definitely recommend you for admission, you should come do research with me.'

UTSA: 8/13/07 I'm neither here nor there regarding this one. Students seemed alright, interviewers were nice (my 1st was a psychiatrist), facilities were okay. San Antonio is a nice place I guess, but that doesn't matter much to me. They were reticent about sharing their USMLE scores (which worries me). The impression that I got from this school was 'here's our school, we don't care if you like it or not.' This attitude, coupled with the fact that I still don't know a THING about their 1st and 2nd year curricula made for a somewhat 'meh' interview experience. Second interviewer told me I did a good job and that I was a 'great interviewee.'

A&M: 8/16/07 I liked A&M a lot. Students seemed happy and not overstressed, Scott and White is an awesome clinical facility and there's no doubt I'd get great training there. Interviewers were really nice and gave me a lot of positive feedback and encouraging advice. My second interviewer told me 'I have no doubt you'll get in this year' and 'we'd better offer you a spot before someone else does.' Easily my best interviews.

UT HOUSTON: 8/31/07 Honestly, I didn't think I would like it here as much as I did. The med center is amazing, the people seem cool, and the facilities are all pretty new. My interviews went well and my student host was great. The only thing that concerns me is the traditional curriculum, but it's been done that way before, so I'm sure it's fine. First interviewer said 'I hope you consider us for next year' and talked Aggie football with me :-).

UT SOUTHWESTERN: 10/13/07 I really liked it here. Parkland and Children's would be amazing places to rotate, and the faculty here seem great too. Dallas has lots of practical advantages for my future family situation, so in a lot of ways, Southwestern would be a great fit for me. No real negatives to going to school here, I don't think. The back-stabbing competition myth was completely dispelled by every student and faculty with whom I spoke. First interviewer was very complementary, and my second interviewer said 'you're a very strong applicant, hopefully we can attract you here.'

BAYLOR COLLEGE: 11/16/07 To be honest, I didn't fall in love with it here like so many people do. It seemed nice, and of course the TMC is impressive as always, but I don't see what all the fuss is about. As well, I got a little bit of the Ivory Tower feeling from this school. Interviews were okay, no real positive feedback of which to speak. But hey, in general it seems like a great school that matches its graduates to top-notch residencies.
