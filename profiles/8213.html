Summary of Application Experience

I started with a list of some 20-odd schools clustered in New York, Philadelphia, Chicago, DC, and Boston, because my fiance was planning on applying the following year and I wanted to make sure he would have at least a reasonable chance to get into a school in the geographic area I was. Harvard was, of course, a top choice for me. The fiance wanted very much to live in NYC (and there are lots of schools in the metropolitan area), so that was a second top choice. Other than that I didn't think about outcomes too much at the beginning.

With a few surprises (like no interviews from GW or Georgetown), the initial part of the process went smoothly. Getting an interview invite from Harvard felt great. I was also very very pleased, and got a confidence boost, by getting a screened secondary from UCSF, which stayed on my list despite the obvious geographic limitations it would pose my fiance. Much later in the process (February), it was a hard call to not go to that interview when I was invited, but by that point I knew I would have other good options that were better for us as a couple.

My interviews went almost uniformly well. I stayed with students at all of them, and completed most during a two-week stretch during which I made the trip from DC to Boston twice by bus. In the end, I'm not sure how much they mattered. I was extremely turned off by my Columbia interview, and the feeling I got from the school of elitism. My interviewer said 'if you decide you want to be here, write me a letter and I'll put it at the top of my list.' I never wrote him (not even a thank you letter), yet I got in. My only 'bad' interview was at Mt. Sinai, in the middle of week 2, at which point I was exhausted and fighting a cold. I reacted very defensively to a reasonable question during my first interview and never felt like I was able to recover. No surprise, it was my one waitlist. Harvard, two days later, felt great. I had fantastic rapport with my faculty interviewer, and thought I had a decent interview with my student. The rejection from them hurt a LOT.

Because I had been semi-consciously crossing my fingers for Harvard to come through, I had not really thought much about the other schools. Cornell or Columbia seemed like the obvious choices because of the appeal of NYC to me and my fiance. Chicago was appealing because fiance's family is there and I liked the school. In the end though, Penn really struck a chord both during the interview day and during the revisit weekend, and I'm thrilled to be heading there!
