Summary of Application Experience

5.30.07 - Submitted Transcripts to AMCAS
6.30.07 - Submitted AMCAS
7.10.07 - Verified by AMCAS
7.17.07 - Released LOR w/ Secondaries

Cost:

Primaries: $1750
Secondaries: $3800
Suit: $450
Interviews (lodging,flight, food, a 'little' entertainment): $5400
Running Total: $11400

This is basically a journal of my experiences as they occured. Comments in brackets are inserted in previous posts after I completed my 11th interview. After completing so many interviews, I could give a more accurate description about my earlier interviews.

8.20.07 I got a $7500 scholarship from Genentech, used it on med applications, and I am broke now! Too many schools... I have withdrew from several schools and refocusing. I plan on developing a robust knowledge base of each school, doing mock interviews with a peer, finishing my summer internship, and spending the few days of vacation with my family before I head off to interviews..............

8.23.07 I plan on doing the Student Host Exchange program at Creighton and Case.. can't decide whether it is risky to stay with a random medical student in a strange dorm the night before and save some $$, or get a hotel and go with convenience. Your thoughts? Comments?

9.5.07 Also interesting on hearing advice for the interview. I am scared as $hit! They are in two weeks and I have spent my last few weeks chillen, partying, or working which gives me a sense of ill preparation, especially at Case. I haven't bought my ticket yet but I will by the end of this week. Also, I changed my mind about the Student Exchange Program... I decided that I will try the student hosts at Case and Creighton. Thanks for everyone's advice.

9.12.07 I am ready for Case interview on Monday and Creighton on Friday. I am nervous about Mayo since I heard interviews are being offered and I haven't heard back yet.

9.14.07 Got a Boston Interview... my greatest day ever. Leaving for Cleveland as we speak (literally at the airport).

9.17.07 My first interview exp: The Case interview was relaxing. They really emphasized through-out interview day how unique and innovative their school is and that it is a low pressure, non-competitive system; no strange questions, standard 'Why medicine, Why case' and the rest of the conversations were predominantly about the curriculum changes. The school stands apart on its own with the traditional 9-5 pm lectures replaced by a self-directed learning system, augmented by a world class learning environment, located next to a growing medical hub-Cleveland Clinic, UH, and the newly constructed Heart Center (Number 1 in the country). The tuition is 60K/year, kinda steep compared to Cali schools, and apparently, Cleveland is the poorest city in the nation?! It is another one of those 'cases' (pun intended) where you have a great school in a ghetto. -[4.17.08 ... looking back at this interview after having 10 more, I think I did terrible and was poorly prepared. Be sure to prepare hard for your interview!]

9.27.07 The Creighton interview was also lax, and the school philosophy is markedly different from Case. They are very service oriented as opposed to Case. But the school was incredible, the students make it a positive environment to school in. My student st Case Western seemed cold in comparison to my host at Creighton... Nebraskan's are really good people. He showed me the Anatomy lab, took me out, even lent me $$ when I didn't have any for a final dinner. They made me feel like I am already a student there!! Come on Baby, October 15th is the date of reckoning...


10.17.07 Boo Hoo. My app was put on the Alternate List at both Creighton and Case. I know I didn't bomb the interviews [YES I DID!], I played it safe, and I developed rapport. I was told, officially by a dean, that there is nothing wrong with my application, everything looks good, but they are not ready to offer me an acceptance. WTF! Your thoughts?

10.18.07 Optimistic... got an interview invite from Davis. Interestingly, I am at the airport (again) leaving for Boston. Is it coincidence that I get these invites at the airport?

10.19.07 I am officially going to burn in the history books as the one who blew his chances. I missed the airplane to Boston! Then the red eye flight got delayed till the morning of my interview. I will be paying dearly for this.

10.29.07 Interviewed at Davis. I love it. Want to go there for sure. I am going to write a letter of intent. Rescheduled Boston interview to Dec. 4th, but I might hear from Davis by then. Fingers crossed.

10.31.07 Oh $hit. UCSF is crazy enough to interview mz. Happy Halloween...

11.2.07 Dartmouth invite (wow!)

11.14.07 Going through alot of interview preparation for the biggest one I can ever conceieve of - UCSF. What does a person have to do to get in there? Planning my trip to Dartmouth and BU right now and writing a letter to nearby Univ. who have me on Hold to notify I am traveling near them and it would be nice if they can squeeeeeeeze me in for an interview.

11.21.07 Interview invite fro Univ. of Vermont... the 'in your area' letter possibly helped!!!

11.27.07 I feel very positive about the UCSF interview [DID TERRIBLE, too nervous and underprepared]. It went so well mainly because I was in my home town, and the school is a perfect fit for me; clinically/ and research oriented, metropolitan city, early clinical exposure, $$ considerations, and established contacts and support). The interview at UCSF taught me three strategies... 1) SHINE- smile entering the interview and especially shine exiting the interview. My second interviw was my 'perfect' interview because I made my first big connection with my interviewer (it helped that we had so much in common and that we just had great rapport) and when I left, I couldn't hold back a HUGE SMILE and that really shined through to him. Being warm and friendly is how you get an interviewer to warm up to the adcoms and passionately advocate for your candidancy. They are your friends. Mannerisms is also a big part of shining. Be very polite and maintain all formalities. 2) ANTICIPATE- seriously organize your thoughts in a skeleton outline, and prepare in advance by practicing alone or doing mock interviews that simulate the interview setting. Repeated practice will reduce rambling, downplay the emotional tones, and help your stamina for the long day. 3) OBSERVE- this is what will set students apart. If your not observant of everything that is happening around you, you will pass on many opportunities, especially the common threads that may help an interviewer or dean remember you amongst the rest. Relax, take a deep breath, and instead of zoning out when the dean is talking about boring statistics, look at what he is wearing.... maybe you have a shirt or tie in common? Maybe you have a similar watch or hairstyle? 'Through little things do great things come' is a proverb well worth remembering. However, don't fabricate, or be siccophantic. [BE OVERCONFIDENT RATHER THAN UNDERCONFIDENT..]

12.06.07 I am f*cking exausted. Three interviews in 4 days; BU, UVA, and now Dartmouth tommorrow. Arrived in Boston on Sunday, was so tired- got R&R all day. Interviewed at BU yesterday morning. My interview was stagnant in the beginning, partly because I was nervous, and partly because my interviewer wasn't up to date on my application and nothing really excited him. Interestingly, he asked me what happened to me in October and that he was dissapointed I couldn't make it to the interview. He had read my app last time and forgot everything by now, haha. I had to explain some things to him and he asked me superficial questions as he browsed my AMCAS. After we did that for 30 minutes, he dropped an ethical question: A doctors role in euthanasia. I told him about a report I did for med ethics on this topic and the conversation got interesting as it turned into a debate that I enjoyed thoroughly. We went 15 minutes over our time. I had a great interview overall and I learned something; a good interview ought to give the interviewee positive energy, which I had alot of afterwards. Then Dean Wolitzer summarized the day on a down note: of the 1000 interviewees, only 350 will be offered acceptance. What a crapshoot! Boston is a great city though. Loved its youthful energy and historic scenary.

Then it was off to Vermont. I had to catch the late Greyound bus to Burlington to get their by morning. It sucked. I got their at 5 am, the interview started at 10. I hadn't planned my travel accomodations in advance, so I ended up going to the medical center and fell asleep on the floor of the Davis lecture hall. I got 1.5 hours of sleep, fuckn woke up, went into the bathroom, changed into my suit, and headed to the admissions office. I fell asleep during the deans introduction, though nobody really noticed in the dark. Then went on a tour I was indifferent to, finally went to my interview, killed it [I still think I killed it, one of my better interviews] left the school, got a motel, was about to crash when I saw another UVM applicant in the hallway, got invited for dinner and beer, went along half asleep, got tipsy, and came home and slept for 13 hours straight. I might end up in Vermont, though I disagree with the out-of-state tuition, its ridiculous.

Off to Dartmouth. Arrived in Hanover this morning, taxied to DMS, arranged for a student host, and now I am in the library killing time while she is off studying. New England is damned cold btw.

12.14.07 The day after I got back from Dartmouth, got a Duke invite. Its the second to last day of the season =) I was originally rejected last month. I was told that they had some additional interview spots that were vacant and they re-evaluated my app and reconsidered. I am ecstatic.

12.17.07 I got a request from MSU for LOR. Is this a precursor to an interview? [Sometimes]

2.3.07 Its been two months since I've update my profile. The month of January was particularly devastating. Rejection after rejection came in, with the exception of Vermont. I am insanely conflicted about these results and depressed that I have no opportunities remaining to stay in Ca. [I WAS IN DENIAL ABOUT MY INTERVIEW PERFORMANCE, REJECTIONS WERE INEVEITABLE] I disagree with the obvious claim that I need to brush up on my interview skills [NOT ANYMORE]. I might even admit that because of my diverse interests in fields beyond medicine, I have a greater marger for dialgoue than most pre-med students [THIS I STILL BELIEVE]. This has been pointed out by several of my interviewers, with words like 'multifaceted' and 'unique.' Nonetheless, these rejections don't lie. Something fundamental to the nature of a physician is missing, or hidden, in me. How do I answer this question? [PREPARE GOD DAMMIT!]

4.15.08 Rosalind Franklin and Michigan State did not create an organized experience like Duke. Duke was phenomenal, I did alright in my interviews, had good rapport, etc. Got put on the waitlise, with 200 other students. Wrote them an LOI.... I would die tommorrow a happy man if Duke took me off their waitlist. In the meantime, I got accepted to Rosalind Franklin (YEAH!!) my first one (FINALLY!). Whew, at least I have one. I will assume that is the place I will attend in the fall but I will keep update Case and Duke, which are my two favorites at the moment. Also waiting to hear back from MSU. if I get into MSU, when it comes to decision time, I think I will really focus on financials since both schools had tuition near 40K.

4.21.08 Keeping my fingers crossed for Duke, Case, or UVM ..

8.3.08 Rosalind Franklin it is.
