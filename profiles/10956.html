Summary of Application Experience

6/13 I'm in!!!!!!!!!!!!!!! Dr. Quinn called me this afternoon to let me know I've been accepted to USC! I am SO incredibly excited and happy right now.

5/31 I spoke with Dean Frantz (Columbia) roughly a week ago and was told that Columbia has overaccepted (very few people are declining their acceptances this year), and will not be taking many people off of the waitlist. I'm preparing a new personal statement and getting my AMCAS application ready to go, since it seems likely that I'll be applying again next year.

5/18 Rejected from AECOM.

3/29 Waitlisted at Boston. Looks like it'll be at least another month and a half until I know where I'll be headed - if anywhere.

3/21 I called Albert Einstein today to check on my status and was told I've been put on the waitlist.

3/11 Put on hold at UCLA. I guess that's essentially a rejection. This was my top choice, but oh well. Now I'm just hoping to get an acceptance somewhere.

3/7 I've been waitlisted at Columbia. I was actually expecting a rejection, so the waitlist is good news.

2/28 I decided to withdraw from Dartmouth. It's a great school, and everyone was really nice, but I can't see myself being able to live there. I definitely think it'd hurt my relationship, and make my fiance's life very difficult while I'm in med school. As hard as this would be on my self-esteem, I know I'd rather have to reapply to medical school next year than live in Hanover. This was the one school I applied to that didn't fit my criteria, and that I didn't somewhat research before applying.

2/24 I received a rejection letter from NYU last week, but since they stopped interviewing in December and I obviously didn't get an interview, I knew it was coming. I think it's safe to assume at this point that I'm rejected from any school where I haven't receive an interview yet.

2/10 I've been waitlisted at USC. I'm seriously down right now, but it'll hopefully pass in a day or two. This was my one realistic shot at staying in California.

2/2 Gmail lost my Columbia interview invite! Columbia sent me an email today letting me know I am being withdrawn because they emailed me an interview invite in November and I never responded! I called the admissions office right away and was able to schedule an interview for 2/16 (the last day they are interviewing), but I feel sick thinking what a difference it could've made for me to interview back in November or December.

12/5 I'm finally complete at Yale. I resent my letters around a week ago, after waiting since the end of September for my file to become complete. The first letters must've gotten lost somewhere along the way. And, by the way, I'm still in a state of grateful amazement at being offered an interview at UCLA.

12/2 I got home from work today to find an interview invitation in the mail from UCLA!!! Yay! Yayayayayayayayayayay!!!

12/2 GWU sent me an email to let me know that my application has been reviewed and put on hold, and will eventually be reviewed again. Oh well. I'm happy so far with how this process is going, so one hold won't get me down.

12/1 Interview invite from Albert Einstein!

11/21 I got an interview invite from Boston U by email this morning!

11/7 I had a letter waiting for me from Dartmouth this evening. I was under the impression that most interview invites come by email, and rejections come by mail, but... it was an interview invitation!!

11/2 I got an interview invite from USC!! My first interview invitation!

10/11 I mailed letters of update this morning, and sent each school an email to let them know it's coming (just in case they decide to look my application over within the next few days). Dartmouth and George Washington each emailed me back to let me know they'll be sure the letter gets into my file. That definitely makes me like DMS and GWU.

10/7 I really need to send update letters out soon because my manuscript was accepted for publication and the abstract has been published. I'll try to get that done tonight or tomorrow.

10/3 I've decided not to fill out the secondaries for Stanford and Johns Hopkins. The odds are so heavily against me being accepted at either that I think it'd just be a waste of time and money.

9/29 My LORs were sent to Harvard and Dartmouth today. I also received my first rejection (UCSF), which is a little depressing, although I was expecting it.

9/28 NYMC just told me they will not consider my application complete until they receive another science LOR. I do have 1 science, 1 non-science, and 1 research letter, but the research letter won't count. Unfortunately my 2nd science LOR was the professor who disappeared, so it looks like NYMC is out.

9/21 My letters were sent out this morning! They were sent to 11 of the schools I've applied to, since I only requested they be mailed to the ones I have completed secondaries for (minus Harvard, because I couldn't find the address they want letters mailed to quickly). I'll probably request a few more be sent today or tomorrow to push myself to find time for completing more secondaries - it just gets so hard with working full-time and commuting 3 hours a day.

9/16 My last letter is finally in! It arrived at the career center/letter service this morning, and I requested my letters to be sent this afternoon. They say it should take a few days to process, so hopefully my letters will go out Tuesday or Wednesday. I can't wait until I'm complete at the schools.

8/26 I'm still waiting for my last letter of rec to arrive at the letter service. I've talked to the recommender recently, so I know it'll be there soon. It's technically my second-to-last letter of rec, but the other recommender is MIA, and hasn't been answering my emails for nearly a month and a half, so I've given up on her. Really odd, seeing as I knew that professor well and she was enthusiastic about writing me a letter. Oh well..

8/19 I added Chicago schools (Rosalind Franklin, Loyola, Northwestern, Pritzker, Rush), George Washington, and Dartmouth.

8/11 I just did a total marathon run of secondary completing! Go me! I was in one of those nice productive moods this evening. I still have to call my mom to ask for a loan, though, which I'm not looking forward to. I know she'll give me the money gladly, I just don't like needing to borrow. This process is so expensive.

8/9 I received a talk from my research advisor today about how I shouldn't limit myself just to try to be in the same city as my fiance. She had a point, so while I'm not going to apply to tons more, I will still increase my list to include Chicago and Washington D.C. I think. I just have to find the money to do this.

7/1 My fiance plans to start law school a year or two after I start medical school (if I get in this cycle), so the schools I am able to apply to are limited to the cities with top law schools (Boston, New Haven, Palo Alto/Bay Area, New York, Los Angeles).
