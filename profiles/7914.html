Summary of Application Experience

I applied late. My AMCAS wasn't verified until October 10th because I was waiting for my MCAT scores to be released. My obvious weakness in my application is my MCAT. While it isn't atrocious, I think it will hurt me significantly, particularly that 8 in verbal. However, I might get the benefit of the doubt at some schools(that take the highest subsections) because I got a 9 on the VR the first time I took the MCAT. I feel that the rest of my application is pretty solid. Overall, I tried to apply to low-middle tier schools, but some schools I applied to are definitely reach schools (i.e. Ohio State OOS). I don't have my heart set on any one particular school, and would pretty much go anywhere. I feel like my chances of getting accepted this year are neither bad nor good, but I hope to stay somewhat optimistic. Let the fun begin!

October 31st-Rejected pre-secondary by Wake Forest University. Still haven't received secondaries from Loyola and EVMS. Received interview at USUHS which I will go to. While I'm not sure military medicine is for me, I feel like I should take advantage of the interivew for practice. Who knows, I might even be turned on by the whole idea of being a military doctor. All other secondaries finished and submitted/certified.

November 9th-Received/submitted Loyola secondary last week. I'm not holding my breath for EVMS. Something tells me they want an OOSer with an MCAT better than a 28, since these days apparently the MCAT is the end all be all of who will perform well in medical school and become a good doctor. Well, I guess they have to find some way to narrow down thousands of applicants to a few hundred.

December 3rd-Rejected WVU b/c they screen out OOSers with a sub-9 on any subsection of the MCAT. I would be okay if I didn't get in this cycle, but will s*** a brick if I don't get in next cycle. I should have a published research paper in a few months, and am currently boosting my volunteer hours and shadowing experience. I will do anything within my power to not take the MCAT again (3rd time). I'm thinking of calling the schools I get rejected from and asking them if my MCAT is killing my application. If the vote is unanimous (gulp), MCAT retake it is. Otherwise, it would be really nice to at least get a few interviews this cycle to confirm that I'm competitive.

December 20th-Just got back from USUHS interview. The school is great: great student body, great facilities, NO TUITION, great everything...except you have to serve a ton of time as a military doctor after graduation. I'm still really unsure about the military thing. From what I've learned about USUHS, the general consensus is that you'll get a top notch medical school education, but likely have a crappy experience in the military afterwards. Elsewhere, I'm becoming the king of pre-interview holds. I'm really hoping for an interview from one of the Pennsylvania schools. Penn State still hasn't determined if my app. is complete...it's been almost 3 months. Don't bother calling them either b/c they completely blow you off on the phone.


January 18th-I withdrew from USUHS. I feel that is possible to have a great experience as a military doctor; however, military medicine is very unpredictable. You never know if your residency will be good, where you'll be deployed, what new laws will be passed concerning your career, etc. You are at the whim of the federal government and thus have little control over certain aspects of your career.

March 6th-I've gotten three interviews in the last 10 days! They're all late but what the hell, an interview's an interview! I seriously can't believe this. Two weeks ago I had basically written myself off for this cycle. Now with three interviews coming up, I have a very realistic shot at getting into medical school. For anyone else suffering from low MCAT syndrome, keep the faith!

April 2nd-I had interiews at Wayne State and NYMC last week. I liked Wayne. The clinical training there is unbelievable. I didn't like the large class size (which seems competitive) and the location. Downtown Detroit is beyond depressing. I would definitely be happy to go there though, as long as I could live in the suburbs. I really liked NYMC. I would go there in a heartbeat if accepted. When I was on campus things clicked: I could really see myself there. Anyways, I plan on writing a Letter of Intent to NYMC unless I like Chicago Medical School more (interivew April 16th). If anyone has any questions about the schools I just mentioned send me a message.

May 9th-FINALLY IT'S OVER. I got into New York Medical College, which was my #1 choice of the 5 schools at which I interviewed. I am both flattered and astonished I got in without even being waitlisted. I hope to defer my acceptance for a year and start in 2009, but if not I'm okay starting this fall. Its been a rough and wild ride. Applying to medical school can be very humbling, but the grueling nature of the process makes an acceptance feel that much better.

May 27th-Deferral request granted! Will be part of NYMC class of 2013.
