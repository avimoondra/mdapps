Summary of Application Experience

7/11/05 - TMDSAS submitted
7/13/05 - AMCAS submitted
7/20/05 - TMDSAS verified
7/21/05 - AMCAS verified

9/9/05 - 1st interview @ UTMB
+: laid-back environment, cheap tuition, strong telemedicine (rural medicine), away-rotation possibilities, PBL curriculum
-: perhaps too laid-back, everyone is from tx, most ppl stay in tx

9/27/05 - 1st withdrawal from UC-Davis
In my mind, I had withdrawn from UC-Davis a couple weeks ago, but today I officially notified their admissions office of my decision, and it was mainly because UC-Davis has a ricidulous secondary application, with 3 essay questions each having a 6000 character limit (which is longer than the personal statement). I still have a lot of schools (16) to be concerned with, so dropping this one will hopefully help me out in the long run by saving time and money.

10/10/05 - interview @ UCI
+: early clinical exposure, small class size, laid-back (not as much as UTMB, but more than other UC's apparently), PRIME-LC, Irvine, close to home, organ systems curriculum
-: lecture based (vs PBL), many subjects simultaneously (vs 1), everyone is from ca, most ppl stay in ca

10/11/05 - interview @ USC
+: diverse student population (private school), dual degree opportunites, flexibility in curriculum (esp 4th yr), STEP 1 scores 2nd only to harvard, P/F grading (not competitive), Los Angeles, organ systems curriculum, great teaching hospital
-: expensive tuition, research opportunities

10/21/05 - interview @ Columbia
+: diverse AND interesting student population (private), dual degree opportunities (excellent grad schools), early clinical exposure, flexibility (esp 4th yr), student life (P&S club), p/f first yr, live close to campus, many underserved communities nearby
-: expensive tuition, high cost of standard of living, NYC life

11/4/05 - interview @ Baylor
+: flexibility in curriculum (1.5 yrs basic science), world's largest medical center (great teaching hospitals), super early clinical exposure, int'l health track, Houston, cheap, organ systems curriculum
-: UTH MD/MPH program available but startup, familiarity, everyone from tx

11/12/05 - interview @ UT-Southwestern
+: research opportunities, MD/MPH, good teaching hospital (Parkland County), anatomy lab, cost, good surgery and internal med
-: academically isolated, few globally minded ppl, traditional curriculum

11/18/05 - interview @ U-Michigan
+: learning resources (esp tech), priority on med students, large socially concious young adult/grad student population, possibility of taking undergrad courses (language), flexibility in test-taking, away-rotations, MD/MPH 3rd-4th (and other relations with grad schools/undergrad), good teaching hospital (highly ranked residency) + large med center, early clinical exposure, organ systems p/f curriculum, research opportunities
-: cost (30k tuition), newly developing global program, perhaps not-as-diverse pt population (predominantly homogeneous white)

12/9/05 - interview @ Northwestern
+: college system mentoring, away-rotations (inc int'l), MD/MPH in 4 yrs, early clinical exposure + good integration, organ systems p/f curriculum, research opportunities, diverse pt population (big city), strong general internal med
-: too many nwu/hpme (70/170), family med prog very small, few globally minded ppl, too many asian americans?

12/13/05 - interview @ UCSD
+: cost, diversity of pts (city), MD/MPH (post 3rd yr - SDSU, Berkeley, Harvard, JHU), community outreach progs (Free Clinic), strong prim care prog, leading HIV/AIDS treatment prog, pre-clinical electives, 4th yr flexible
-: competitiveness? (been told this, but didnt seem like it when i was there), lecture-based (some small group) curriculum, few globally minded ppl

factors important to me (considering my long term interests as well): student diversity, student life and clubs (global health), dual degree opportunities, resources, curriculum, academic environment, local teaching hospitals, location, cost
