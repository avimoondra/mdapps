Summary of Application Experience

September 4th, 2007:

Eh, I figured it was time to flesh this out. I sent my primary in on June 5th, verified June 7th, secondaries started rolling in in the 22nd I believe. Applied all MD-PhD, but will definitely consider MD-only (you can, after all, apply internally to an MD-PhD program).

As of today I have six interview invites, and am starting to think I may pull this off. To all you future applicants, I come from a small, no-name school in the Northeast. I have zero publications upon application. If you want to shoot for MD-PhD, evidently, you will be considered. It is still very early, and I could get rejected everywhere, but I hope I don't tank my interviews like that. However, I will remain cynical - being put on a waitlist everywhere seems highly probable...

September 9th, 2007:

Wow. Shouldn't have scheduled Case so close to Thanksgiving. Cleveland is about a 1.5 hour plane ride from my local airport. If I leave Thursday evening for Case and come home Friday evening - a cool $400. Ouch.

September 21st, 2007:

Thanks to Dartmouth, I now have an interview every single week from October 3rd to Thanksgiving. I don't really need to attend class...or take exams...or go to work...or tutor...or meet any obligations...do I?

October 21st, 2007:

First acceptance to LSU...in October. No matter what happens with my eight (at this point) other interviews, I get to play doctor. Nice.

December 9th, 2007:

Accepted MD-only at LSU, USC, UVM, and WVU; on hold MD-only at Case and Dartmouth; everything is still up in the air at MUSC and VCU; Miami, Saint Louis, and UVM (PhD) interviews coming up.

I wish PhD committees would get together and decide my fate. I'm not knocking 4 medical school acceptances, but one MD-PhD would be sweet.

February 18th, 2008:

As of now, it's between UMiami's and VCU's MD-PhD programs. The only school I'm eager to hear back from at this point is MUSC.

What a great dilemma to have - picking which MD-PhD program to matriculate into...

April 26th, 2008:

VCU it is. Withdrew from every school (even MUSC), and leased an apartment in Richmond yesterday. I loved Miami, but the city is just too expensive and VCU's program seems far more cohesive.

Good luck to every pre-med that stumbles upon this profile, hoping they get into medical school...
