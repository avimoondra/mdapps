Summary of Application Experience

Beginning of August to beginning of November: Submitted all of my secondaries. Got an interview at one the same day I submitted and from another 4 months after submitting the secondary.

1-5-07 Good. Fun mostly. Completely unpredictable with regard to acceptance it seems. Still waiting. Have no answers thus far.

2-27-07 Rough. The interviews and tours themselves are generally amazingly fun and informative. Three wait lists and waiting to hear from the other 6 schools I interviewed with. Know exactly where I'd like to be. And that has certainly changed during this process. So hard to predict how any of this will turn out. Very frustrating. It doesn't make any sense in my opinion. But, god willing, I'll wind up exactly where I'll be most happy.

3-2-07 Finally! My first acceptance! Northwestern. Good school. Nice city. Cold. Very excited. Now I can tell those back-up schools I so disliked (SLU and Miami) to take me off their wait lists. Still have 5 to hear from and am on the wait list for one school I really like. Yay.

3-9-07 Rejected by Tufts. just goes to show you have no way of knowing. At the first interview they kept telling me how good I was doing and how much higher my scores and grades were than their averages etc. The second interviewer also seemed rather excited about me. Rejection in the end. Oh well. Not one of my top choices anyway. Didn't get most rejection letters until now.

3-19-07 Received VERY encouraging wait list letter from Wash U.
'Your application has been carefully evaluated by the
members of our Committee on Admissions who have
determined that you are qualified for admission.
However, at this time we have more outstanding
applicants than available positions.

While I am currently unable to offer you a place in
the 2007 First Year Class, I expect to make at least
50 additional offers of acceptance before class
convenes in mid-August... I hope that you will [choose
to] remain under consideration because I believe you
would thrive here...'
So very excited at the possibility!! LOVE Wash U!

3-29-07 Still waiting to hear from Georgetown,Pitt, and Baylor. Going to 2nd look weekend at Northwestern in two weeks.

3-31-07 Rejected at Georgetown. Not at all disappointed because of acceptance at Northwestern. The interview was very awkward and I didn't connect with my interviewer at all. Georgetown did have some unique, strong points with regard to service and international health... Anxiously awaiting decision from Pitt, and Baylor in particular.

4-20-07 somewhat disappointed over the waitlist at Baylor after waiting SOO long. My interview was amazing but I have no idea how interested they are. Will remain on the waitlist at both Baylor and Wash U but am very excited about Northwestern since I came back from their second look weekend.

5-9-07 Clearly Baylor was very interested, as I just received my acceptance! I loved this school when I visited. However Northwestern has offered me an $80,000 grant over four years which will cover 1/2 of my tuition, I found an amazing place to live and many things have been falling into place. I grew to love Northwestern after 2nd look and have been very excited about moving to Chicago despite the fact that I hate the cold! Will visit Houston again to help make the decision. Still on the waitlist at Wash U, but if they can't help me financially, I'll have to go elsewhere. I'm so excited about these two options, but am unsure which I'll choose!

5-21-07 Who was I kidding? The choice was easy! BCM! They made it easier by giving me a $12,000 per year scholarship plus more need-based money. Financially Baylor is a great choice--- but, more importantly, the school is simply amazing! I remember now why I loved it! People are so friendly. The atmosphere at this school is unique and very warm. Plus it's a great school with a unique curriculum that I believe will suit my needs very well. I couldn't be more excited. I didn't think anyone could top Northwestern's offer, but this offer is unbeatable.
Man, was I scared and discouraged at times. But I'm so fortunate/blessed--a top 10 school for very little cost compared to others. It just seems like the right place for me.

5-25-07 I finally withdrew my application from Wash U, though I was somewhat hesitant for some reason. I love the school--it is an amazing school. But Baylor is probably the only school that I could have loved as much and been certain I was getting an education that is comparable, that will prepare me as well. I am extremely pleased with my choice.

The right school does wind up picking you. Northwestern would have been a pretty good fit for me as well. and Though I was waitlisted at Baylor (likely because I interviewed on the very last interview day), they made it clear that they wanted me by accepting me before May 15th, offering me a scholarship when I told them that I was offered money elsewhere, and in other ways. The students at Baylor are amazing!

Remember to be flexible during this process. Don't stick to your preconceived ideas, just let your opinion change based on your experience and you will be happy in the end.
